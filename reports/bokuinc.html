Executive Summary

Boku Inc is a UK AIM-listed provider of mobile payment and fraud prevention infrastructure serving digital merchants globally. Its carrier billing network connects merchants -- Netflix, Spotify, Disney+ among them -- directly to mobile network operators, enabling payments charged to mobile phone bills. The model is asset-light, high-margin, and structurally advantaged by deep MNO relationships that are difficult to replicate. At 173.25 GBX, the shares trade on a forward P/E of approximately 40x against a business growing revenue mid-teens and generating significant free cash flow. The market appears to apply a UK small-cap/technology discount to what is, in substance, a global fintech SaaS platform with high-quality recurring revenue. BUY conviction: 72/100. Key catalyst is continued margin expansion and potential inclusion in a FTSE index, which would materially broaden the shareholder base. HOLD above 220 GBX.

Business Model

Boku operates a two-sided payments network connecting digital merchants to mobile network operators. The company provides three core services: (1) carrier billing -- merchants add Boku's payment APIs and consumers can charge purchases to their mobile phone bill, particularly valuable in markets where card penetration is low; (2) mobile wallet top-up -- direct top-up of mobile money accounts via the MNO relationship; and (3) identity verification and fraud prevention -- using mobile operator data to authenticate users and detect fraudulent transactions without requiring personal data.

The revenue model is transaction-based, with Boku earning a share of each payment processed. Because the infrastructure is largely built and the marginal cost of new merchants is low, incremental revenue flows at high margins. The MNO relationships represent the core moat -- these contracts take years to negotiate and Boku has accumulated relationships with operators across more than 60 countries. Switching costs for merchants are meaningful since integration requires technical work, and removing Boku would require renegotiating directly with dozens of MNOs. Key merchant verticals: subscription streaming, gaming, digital goods, and utilities. Top clients include Netflix, Spotify, Disney+, and several major Asian digital platforms.

Recent Catalysts

Boku published a strong trading update in January 2026 showing full-year revenue growth in line with expectations and significant margin progression. The company highlighted expansion of its fraud prevention product suite as a key strategic driver for FY2026 and beyond -- identity verification is a higher-margin, stickier revenue line than pure payment processing. The fraud prevention segment is growing faster than the payments business and now represents a meaningful portion of total group revenue. There has been incremental evidence of market share gains in Southeast Asia, where mobile-first commerce is growing rapidly and carrier billing has structural advantages over card-based payments. The shares remain on AIM, which contributes to a persistent liquidity and institutional investor discount relative to premium listed peers. Any move toward a standard listing or FTSE 250 inclusion would be a significant re-rating catalyst.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case (30% weight)

The bull case centres on two vectors: (1) continued margin expansion as the fraud prevention product scales, pushing group EBITDA margins toward 30%+ from current low-20s, and (2) re-rating triggered by FTSE inclusion or a standard listing upgrade. Carrier billing is structurally growing as digital commerce expands in emerging markets where mobile payments are the default. Boku's MNO network is a compounding asset -- more merchants join, more consumers use it, more valuable the network becomes. In this scenario, EPS grows toward 7-8p over two to three years, supporting a market cap in the £900m-£1.1bn range. Conviction score: 82/100.

Base Case (50% weight)

Boku grows revenue mid-teens organically with incremental margin improvement. The fraud prevention product gains traction but the merchant payment business remains the dominant revenue driver. No immediate catalyst for re-rating; shares trade in a range as the P/E remains at a slight premium to growth rate but not yet at a premium SaaS multiple. The bear case centres on MNO contract renegotiation risk and competitive pressure from digital wallets and alternative payment methods. If large MNOs in key markets use Boku's growing volume as leverage to renegotiate better terms, margins compress. There is also the risk that Big Tech payment ecosystems (Apple Pay, Google Pay) expand further into carrier billing territory. Revenue growth could decelerate to high single digits if these headwinds materialise. The shares would de-rate toward 25x P/E on this scenario, implying a price in the 120-140 GBX range. Key Risks

  1. #1 MNO contract risk: Boku's entire proposition rests on relationships with mobile network operators. If major operators in key markets renegotiate terms adversarially or choose to build their own payment capabilities, this directly threatens Boku's margin structure.
  2. #2 Competitive pressure from digital wallets: Apple Pay, Google Pay, Alipay, and regional e-wallets are expanding globally. If these platforms reduce the need for carrier billing, Boku's addressable market shrinks.
  3. #3 AIM liquidity and re-rating risk: The shares trade on AIM, limiting institutional ownership. Until there is a catalyst for a standard listing or index inclusion, the stock may remain range-bound despite solid operating performance.
  4. #4 Emerging market FX risk: A significant portion of revenue is generated in Asia and Latin America. Local currency depreciation against sterling would suppress reported revenue and earnings.
  5. #5 Fraud prevention revenue concentration: The fraud prevention product line is growing rapidly but is relatively nascent. Any significant mis-step in this product -- a data incident, a regulatory issue -- would disproportionately affect the higher-margin growth vector.
