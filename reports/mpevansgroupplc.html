Executive Summary

M P Evans is a UK-listed investment company that provides concentrated exposure to the Indonesian palm oil sector through direct plantation ownership, processing assets, and a listed equities portfolio. The group has a long-track record dating back to 1980, and its portfolio is concentrated in Indonesian plantations with some processing and logistics infrastructure. At 1616 GBX, the shares trade near the bottom of their 52-week range, offering a modest recovery premium to a NAV that has historically traded at a meaningful discount to intrinsic value.

The investment case rests on three pillars: the structural demand tailwind for palm oil from food, oleochemical, and energy end-markets; the discount at which the share price has historically sat relative to assessed NAV per share; and the group's exposure to a commodity that has demonstrated significant price volatility, which creates both opportunity and risk.

The case for HOLD is anchored in valuation: at approximately 14x earnings and a sub-1x price-to-NAV multiple, the downside appears limited on a medium-term view, but the near-term catalyst set is thin and the ESG narrative around palm oil remains a persistent overhang for institutional ownership.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY at 1616 GBX. The valuation offers a reasonable entry for patient investors willing to tolerate commodity price noise, currency risk, and the persistent ESG discount applied to palm oil plantation owners. Not a compelling near-term catalyst story; longer-duration holding preferred.

Business Model

M P Evans operates as an investment company with a primary focus on Indonesian palm oil plantations. Its assets comprise direct ownership stakes in plantation companies and associated processing infrastructure in Indonesia, together with a portfolio of listed equities in the palm oil and broader agriculture space.

The core value driver is the underlying value of its plantation assets versus the market value of the listed entity. Plantation assets are valued on a combination of EBITDA multiples from disclosed results and independent valuations of land banks. The listed equities portfolio provides liquidity and a secondary return driver alongside the direct holdings.

Revenue derives from plantation operations (crude palm oil and palm kernel sales), together with dividends and realised gains from the listed investments portfolio. The group reports annually and its NAV is assessed periodically by the board with independent verification. The December year-end means the annual report cycle concludes in the first quarter.

The Indonesian operating environment introduces specific risks: land title complexity, replanting obligations, sustainability certification requirements (RSPO and ISPO), labour practices scrutiny, and regulatory uncertainty around export bans and pricing controls. These risks are structural to the sector and are reflected in the discount at which MPE trades relative to comparable ASEAN or Latin American plantation companies.

Recent Catalysts

  • CPO price recovery: Crude palm oil prices have stabilised and partially recovered from 2023 lows, supported by demand from the oleochemical sector and Indonesian domestic market absorption policies. A sustained CPO price above IDR 12,000/kg is supportive of plantation company earnings.
  • Indonesian export policy: Indonesia has been active in managing palm oil export flows, including the old DMO (domestic market obligation) framework. Changes to export policy can cause near-term price volatility but represent a stabilising intent rather than a structural threat.
  • Sustainability certification momentum: MPE's portfolio companies hold RSPO certifications across major estates. This is increasingly a condition of access to premium European and North American buyers and is a structural positive for market access.
  • NAV discount narrowing: The shares have historically traded at a 20-35% discount to assessed NAV. At the current price, this discount is wide even by historical standards, which may attract value-oriented institutional interest if the ESG narrative stabilises.
  • Listed equities portfolio: The portfolio of listed palm oil and agriculture equities offers a liquid return driver alongside direct holdings. Performance here is dependent on sector sentiment and broader commodity markets.

Key Risks

  1. CPO price volatility (HIGH): Palm oil prices are among the most volatile of any major agricultural commodity. A sustained fall in CPO prices directly compresses plantation margins. MPE has no meaningful hedging programme and is exposed to spot price movements. This is the single largest driver of earnings volatility.
  2. Indonesian regulatory risk (HIGH): Indonesia accounts for the majority of global palm oil supply. Regulatory interventions including export bans, domestic market obligations, and export duty changes can cause sharp short-term price dislocations that affect MPE's earnings and NAV in ways that are difficult to predict or hedge.
  3. ESG and deforestation pressure (MEDIUM-HIGH): European institutional investors apply significant ESG discounts to palm oil plantation owners. MPE's RSPO certification is necessary but not sufficient to neutralise this overhang. Any reported deforestation, land dispute, or labour practice incident could trigger a significant de-rating. This risk is sector-wide but particularly acute for Indonesian operators.
  4. Currency risk (MEDIUM): MPE reports in GBP but operates primarily in IDR. The Indonesian Rupiah has been subject to depreciation pressure. A significant IDR depreciation against GBP would reduce translated earnings and NAV for GBP-reporting investors, all else equal.
  5. Concentration risk (MEDIUM): As a small-cap investment company, MPE's NAV is sensitive to a small number of underlying holdings. Underperformance or impairment at the level of a key holding would have a disproportionate effect on group NAV.

Short-term traders will find the commodity price and currency risk too large for comfortable position management.
