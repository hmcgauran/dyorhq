1. Executive Summary

Cardinal Health faces a challenging transition as its core US distribution business battles generic deflation and hospital supply chain pricing pressure, while its Medical segment provides modest diversification — the balance of risks tilts toward reduce.

2. Business Model

Cardinal Health operates two segments: (1) Pharmaceutical Solutions (distributes specialty and conventional pharmaceuticals, over-the-counter products, and consumer health items to hospitals, retail pharmacies, and physician offices; also provides nuclear medicine and patient-specific oncology services); and (2) Medical (manufactures and distributes surgical gloves, fluid management products, blood pressure monitoring cuffs, and remote patient monitoring devices to hospitals and healthcare systems globally). The Pharmaceutical Solutions segment handles ~30% of US pharmaceutical distribution (third-largest after McKesson and Cencora). Cardinal's Medical segment (which includes the Pontemed and Windstone assets) provides a degree of diversification but with lower margins than pure-play distribution.

Cardinal Health is a quality essential-services business facing an earnings headwind from generic drug price deflation (particularly the impact of the Humira biosimilar transition on sister product Yuflyma and other adalimumab-adjoining drugs) and hospital budget tightness. The Medical segment is the strategic highlight — surgical procedure volumes are recovering post-pandemic, and the product portfolio (particularly fluid management and gloves) has pricing power in a consolidated hospital market. At 31x P/E, the market is pricing in considerable improvement that may not materialise.

6. Key Risks (Ranked)

1
Generic pharmaceutical pricing deflation — the ADC/biosimilar wave is reducing rebate income and per-prescription economics across the distribution network.
2
Hospital consolidation creates powerful GPO (group purchasing organisation) buyers that extract price concessions from Cardinal.
3
Opioid litigation exposure remains a significant legal tail risk with state AG litigation ongoing.
4
Medical segment margins under pressure from raw material (nitrile, latex) price volatility and overseas manufacturing cost inflation.
5
Customer concentration in pharmaceutical distribution — top customer (Cigna/Evernorth) accounts for ~15% of segment revenue.
