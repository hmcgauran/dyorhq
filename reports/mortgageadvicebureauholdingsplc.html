Executive Summary

Mortgage Advice Bureau (Holdings) PLC is a UK financial services network operating a network of mortgage advisers who place business with lenders across the UK residential mortgage market. The group earns procuration fees on completed mortgage transactions and related protection insurance products. It is a tightly run, asset-light intermediary model with strong cash conversion and a disciplined approach to adviser recruitment and retention.

The investment case rests on structural tailwinds in UK mortgage intermediation - the proportion of mortgages placed through intermediaries has grown from approximately 50% a decade ago to over 60%, and this channel shift favours networks like MAB1 that can offer advisers leading technology, compliance support, and lender access. The business is sensitively calibrated to UK mortgage market volumes and the interest rate cycle.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY at 582.80 GBX. The structural intermediation trend is intact. The near-term is dictated by UK base rate trajectory and mortgage market turnover. At ~18x trailing earnings with a £600m market cap, the stock is fairly valued for the quality of the franchise and the current stage of the cycle. Await a better entry below 550 GBX or clearer evidence of a sustained remortgage upcycle before building a position.

Business Model

Mortgage Advice Bureau operates as a network and franchise partner for self-employed mortgage advisers and appointed representative firms. The group provides the regulatory infrastructure, compliance framework, training, technology platform, and lender relationships that allow advisers to operate under MAB's regulatory permissions. Advisers earn income through procuration fees on completed mortgage and protection insurance cases; MAB1 takes a share of that fee income under its network agreement structures.

The model is asset-light: no balance sheet risk from mortgage book ownership, no funding risk, and no interest rate exposure on a proprietary book. Revenue is directly tied to mortgage completion volumes and average fee per case. The key operational metrics are adviser count, cases per adviser, and average fee per transaction.

Revenue diversification comes from protection insurance (critical illness, life, buildings and contents) which carries higher margins than mortgage proc fees and provides a degree of income stability when mortgage markets are quiet. The group also generates referral income from conveyancing and other associated services.

Recent Catalysts

  • Bank of England rate cycle inflection: The direction of UK base rate is the primary macro driver for MAB1. As the rate-cutting cycle becomes established, mortgage market activity is expected to recover - both from improved purchase market activity and an anticipated wave of remortgaging as fixed-rate deals expire. This is the key near-term catalyst for the sector.
  • UK mortgage market volumes: Gross mortgage advances have been running below long-run averages due to elevated rates suppressing transaction volumes. Industry forecasts point to a modest recovery in purchase mortgage activity through 2026 as affordability improves and wage growth outpaces mortgage cost increases.
  • Intermediary share gains: The structural shift of UK mortgage distribution toward independent advisers continues. MAB1's scale in the intermediary channel positions it to capture disproportionate share of any market recovery. The group's technology investment and lender panel depth are cited as competitive advantages in adviser retention.
  • Protection insurance momentum: Cross-sell of protection products remains a growth avenue. Advisers placing protection alongside mortgages generates higher-margin income and strengthens client relationships, improving retention and referral flow.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case (25% weight)

A faster Bank of England rate-cutting cycle drives a meaningful recovery in UK mortgage volumes. Remortgaging activity surges as fixed-rate borrowers come off sub-4% deals onto even lower rates. MAB1 benefits from its lender panel breadth and technology platform, gaining adviser share. Protection insurance cross-sell accelerates. EPS pushes toward 38-40p. Price target: 700+ GBX.

Base Case (55% weight)

Gradual rate normalisation supports a slow recovery in purchase market activity through 2026. Remortgaging wave is more modest than the bull scenario as many borrowers are already onTracker orStandard Variable Rates. MAB1 maintains adviser count and per-adviser productivity. EPS holds around 30-34p range. Price target: 560-640 GBX.

Bear Case (20% weight)

Base rate remains elevated into 2027. Mortgage market recovery is delayed. Purchase volumes stay depressed. MAB1's top line comes under pressure as proc fee income is volume-dependent. Adviser attrition increases if income per adviser falls. Competition from digital mortgage brokers intensifies. EPS falls toward 24-27p. Price target: 420-470 GBX.

Key Risks

  1. UK interest rate cycle (HIGH): MAB1's revenue is acutely sensitive to UK mortgage market volumes, which are directly driven by the interest rate cycle. A prolonged period of elevated base rates suppresses both purchase and remortgage activity, directly impacting proc fee income. This is the dominant near-term risk.
  2. Volume concentration risk (MEDIUM): Revenue is tied to completions rather than applications. Any economic shock that reduces buyer confidence and purchase transactions - including a housing market correction - would reduce volumes. MAB1 has no control over these exogenous factors.
  3. Competition from direct and digital lenders (MEDIUM): Some lenders have invested heavily in direct origination channels and digital mortgage platforms, potentially reducing intermediary market share over time. While independent advisers retain a structural advantage in complex cases and protection insurance, simplified mortgage products can be replicated digitally.
  4. Regulatory and conduct risk (MEDIUM): As a regulated intermediary, MAB1 is subject to FCA oversight. Any conduct issues within its network - mis-selling, unsuitable advice, compliance failures - create reputational and financial exposure. FCA regulatory changes affecting mortgage advice standards or lender-adviser relationships could increase compliance costs.
  5. Adviser recruitment and retention (MEDIUM): The business depends on attracting and retaining quality advisers. In a low-volume environment, adviser income falls and attrition risk rises. MAB1's ability to retain its adviser network is a key operational risk and a driver of long-term competitive position.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors seeking exposure to the structural growth of UK mortgage intermediation. Those who believe the Bank of England rate-cutting cycle will accelerate and drive a recovery in UK mortgage volumes. Income-oriented investors who value the group's cash generation and dividend policy. Defensive-minded investors who want a regulated, asset-light financial services franchise without balance sheet risk.

Should avoid: Growth investors requiring a high-conviction multi-year earnings expansion story. Investors who believe digital-first mortgage platforms will rapidly disintermediate traditional adviser networks. Those wanting a low-volatility holding - MAB1 is a leveraged play on UK mortgage market volumes and will exhibit elevated sensitivity to rate cycle changes.
