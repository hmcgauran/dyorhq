Executive Summary

AbbVie's replacement cycle for Humira through Skyrizi and Rinvoq is the central thesis, but the shares trade at a stretched 88x P/E that prices in near-perfect execution — the risk-reward at current levels favours restraint, and REDUCE is the weighted recommendation.

Business Model

AbbVie is a biopharmaceutical company focused on immunology, oncology, neuroscience and aesthetics. Its flagship product, Humira (adalimumab), was the world's best-selling drug for a decade, generating over $20 billion annually at peak, but has faced biosimilar competition in the US since January 2023. AbbVie has managed this cliff through Skyrizi (risankizumab, IL-23 inhibitor) and Rinvoq (upadacitinib, JAK inhibitor), both of which have demonstrated superior clinical profiles in immunology and are growing rapidly.

The Immunology segment accounts for approximately 55% of total revenues. The Botox, Juvederm and other aesthetic products form theAllergan (Allergan) aesthetics franchise. The acquisition of ImmunoGen (targeting antibody-drug conjugates in oncology) adds an emerging ADC platform. Cash generation remains strong, supporting AbbVie's dividend and debt service obligations from theAllergan acquisition.

Recent Catalysts

Skyrizi and Rinvoq have continued to post strong quarterly revenue growth, with combined immunology franchise sales exceeding expectations. The acquisition of ImmunoGen closed in early 2024, adding Elahere (a first-in-class FRα-targeting ADC for platinum-resistant ovarian cancer) and an ADC technology platform. However, Elahere's label expansion into broader ovarian cancer populations has shown mixed Phase III data, and competitive dynamics in ADC oncology are intensifying rapidly. AbbVie has maintained full-year guidance, but EPS growth is highly sensitive to the pace of Humira erosion versus Skyrizi/Rinvoq uptake.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case (77): Skyrizi and Rinvoq fully replace Humira's revenue base and grow total immunology sales above prior Humira peaks. ImmunoGen's ADC platform produces multiple oncology approvals. The aesthetics franchise recovers in volume. EPS grows to $3.20+. Price: $260+.

Base Case (62): Skyrizi/Rinvoq replacement is partially successful but takes longer than market expects. Humira biosimilar erosion stabilises at a lower floor than hoped. ImmunoGen integration is neutral. EPS grows modestly to $2.75–$2.90. Price: $210–$235.

Bear Case (40): Skyrizi and Rinvoq face JAK-class safety label warnings and competitive JAK/IL-23 entrants compress pricing. Elahere fails broader label expansion. Humira biosimilar erosion is faster and deeper than modelled. EPS falls to $2.20–$2.30. Price: below $175.

Weighted Score: 58 — Bull 77 × 30% + Base 62 × 50% + Bear 40 × 20%

Key Risks

  1. Skyrizi/Rinvoq execution risk: These two drugs must collectively replace $20+ billion of annual Humira revenue. Any clinical setback, safety signal, payer pressure or competitive entry that slows uptake directly impairs the investment thesis.
  2. JAK class safety risk: JAK inhibitors carry boxed warnings for serious cardiovascular events and cancer. Regulatory scrutiny could tighten prescribing labels, benefiting competing IL-inhibitor mechanisms (Skyrizi's advantage over Rinvoq in safety profile may erode over time).
  3. Valuation sensitivity: At 88x P/E, AbbVie is priced for near-perfect execution. A single quarter of disappointing guidance could produce significant multiple compression, as the market reprices the timeline for Humira replacement.
  4. ImmunoGen/ADC platform uncertainty: The ADC space is crowded with competitors (Roche, AstraZeneca/Daiichi, Gilead). Elahere's commercial trajectory and the broader platform's productivity remain uncertain.
  5. Aesthetics cyclicality: TheAllergan aesthetics franchise is discretionary and sensitive to consumer sentiment and economic conditions. A consumer slowdown disproportionately affects Botox and filler revenues.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Patients with long investment horizons who believe in the Skyrizi/Rinvoq growth thesis and want a dividend-paying pharma name with an ADC oncology optionality. Not a value proposition at current levels.

Avoiders: Quality-at-a-reasonable-price investors will find better risk-reward in large-cap pharma peers trading at lower P/E multiples. The risk-reward asymmetry is unfavourable at 88x P/E with the Humira cliff still playing out.

Recommendation

REDUCE. AbbVie is a well-managed pharmaceutical franchise with genuine growth catalysts, but the shares are priced for a best-case scenario. The P/E of 88x is difficult to justify on base-case earnings, leaving limited upside and meaningful downside if Skyrizi/Rinvoq uptake slows. Trim on strength and await a more compelling entry.

Entry framework: BUY below $175 (implies 74x P/E on base-case EPS — provides some margin of safety). Reduce on rallies above $225 (implies 95x+ P/E — excessive for a company navigating a major patent cliff). The bar for accumulation is high; patience is warranted.
