Executive Summary

Iris Energy is one of the cleaner listed ways to play the intersection of Bitcoin mining, power infrastructure, and AI/HPC optionality. The market likes that combination, which is why the stock sits just 5.1% below its 52-week high at $36.92. The problem is familiar: when these names are working, they rerate fast and leave little valuation cushion.

The bull case is that IREN successfully evolves from a pure mining name into a broader compute infrastructure story. The bear case is that it remains hostage to Bitcoin economics and capital intensity. Bottom line: HOLD — SPECULATIVE.

Business Model

IREN operates data-centre infrastructure initially built around Bitcoin mining, with a growing narrative around AI and high-performance compute adjacency. The core advantage is access to power and infrastructure in locations where large-scale compute deployment is feasible.

The investment case improves materially if management can monetise that infrastructure beyond mining. If not, the name remains highly cyclical and heavily exposed to crypto economics.
