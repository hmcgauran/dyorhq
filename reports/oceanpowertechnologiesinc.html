Executive Summary

Ocean Power Technologies is a pre-revenue marine energy and autonomy company that has just started converting a record backlog into delivery. The stock has been obliterated - down ~61% from its 52-week high of EUR 0.769 to EUR 0.2976 - largely on cash burn fears and liquidity concerns. That selloff looks overdone relative to the actual contract trajectory.

The company announced a record $19.9M backlog as of January 2026 (+165% YoY), a $163.9M pipeline (+84% YoY), and is executing on a $6.5M DHS/Coast Guard contract with Anduril Industries as prime. First PowerBuoy shipped March 2026. This is a company that has moved from R&D to delivery.

The bear case is real - cash burn is significant, equity raises are likely, and there are no guarantees on pipeline conversion. But at EUR 0.2976, the market is pricing in near-total failure. That's the wrong price for a company with $163.9M in pipeline and proven government contract wins.

Bottom line: BUY on weakness, especially below EUR 0.25. Position size should be speculative (small) given binary cash risk. Treat this as a options-like trade on the backlog converting to revenue. Accumulate in tranches - expect further volatility around quarterly results.

Business Model

Ocean Power Technologies designs and deploys PowerBuoy systems - autonomous offshore platforms that generate power from wave energy and can operate as persistent, unattended marine infrastructure. They also produce WAM-V (Wave Adaptive Modular Vessel) unmanned surface vehicles (USVs) for defence and research customers.

Revenue streams:

  • PowerBuoy systems - sold or leased to defence, security, and offshore energy customers
  • WAM-V USVs - sold to defence/research clients (Nordic order March 2026)
  • MERROWS payload - persistent maritime surveillance payload integrated onto PowerBuoy (DHS/Anduril contract)
  • Data/monitoring services - power + data-as-a-service from deployed buoys

Economic moat: early mover in US government-approved wave energy; Anduril partnership provides credible defence prime integration; growing patent portfolio in marine autonomous power. The defence market for persistent offshore maritime awareness is genuine and growing (US Coast Guard, DHS, NATO allies).

Key vulnerability: pre-revenue. No significant commercial revenue yet. Backlog conversion is everything.

Key Risks

  1. Cash/burn risk (HIGH): Pre-revenue company burning cash. If backlog conversion slips or pipeline doesn't convert, equity raise at distressed prices will heavily dilute. This is the binary risk.
  2. Liquidity risk (HIGH): Trading on FRA (RUOC.F) as main venue. NYSE is the primary exchange but US investors may face liquidity mismatches. Spreads can be wide. Confirm actual liquid ticker with your broker.
  3. Pipeline conversion risk (MEDIUM): $163.9M pipeline sounds large but conversion rates in government contracting are uncertain. 10-15% is realistic; 30%+ would be exceptional.
  4. Contract/timeline risk (MEDIUM): DHS contract is multi-quarter - delivery timing, government budget cycles, and political dynamics can all introduce delays.
  5. Competition risk (MEDIUM): Wave energy is nascent; larger energy majors could enter and outcompete. Defence-USV space is also getting more competitive.
  6. Management/shareholder dilution (MEDIUM): Historical pattern of equity raises suggests further dilution is likely. Check management incentives and cap table.
  7. Volatility risk (HIGH): Micro-cap with news-driven price action. Earnings dates and contract announcements will cause large swings - use this, don't fear it.
