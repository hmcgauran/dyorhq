Executive Summary

Science Group PLC is a Cambridge-based science-led technology and product development company operating across medical, consumer, food and beverage, and industrial end markets. The group provides R&D services, technical consulting, and product development, generated revenue of approximately GBP 95 million in FY2024 with EBIT margins in the mid-teens. The dividend yield is the primary near-term return driver at current levels. HOLD. Conviction Score: 53/100 - HOLD

Business Model

Science Group PLC operates through two principal divisions. The first is Science Group (formerly Sagentia), a technology and product development consultancy serving medical device, consumer product, food and beverage, and industrial clients. Engagements typically span concept development, prototyping, regulatory compliance, and commercialisation support. The second division is Oakdene Hollins, a sustainability consultancy focused on the food, drink, and agri-business sectors, providing lifecycle analysis, carbon footprinting, and strategic sustainability advisory. Revenue is project-based and contractually structured, with a mix of fixed-fee and time-and-materials arrangements. The model is people-intensive with low capital expenditure requirements. Client relationships are typically long-standing, with top-10 clients accounting for a meaningful share of group revenue. Geographic exposure is predominantly the UK and Europe, with a growing share of US-based work through the medical and consumer verticals.

Key Risks

  1. #1 Economic sensitivity: Science Group's client base includes a material share of consumer, food, and industrial companies that have reduced discretionary R&D spend during the prior 18 months. A prolonged downturn would suppress utilisation and margins.
  2. #2 Client concentration: The group derives a significant share of revenue from its top 10 clients. Loss of any material client relationship would have a disproportionate impact on group earnings.
  3. #3 Small-cap liquidity: The stock trades on a narrow register with limited free float. Institutional ownership is thin, which suppresses valuations relative to listed peers and creates liquidity risk for larger investors.
  4. #4 US expansion execution risk: The growth strategy relies on winning US-based clients in medical and consumer verticals. This requires sustained investment in business development, talent acquisition, and local credibility -- with no guarantee of a predictable revenue payback.
  5. #5 Key person risk: Given the people-intensive nature of the business, the departure of senior technical or commercial leadership could disproportionately affect client relationships and delivery quality.
