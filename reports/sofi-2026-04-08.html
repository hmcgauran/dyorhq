Executive Summary

SoFi is a digital financial services company offering student loan refinancing, personal loans, credit cards, and savings accounts through a mobile-first platform. The company is attempting to transition from a fintech lender to a full financial services platform, competing with traditional banks and neobanks for deposit gathering and lending.

At $16.62, the stock has de-rated significantly from its SPAC-era highs. The path to profitability is underway but the interest rate environment creates significant headwinds for lending businesses — higher rates reduce loan demand and increase funding costs simultaneously.

Bottom line: REDUCE. The fintech lending model is structurally challenged by the current rate environment. Only appropriate as a speculative position for investors with high risk tolerance. Conviction: 42/100.

Business Model

SoFi earns revenue from: (1) net interest income on loans it holds on balance sheet, (2) loan origination fees, (3) membership and subscription fees (SoFi Money, SoFi Invest), and (4) referral fees from third-party financial products. The Galileo banking-as-a-service platform provides B2B revenue from fintech companies using SoFi's infrastructure.
