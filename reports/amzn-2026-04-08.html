Executive Summary

Amazon is the world's largest e-commerce marketplace and cloud infrastructure provider, operating a network effect business with two dominant platforms — marketplace and AWS — that reinforce each other. AWS is the leading cloud platform, generating $100B+ in annual revenue with strong growth and significant AI opportunity through Bedrock. The e-commerce marketplace generates massive cash flow that funds AWS's AI infrastructure investment. The stock at $221.65 is 2% below its 52w high and is the best-risk-adjusted large-cap tech exposure available.

The investment case centres on AWS's AI-driven growth re-acceleration, the profitability improvement in the e-commerce business through regionalisation and logistics optimisation, and the emerging monetisation of Alexa AI and drone delivery infrastructure. Amazon is one of the few businesses that can sustain 15%+ revenue growth at a $2.4T scale.

Bottom line: BUY. The pullback from 52w highs is modest but the risk/reward is compelling at $221.65. AWS AI growth, e-commerce profitability expansion, and advertising momentum are all pointing in the right direction. Conviction: 76/100.

Business Model

Amazon operates three core businesses: North America e-commerce (approximately 45% of revenue), International e-commerce (approximately 25%), and Amazon Web Services (approximately 25%). The Advertising segment is a high-margin overlay on all three that has become a significant profit driver in its own right.

AWS is the dominant cloud platform, competing with Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud. AWS's AI strategy centres on Bedrock — a managed service for building generative AI applications — and Trainium/Inferentia custom AI chips that compete with NVIDIA GPUs. The AI opportunity for AWS is not just compute but the full stack of enterprise AI adoption.

The e-commerce marketplace is transitioning from a logistics company that happens to sell goods to a logistics infrastructure provider that monetises its fulfilment network through third-party seller services. This shift is improving margins structurally.

Recent Catalysts

  • Q4 2025 earnings (6 Feb 2026): AWS grew 19% YoY, ahead of expectations, as AI workloads drove accelerated adoption of compute and Bedrock. Advertising grew 27%. North America e-commerce margins hit a multi-year high. EPS of $1.89 beat consensus by $0.12.
  • AWS Bedrock momentum: Bedrock is the fastest-growing service in AWS history. Major enterprise customers are using Bedrock to build RAG applications, code generation tools, and customer service AI. This is the primary AI monetisation vehicle.
  • Kuiper satellite broadband launch: Amazon's Project Kuiper satellite constellation is launching, competing with Starlink. The long-term opportunity is significant but capital intensive and multi-year.
  • DOGE/government efficiency focus: Amazon Web Services is a major beneficiary of federal government cloud migration. Any federal efficiency initiatives that drive cloud adoption could be a direct catalyst.

Thesis Evaluation

Base (55%): AWS grows 18–22% on AI compute demand. Advertising sustains 20%+ growth. E-commerce margins improve gradually to 6–7%. Price target $230–255.

Key Risks

  1. Azure competition (MEDIUM): Microsoft Azure's integration with OpenAI and Microsoft 365 is a meaningful competitive advantage in enterprise AI adoption. AWS must demonstrate that Bedrock's model choice and enterprise features can compete effectively.
  2. E-commerce competition (MEDIUM): Temu and Shein are taking share in ultra-fast fashion and general merchandise. If they expand categories and logistics capability, they become a structural headwind for Amazon's marketplace.
  3. AI capex pressure (MEDIUM): Amazon is investing heavily in custom AI chips and data centre infrastructure. The ROI of this investment is uncertain — if AI adoption is slower than expected, the capex burden will weigh on free cash flow.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Anyone who wants large-cap technology exposure with the best risk/reward at current prices. Investors who want AI infrastructure exposure through a business with a proven monetisation model. Those who want a business that can sustain 15%+ revenue growth at $2.4T scale.

Should avoid: Pure value investors — Amazon's P/E reflects significant AI optimism. Investors who think Azure will definitively win enterprise AI. Those who prefer dividend income — Amazon does not pay a dividend.

BUY at $221.65. Amazon offers the best combination of quality, AI exposure, and valuation among the mega-cap technology companies at current levels. The AWS AI growth story is credible, the advertising business is a high-margin flywheel, and the e-commerce profitability improvement is structural. This is a core holding for a technology portfolio.
