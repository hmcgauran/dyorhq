Executive Summary

Tamboran Resources Corp is a micro-cap Australian gas exploration company focused on the Beetaloo Basin in the Northern Territory. With a market capitalisation of approximately £30 million and no material revenue, TBN is a binary catalyst play: success at the Shenandoah-1 and other Beetaloo wells unlocks material value; failure leaves the company requiring additional capital raises that would be highly dilutive at current levels.

The investment case rests on the quality of the Beetaloo Basin itself — a world-class dry gas system comparable to major US shale plays — combined with early well results that have demonstrated positive flow rates. The risk is execution: this is pre-revenue, pre-development, and capital-intensive. TBN needs either a strategic partner, an LNG off-take agreement, or farm-in partner to fund the development pathway beyond exploration.

Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY. TBN offers leveraged exposure to Australian gas at a micro-cap entry point. Appropriate for investors with high risk tolerance who understand the binary nature of the catalyst. Position size should be limited accordingly.

Business Model

Tamboran Resources is an exploration and development-stage oil and gas company. Its primary asset is a 100% working interest in approximately 1.8 million acres across four exploration permits in the Beetaloo Basin, NT Australia — one of the largest contiguous acreage positions in the basin. The company is focused on the dry gas window of the Beetaloo, targeting the middle Velkerri formation.

The business model is early-stage: appraising discovered resources, seeking to prove commercial flow rates, and advancing toward a development decision. Tamboran has no current production and no material revenue. All activity is funded by equity capital raises and farm-in arrangements.

Key assets include the Shenandoah-1 well (which encountered gas shows across multiple intervals), and the A series of wells targeting the middle Velkerri shale. TBN operates its own acreage and has engaged with Santos and Origin Energy on neighbouring Beetaloo positions, indicating basin-level commercial viability.

Recent Catalysts

  • Shenandoah-1 flow testing: Results from the Shenandoah-1 well in the Beetaloo Basin demonstrated gas flow. This was the primary catalyst the stock has been building toward. Investors will be watching for sustained flow rates and gas composition data.
  • Beetaloo Basin commercial progress: Origin Energy and Santos have advanced development discussions on adjacent acreage. Interoperability of infrastructure (pipelines, processing) across the Beetaloo is improving the development economics for all operators, including Tamboran.
  • Australian east coast gas scarcity: The Australian Energy Market Operator (AEMO) has flagged supply constraints on the east coast gas market over the next several years. New supply from the Beetaloo would be structurally advantaged in this environment.
  • Capital raising risk: TBN has required periodic equity raises to fund operations. Any dilution event is a known risk at this stage. The market has occasionally priced in negative scenarios around capital availability.

Key Risks

  1. Well result binary risk (HIGH): This is the primary risk. If Shenandoah-1 or subsequent wells fail to demonstrate sustained commercial flow, the investment thesis collapses. Exploration stage assets are binary by nature; there is no middle ground.
  2. Capital availability and dilution (HIGH): TBN has no revenue and material capex requirements to advance development. Further equity raises are likely. At a sub-£30m market cap, even modest raises at prevailing prices are significantly dilutive. The market has historically priced in dilution risk.
  3. Liquidity and spread (MEDIUM): At approximately 2.5m shares traded daily on average, this is a low liquidity micro-cap. The bid-ask spread is wide, making entry and exit at precise prices difficult. Position-building requires patience and an understanding of market impact.
  4. Development pathway complexity (MEDIUM): Beetaloo gas requires pipeline infrastructure to reach east coast markets. While Origin and Santos are advancing pipeline solutions, the timing and cost of midstream infrastructure is a key variable for all Beetaloo operators, including TBN.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors with high risk tolerance seeking micro-cap energy exposure with a specific catalyst. Those who understand that early-stage Australian gas explorers can generate significant returns from successful appraisal campaigns. Investors comfortable with binary outcomes and the potential for a total loss of capital in a downside scenario.

Should avoid: Anyone requiring income or near-term returns from their investment. Risk-averse investors who cannot tolerate binary outcomes or the potential for significant dilution. Those who need liquidity — the spread and daily volume make this an illiquid position relative to typical portfolio allocations.
