Executive Summary

HydroGraph Clean Power Inc is a development-stage clean technology company operating at the intersection of advanced materials and green energy. The company's core proprietary technology is a detonation synthesis process for producing high-purity graphene and graphene-related materials, alongside a complementary green hydrogen production capability. Graphene — a single-atom-thick sheet of carbon arranged in a hexagonal lattice — is widely regarded as a transformative material with applications across composites, electronics, energy storage, filtration, and coatings. HydroGraph's detonation process is designed to produce graphene at scale and at lower cost than conventional production methods such as chemical vapour deposition (CVD) or liquid-phase exfoliation.

The stock trades on OTCMKTS, limiting it to a US investor audience that can trade OTC securities. At $5.11 the company carries a market capitalisation of approximately $2.36 billion on 340.175 million shares outstanding — a headline valuation that requires significant scrutiny in the context of a development-stage OTC-listed company. The 52-week range of $0.15 to $8.37 reflects the extreme volatility typical of micro-cap OTC names, and the gap between this valuation and any observable revenue trajectory is a material risk that investors must price in explicitly.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY — SPECULATIVE. Conviction: 53/100. Business Model

HydroGraph Clean Power's business model is centred on the commercialisation of proprietary detonation synthesis technology for graphene production. In a detonation synthesis process, a carbon-containing precursor (typically a hydrocarbon fuel or explosive compound) is detonated in a controlled chamber, producing graphene sheets as a byproduct of the rapid thermal decomposition. The process requires no catalyst, uses relatively inexpensive inputs, and is designed to be scalable in a way that conventional graphene synthesis methods are not.

The company has also positioned itself in the green hydrogen space, typically by integrating hydrogen production with its core process or positioning its graphene materials as components in hydrogen storage and fuel cell applications. The strategic logic is to offer a materials solution that addresses both sides of the clean energy transition: carbon-based advanced materials and hydrogen energy carriers.

The commercial model is likely structured around direct graphene sales to industrial customers, licensing of the production process to partners, and potentially bespoke R&D collaborations with universities or corporate research divisions. However, the specific customer base, contract pipeline, and revenue recognition methodology are not available from live research sources at the time of this report — a material information gap that investors should treat as a yellow flag.
