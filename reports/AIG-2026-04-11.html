Executive Summary

AIG is a complex, restructuring multi-line insurer that has made meaningful progress simplifying its operations and strengthening its balance sheet, but the valuation at low-14x earnings is not compelling given persistent catastrophe volatility and execution uncertainty, warranting a REDUCE.

Business Model

AIG is one of the largest insurance organisations in the world, operating through two core divisions following its multi-year restructuring:

General Insurance (Commercial & Personal Lines)~65% of pre-tax income; commercial P&C, specialty lines, A&H, personal auto and homeowners
Life & Retirement~35% of pre-tax income; individual life, group benefits, retirement accounts

AIG's commercial insurance operations serve large multinational corporations, middle-market businesses, and specialty niche markets including political risk, cyber liability, and marine insurance. The personal lines business has been significantly scaled back following the 2021 Strawberry Hill reinsurance transaction. AIG earns revenues primarily through insurance premiums and investment income on the float generated by its insurance liabilities.

Key Risks (Ranked)

#RiskDescription
1Catastrophe loss volatilityAIG retains meaningful catastrophe exposure in its General Insurance book. A major US hurricane or earthquake event in a single quarter could wipe out annual earnings and trigger a sharp share price decline.
2Reserve adequacyAIG has a history of reserve deficiencies, most notably from the 2000s ALFS business. Any re-emergence of reserve shortfalls would undermine investor confidence and trigger a sell-off disproportionate to the actual loss.
3Leadership transitionCEO Zaffino has been the architect of the restructuring; an unplanned or poorly managed succession could signal a pause in strategic execution or a change in capital allocation priorities.
4Life & Retirement spread compressionAIG's Life & Retirement segment is sensitive to interest rate movements; if the Federal Reserve cuts rates faster than expected, the spread income on fixed annuity products compresses, reducing earnings.
5Structural complexityAIG's multi-segment, multi-geography structure creates opacity for investors; the holding company complexity and legacy litigation overhang (AIGFP / Financial Products unit) still generate occasional surprise charges.
