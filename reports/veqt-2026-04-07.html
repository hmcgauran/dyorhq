Executive Summary

VEQT is a passive, all-equity index fund-of-funds ETF that holds a diversified portfolio of Vanguard's underlying regional equity ETFs — covering Canada, the US, Europe, Asia, and emerging markets. It offers 100% equity exposure with 13,000+ stocks globally, at a confirmed 0.00% management expense ratio (MER). There is no investment thesis here beyond the long-term equity risk premium: buy global equities cheaply, hold forever, benefit from compounding.

Business Model

VEQT is a fund-of-funds ETF. It does not hold individual stocks — it holds other Vanguard ETFs, which in turn hold individual securities. The portfolio is weighted approximately:

  • ~45% North America — VCN (Canada), VSP (US), VUN (US), VGG (US growth)
  • ~25% Europe — VEF (Europe), VDU (UK), VIU (Europe ex-UK)
  • ~15% Asia Pacific — VRE (Real estate), VA (Australia), VEE (Emerging markets Asia)
  • ~15% Emerging Markets — VEF, VEE, VWO-weighted exposure

The fund rebalances quarterly. It holds 13,000+ individual stocks across 40+ countries. It is a complete, self-contained global equity portfolio in a single ticker. The 0.00% MER is the headline — it is effectively free to hold from a fee perspective. Vanguard achieves this partly through fee waiver programmes that may have an end date; investors should confirm the current fee structure is still honoured.

There is no active management, no stock selection, no market timing. The fund owns the market. That is the entire proposition.

Key Risks

  1. Market risk (HIGH): VEQT is 100% equities. In a sustained bear market, a 30–40% drawdown is realistic. If you need the money within 3–5 years, this is the wrong product. The 2025 low of CAD 39.75 (from CAD 56 high) was a −29% drawdown — that is the risk you are taking.
  2. MER/fee waiver risk (LOW–MEDIUM): The 0.00% MER is confirmed but may reflect a fee waiver programme. If Vanguard reduces the waiver, the MER could increase. Monitor the fund's annual report for fee changes.
  3. Currency risk (MEDIUM): VEQT holds significant US and international exposure but is denominated in CAD. A rising Canadian dollar will reduce returns for CAD-based investors from non-Canada holdings. A falling CAD will amplify returns from international holdings.
  4. Canada concentration (LOW–MEDIUM): The underlying ETFs include significant Canadian equity exposure. Canada is ~25-30% of global equity markets by cap but VEQT likely has higher Canadian weighting than a pure global market-cap weighting would suggest.
  5. Complexity risk (LOW): As a fund-of-funds, VEQT has a layered fee structure (the underlying ETFs also have small MERs). The total fee drag is still extremely low but not technically zero when you include the cost of the underlying holdings.
  6. Liquidity risk (LOW): ETF structure means you can sell at any time at market price. Bid-ask spreads are narrow. This is not a concern for VEQT.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: long-term investors who want a simple, low-cost, globally diversified all-equity core holding and can tolerate equity-market drawdowns.

Should avoid: anyone with a short time horizon, low tolerance for volatility, or a need for income stability over growth.
