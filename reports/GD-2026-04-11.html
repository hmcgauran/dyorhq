Key Risks

  1. Programme cost overrun exposure: Fixed-price development contracts carry inherent risk of cost overruns, particularly in submarine and advanced aircraft programmes.
  2. Electric Boat concentration risk: The Columbia-class submarine programme represents a huge revenue chunk and any disruption (labour, supply chain, design change) has outsized financial impact.
  3. Gulfstream cyclicality: Large-cabin business jet demand is sensitive to economic conditions and corporate profit cycles.
  4. Defence budget political risk: Bipartisan support for defence spending is generally strong, but periodic budget ceiling debates and sequestration risk create uncertainty.
  5. Customer concentration: US Navy and US Army represent the majority of revenues; any shift in DoD priorities could materially affect GD's addressable market.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Defensive-minded investors seeking high-quality, dividend-paying US defence exposure with long-cycle backlog visibility. Suitable as a core defence sector holding.

Avoiders: Value investors will find cheaper defence peers. Growth investors seeking secular AI-adjacent themes should look elsewhere. Short-horizon investors should avoid due to programme-cycle illiquidity.
