Executive Summary

The stock has been obliterated — down ~61% from its 52-week high of €0.769 to €0.2976 — largely on cash burn fears and liquidity concerns. The company announced a record $19.9M backlog as of January 2026 (+165% YoY), a $163.9M pipeline (+84% YoY), and is executing on a $6.5M DHS/Coast Guard contract with Anduril Industries as prime. First PowerBuoy shipped March 2026. This is a company that has moved from R&D to delivery.

The bear case is real — cash burn is significant, equity raises are likely, and there are no guarantees on pipeline conversion. But at €0.2976, the market is pricing in near-total failure. That's the wrong price for a company with $163.9M in pipeline and proven government contract wins.

Bottom line: BUY on weakness, especially below €0.25. Position size should be speculative (small) given binary cash risk. Treat this as a options-like trade on the backlog converting to revenue. Accumulate in tranches — expect further volatility around quarterly results.

Business Model

Ocean Power Technologies designs and deploys PowerBuoy® systems — autonomous offshore platforms that generate power from wave energy and can operate as persistent, unattended marine infrastructure. They also produce WAM-V (Wave Adaptive Modular Vessel) unmanned surface vehicles (USVs) for defence and research customers.

Revenue streams:

  • PowerBuoy systems — sold or leased to defence, security, and offshore energy customers
  • WAM-V USVs — sold to defence/research clients (Nordic order March 2026)
  • MERROWS® payload — persistent maritime surveillance payload integrated onto PowerBuoy (DHS/Anduril contract)
  • Data/monitoring services — power + data-as-a-service from deployed buoys

Economic moat: early mover in US government-approved wave energy; Anduril partnership provides credible defence prime integration; growing patent portfolio in marine autonomous power. The defence market for persistent offshore maritime awareness is genuine and growing (US Coast Guard, DHS, NATO allies).

Key vulnerability: pre-revenue. No significant commercial revenue yet. Backlog conversion is everything.
