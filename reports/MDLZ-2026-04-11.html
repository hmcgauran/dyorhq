Executive Summary

Mondelez is a high-quality global snacks franchise with strong brands but faces structural volume headwinds in developed markets and is priced at a premium multiple that leaves insufficient upside to warrant a new position, supporting a REDUCE.

Business Model

Mondelez is one of the world's largest snack companies, with a portfolio of iconic brands across biscuits, chocolate, confectionery, and powdered beverages. The business is geographically diversified, with significant emerging market exposure that provides long-term volume growth potential:

Biscuits (COGS, biscuits, baked snacks)~35% of revenue; Oreo, Ritz, Chips Ahoy, BelVita, Milka
Chocolate (global chocolate)~30% of revenue; Cadbury, Milka, Toblerone, Lacta
Confectionery (gum & candy)~18% of revenue; Trident, Halls, Strudel, Sour
Beverages & Other~17% of revenue; Tang, Cadbury Hot Chocolate, powdered drinks

Mondelez generates approximately 45% of revenue from emerging markets, providing demographic tailwinds but also currency and political risks. It operates a "smart snacking" strategy focused on portion control, on-the-go formats, and premium niche brands. Pricing power is moderate (~3-4% annually) but cannot fully offset volume declines in mature markets.

Recent Catalysts (Last 3-6 Months)

Q4 2025 Organic Revenue Growth: Mondelez reported 4.5% organic revenue growth in Q4 2025, driven primarily by pricing with flat to modestly negative volume in North America, continuing the pattern of price over volume that characterises the current environment.

Emerging Market Currency Headwinds: Strengthening of the US dollar in early 2026 has weighed on reported revenue from key emerging markets including Brazil, India, and Indonesia, creating a headwind versus consensus estimates.

Cocoa Price Surge Impact: Cocoa futures have moderated from 2024 highs but remain elevated year-on-year, creating input cost pressure on the chocolate segment margins; management has taken additional pricing action but the full pass-through remains incomplete.

Voluntary Recall in Europe: A voluntary recall of certain Milka products in Western Europe was announced in February 2026 due to a supplier-side contamination issue; the financial impact is immaterial but the reputational risk is a watch item.
