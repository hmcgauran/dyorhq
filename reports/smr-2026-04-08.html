Executive Summary

NuScale has genuine strategic value as the first U.S. NRC-approved SMR design, but the equity has been crushed because regulatory leadership has not yet translated into robust commercial execution. The live price is $9.16, down 84.0% from the 52 week high of $57.42 and effectively sitting on the 52 week low.

The theme is attractive, nuclear power for grid resilience and AI-era electricity demand, but the stock setup is poor. This is still a loss-making business with EPS -2.17 and a market cap of roughly $3.10bn. The market is no longer paying for the story alone.

Bottom line: REDUCE. Keep only as a small speculative position if you want exposure to SMR optionality. It is not the strongest way to play the nuclear build-out today.

Business Model

NuScale develops small modular reactor technology intended for utility, industrial and data-centre scale deployment. The commercial model depends on selling reactor design packages, engineering services, project development support and ultimately participating in long-duration reactor build programmes.

The strategic edge is regulatory credibility and early mover status. The problem is that nuclear technology alone does not create equity value unless projects are financeable, customers commit and timelines stay credible.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull (25%)

Large commercial agreement, financing clarity and improving sentiment on nuclear-for-AI re-rate the stock sharply from depressed levels.

Base (45%)

NuScale remains strategically relevant but commercial timelines stay slow, leaving the stock range-bound and dependent on sentiment swings.

Bear (30%)

Project conversion remains weak, dilution risk rises and the market further cuts the valuation of non-profitable nuclear developers.

The key issue is not technology relevance. It is whether NuScale can turn credibility into bankable commercial reality before investors lose interest again.

Key Risks

  1. Commercial execution risk: projects may stay conceptual rather than contractual.
  2. Funding risk: prolonged losses can force dilutive financing.
  3. Timeline risk: nuclear schedules slip easily and punish sentiment quickly.
  4. Competitive risk: other reactor platforms or suppliers may capture the real economic value.
  5. Narrative decay: the AI-power theme may rotate into stronger businesses rather than pure-play developers.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: investors who want a small, high-risk option on the SMR theme and are comfortable with deep volatility, funding risk and long commercial timelines.

Should avoid: anyone looking for proven earnings, stable compounding or a high-confidence nuclear exposure.

Recommendation

REDUCE.

NuScale is still investable as a small, high-risk option on the SMR theme, but it is not the highest-quality nuclear equity. The stock is telling you that the market wants proof, not promise. Until commercial traction improves materially, risk-adjusted capital is better deployed elsewhere.

Entry / exit framework:

  • Below $9: only for aggressive speculative nibbling, because that would mean the market is pressing into fresh lows
  • $9 to $12: avoid adding size until the chart and commercial newsflow improve
  • $15 to $18: first zone where a recovery starts to look credible rather than reflexive
  • Above $20: reassess, because that would imply the market is finally paying for improved execution again
