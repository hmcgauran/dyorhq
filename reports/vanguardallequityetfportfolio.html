Executive Summary

VEQT is a passive, all-equity index fund-of-funds ETF that holds a diversified portfolio of Vanguard's underlying regional equity ETFs - covering Canada, the US, Europe, Asia, and emerging markets. It offers 100% equity exposure with 13,000+ stocks globally, at a confirmed 0.00% management expense ratio (MER). There is no investment thesis here beyond the long-term equity risk premium: buy global equities cheaply, hold forever, benefit from compounding.

At CAD 54.60, the fund is sitting ~4% below its 52-week high of CAD 56.92 and has recovered strongly from the CAD 39.75 low during the 2025 market volatility. The fund is inherently unexciting - which is the point. For a Canadian investor looking for a single, set-and-forget global equity holding, VEQT is extremely difficult to beat on a cost and diversification basis.

Bottom line: BUY for long-term investors (5+ year horizon). The 0.00% MER makes this one of the cheapest ways to own global equities in Canada. The near-term technical level at CAD 56.92 is the resistance to watch. Below CAD 50 would be a particularly compelling entry point given the recovery from the CAD 39 lows. Not a trading vehicle - a core portfolio holding.

Business Model

VEQT is a fund-of-funds ETF. It does not hold individual stocks - it holds other Vanguard ETFs, which in turn hold individual securities. The portfolio is weighted approximately:

  • ~45% North America - VCN (Canada), VSP (US), VUN (US), VGG (US growth)
  • ~25% Europe - VEF (Europe), VDU (UK), VIU (Europe ex-UK)
  • ~15% Asia Pacific - VRE (Real estate), VA (Australia), VEE (Emerging markets Asia)
  • ~15% Emerging Markets - VEF, VEE, VWO-weighted exposure

The fund rebalances quarterly. It holds 13,000+ individual stocks across 40+ countries. It is a complete, self-contained global equity portfolio in a single ticker. The 0.00% MER is the headline - it is effectively free to hold from a fee perspective. Vanguard achieves this partly through fee waiver programmes that may have an end date; investors should confirm the current fee structure is still honoured.

There is no active management, no stock selection, no market timing. The fund owns the market. That is the entire proposition.
