Executive Summary

Comcast trades at 5.5x earnings with a 3.2% dividend yield — the market is pricing a structural and permanent earnings decline. That may be too pessimistic. Q1 2026 earnings on April 23 are expected to show a sharp EPS decline (consensus $0.82 vs $1.08 in Q1 2025, a fall of approximately 24%), but the Xfinity StreamSaver bundles launched in April 2026 — now incorporating Peacock alongside Netflix, Apple TV, Disney+, Hulu, and HBO Max in a single marketplace — represent a meaningful strategic pivot. If cord-cutting headwinds are priced in too aggressively, Comcast is a deep-value case. SPECULATIVE BUY reflects the compelling valuation support at 5.5x but not a high-conviction view given the secular cable subscriber losses that remain the primary bear argument.

Business Model

Comcast operates two core businesses. NBCUniversal owns and produces film and television content, operates the Peacock streaming platform (36 million subscribers), and runs theme parks and studios. The Connectivity segment provides residential broadband internet via the Xfinity brand — the dominant cable broadband provider in the US — and mobile services through Xfinity Mobile (MVNO on the Verizon network). The Xfinity brand also serves as the aggregation platform for the expanded StreamSaver marketplace, bundling multiple third-party streaming services alongside Peacock.

Revenue is split roughly equally between Connectivity (broadband, cable TV, mobile) and NBCUniversal (content, streaming, parks). The cable broadband business remains competitively advantaged versus fixed wireless alternatives in most markets due to superior speed and reliability at comparable price points, though 5G Home Internet from T-Mobile and AT&T represents a growing competitive threat in suburban markets.

Recent Catalysts

The primary near-term catalyst is Q1 2026 earnings, scheduled for April 23, 2026. Analysts expect EPS of approximately $0.82 on revenue of $30.42 billion, representing a year-over-year EPS decline of roughly 24% from the $1.08 reported in Q1 2025. Full-year 2026 EPS consensus stands at $3.66.

Strategically, Xfinity's April 2026 expansion of StreamSaver bundles is the most concrete development. The marketplace now aggregates Peacock, Netflix, Apple TV, the Disney+/Hulu Bundle, and HBO Max in a single destination, with savings of up to 45% for customers purchasing multiple services. This positions Comcast as a streaming aggregator rather than purely a content competitor — a potentially valuable position as the streaming market saturates and consumers seek simplification. Q4 2025 results showed an EPS beat, providing some confidence in execution.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case (60)

StreamSaver aggregation strategy succeeds in retaining broadband customers and cross-selling multiple subscriptions. Cord-cutting slows as fixed wireless alternatives prove inferior at scale. Peacock reaches profitability. Management deploys strong free cash flow into debt reduction and buybacks, supporting the balance sheet. Price: $42+.

Base Case (45)

Cable broadband subscriber losses continue at a moderate pace. Xfinity Mobile provides partial offset with net additions. NBCUniversal content revenues stabilise. The dividend is maintained and covered by FCF. Earnings-per-share declines modestly before stabilising. Price: $32-$40.

Bear Case (28)

Fixed wireless broadband (T-Mobile, AT&T) accelerates subscriber losses beyond current trend. Peacock content costs remain elevated without commensurate subscriber growth. Key Risks

  1. Accelerating cord-cutting: Cable TV subscriber losses have been underway for a decade but may be entering a more damaging phase as fixed wireless broadband improves. Once broadband customers churn, the entire revenue stack is at risk.
  2. Fixed wireless competition: T-Mobile 5G Home Internet and AT&T Fiber expansions are credible threats in Comcast's suburban heartland. If these services achieve price/speed parity with cable, Xfinity's primary moat — last-mile broadband dominance — erodes.
  3. Streaming margin pressure: Peacock's 36 million subscribers represent meaningful scale but the service has not yet demonstrated profitability. Content investment remains high, particularly for live sports rights (NFL, Premier League, Olympics), which are expensive and may not retain subscribers at expected rates.
  4. Re-rating requires earnings stability: At 5.5x, the market has already made a bearish call. A re-rating to even 7-8x requires evidence that earnings declines are bottoming, not accelerating. Q1 2026 results are the immediate test.
  5. Advertising cycle exposure: NBCUniversal's advertising revenues are exposed to economic cycles and the shift of ad spend to digital platforms. A recession would compound the structural pressures on the content business.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Deep-value investors who believe the cable broadband cash flow is more durable than the market prices, and who see the StreamSaver bundling strategy as a legitimate retention tool. Yield seekers comfortable with the dividend at 3.2% and willing to accept some risk of a dividend cut if Q1 results disappoint. Contrarians who think the market is extrapolating the bear case too aggressively.

Avoiders: Growth-oriented investors will find better risk-reward in technology or pure-play streaming peers. Anyone who believes cord-cutting is an irreversible secular trend that will ultimately claim the broadband business, not just cable TV, should not own this. The thesis requires execution on a hard thing — reversing or stabilising subscriber losses — not just better content.

Recommendation

HOLD / ACCUMULATE ON WEAKNESS. At approximately $34 and 5.5x forward EPS, Comcast is not obviously expensive. The dividend at 3.2% provides a floor of sorts. The April 23 Q1 2026 earnings report is the critical near-term catalyst: a beat could provide a re-rating catalyst; a miss (particularly EPS below $0.73) would confirm the bear case and likely push the stock toward the $26-27 area.

Entry framework: BUY below $28 (sub-5x P/E on FY2026E EPS, closer to bear-case pricing). Add on further weakness below $26. Reduce if price reclaims $38+ as that approaches fair value range on base case. Position size should remain speculative given the structural uncertainty on the Connectivity segment.
