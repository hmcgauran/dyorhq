Executive Summary

Business Model

Citigroup is a global diversified bank operating across five primary segments:

  • Services: Treasury and trade solutions, working capital, and cash management for corporate and institutional clients.
  • Markets: Fixed-income and equities trading, securities services, and prime brokerage - providing market access and liquidity.
  • Banking: Investment banking, advisory, equity and debt underwriting, and strategic financing for corporate clients.
  • US Personal Banking: Retail branch banking, credit cards, personal loans, and deposits through the US consumer franchise.
  • Wealth Management: Private banking, investment advice, and tailored credit solutions for HNW and ultra-HNW individuals.

Citigroup is in the midst of a multi-year efficiency programme under CEO Jane Fraser's leadership, targeting cost reductions and a simplified organisational structure to improve returns on capital.

Key Risks

1Commercial real estate credit: Citi holds a disproportionate share of office-centric CRE loans. A prolonged work-from-home trend and rising vacancy rates could drive significant loan loss provisions beyond consensus estimates.
2FRB resolution plan failure: Repeated non-acceptance of the living-will plan by the Federal Reserve could impose operational restrictions, higher capital requirements, or mandated structural changes that impair franchise value.
3Structural competitive disadvantage: JPMorgan and BofA consistently outperform Citi in key revenue pools (investment banking, consumer banking, credit cards), suggesting the franchise is losing ground structurally rather than cyclically.
4Transformation execution risk: The multi-year cost-reduction and simplification programme is running behind original timelines and cost savings targets; further delays erode market confidence and management credibility.
5Macro slowdown: A deterioration in US economic conditions would amplify all the above risks - credit losses, capital markets revenues, and the pace of transformation would all be adversely affected simultaneously.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Appropriate For
