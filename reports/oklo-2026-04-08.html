Executive Summary

Oklo is the cleaner listed bet on the AI-power and advanced nuclear narrative. The company is pitching compact reactor deployment into a market that suddenly cares about firm power for hyperscalers, industrial loads and constrained grids. The live price is $46.59, with a market cap of about $8.09bn.

The attraction is obvious: if hyperscaler-linked nuclear demand becomes real, Oklo has a narrative that can scale fast in investor imagination. The problem is that this is still a pre-profit company with EPS -0.72, and the stock has already shown how violently expectations can reverse. It is down 76.0% from the 52 week high of $193.84.

Bottom line: HOLD for speculative upside. Of the two higher-risk names here, Oklo is the better one, but it is still a story stock rather than a proven operating business.

Business Model

Oklo is developing microreactors intended to provide reliable low-carbon power for customers that cannot depend on intermittent generation alone. The commercial appeal is strongest where uptime matters, data centres, industrial sites, military-related applications and remote power needs.

The equity story is tightly linked to the idea that AI infrastructure growth will require dedicated clean baseload power. If that thesis holds, Oklo has stronger thematic positioning than many peers because it sits directly inside the “power for compute” narrative.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull (30%)

Oklo lands additional strategic counterparties, keeps regulatory progress moving and becomes the market’s preferred listed AI-power nuclear equity again.

Base (45%)

The narrative stays alive, but delivery remains gradual. The stock trades on sentiment, partner announcements and licensing milestones rather than operating cash generation.

Bear (25%)

Development or licensing timelines slip, customer interest proves softer than expected and the stock de-rates further because valuation still outruns business maturity.

The attraction here is upside asymmetry if advanced nuclear becomes part of the real AI infrastructure stack. The risk is that the market has already capitalised a large share of that possibility despite limited present-day operating evidence.

Key Risks

  1. Execution risk: commercial deployment is still ahead, not behind.
  2. Licensing risk: regulatory progress matters, but nuclear pathways remain slow and uncertain.
  3. Valuation risk: the stock can compress sharply even without bad company-specific news.
  4. Financing risk: pre-profit companies remain dependent on capital markets confidence.
  5. Narrative concentration: much of the equity case depends on the AI-power theme staying hot.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: investors who want aggressive upside to the AI-power and advanced nuclear theme and can tolerate large valuation swings.

Should avoid: anyone who needs current profitability, valuation discipline or low-drama execution.

Recommendation

HOLD — SPECULATIVE.

If you want upside torque to the nuclear-plus-AI theme, Oklo is the better speculative vehicle than NuScale. But position sizing matters. This should be treated as a venture-style listed equity, not a stable compounding business. Attractive for thematic upside, but not low-risk.

Entry / exit framework:

  • $40 to $45: attractive zone for speculative adds if the AI-power thesis remains intact
