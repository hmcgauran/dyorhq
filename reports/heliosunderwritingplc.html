Executive Summary

Helios Underwriting PLC is the only publicly listed vehicle providing investors with direct, limited-liability exposure to the Lloyd's of London insurance market. The company invests in a diversified portfolio of syndicates managed by specialist managing agents, providing cross-cycle returns uncorrelated with equity markets.

The investment case rests on four pillars: structural access to one of the world's most prestigious (and hard-to-penetrate) insurance markets; a long track record of underwriting profits driven by favourable pricing conditions; a NAV that has grown consistently to £2.48 per share (Q3 2025) while the share trades at a discount to that NAV; and a management team committed to returning capital to shareholders, with 20p per share distributed in 2025.

The key near-term catalyst is the receipt of approximately £40 million in net underwriting profits from the 2023 year of account, expected in 2026. This will be the largest profit distribution Helios has ever received by a significant margin. At a price of 198p, the shares trade at roughly 0.80x last known NAV of £2.48, offering a combination of NAV upside and income return that is rare in the small-cap listed space.

Bottom line: BUY (strong). The combination of NAV discount, imminent large profit distribution, ongoing capital returns, and unique structural access to Lloyd's makes HUW a compelling investment for those seeking insurance market exposure without direct syndicate participation. The P/E of approximately 8x and price-to-NAV discount of ~20% provide a margin of safety that is not present in most Lloyd'slisted peers.

Business Model

Helios Underwriting PLC is an investment company with variable capital, incorporated and domiciled in the United Kingdom, quoted on AIM (ticker: HUW). Its core purpose is to provide investors with exposure to the Lloyd's of London insurance market through an actively managed portfolio of syndicate participations held via wholly owned subsidiary corporate members.

The model is straightforward: Helios raises capital from external investors and deploys it as capacity within Lloyd's syndicates. Managing agents underwrite insurance and reinsurance risks on behalf of syndicate members, including Helios's subsidiary vehicles. Helios earns its return from the underwriting profits generated by its syndicate portfolio, net of managing agent fees, Lloyd's levies, and Helios's own operating costs.

The company operates across approximately 40+ syndicates spanning multiple classes of business, including US and international wholesale insurance, reinsurance, property, casualty, marine, aviation, and specialty lines. The portfolio is deliberately diversified to spread risk across geographies, syndicate managers, and business classes.

Profit recognition follows a multi-year lag consistent with Lloyd's reporting conventions. Underwriting results for a given year of account (YOA) typically mature over three to five years as claims develop. This creates a pipeline of unrealised profits that Helios recognises through its fair value accounting methodology under IFRS 10. The pipeline profit from the 2023 YOA - expected to generate approximately £40 million in net underwriting profits received in 2026 - is the single most important near-term financial event.

Revenue is derived entirely from the underwriting results of its syndicate portfolio. There are no consulting fees, no advisory revenues, and no third-party asset management fees. This alignment of interest between Helios and its shareholders is a structural feature of the model.

Following a strategic review, the company exited the establishment of a new Helios follow syndicate (which had inflated 2024 operating costs) and pivoted to a leaner operating model. Operating costs relative to capacity fell 53.4% in H1 2025 versus H1 2024, and the company is focused on maintaining a sustainable cost ratio going forward.

Recent Catalysts

  • 2026 YOA Portfolio Update (20 January 2026): Helios announced managed capacity of £467 million for the 2026 year of account, down from £491 million in 2025, reflecting a deliberate improvement in portfolio quality. Freehold capacity increased 35% to £218 million. Two LLVs were acquired for £4.85 million (below Humphrey Valuation of £5.09 million), adding to portfolio depth. NAV per share as at Q3 2025 rose 3.8% to £2.48, driven by pipeline profits and Lloyd's capacity auction results.
  • H1 2025 Results (29 September 2025): NAV increased 6p to £2.39 per share in the first half. Operating cost ratio halved. Profit before tax of £4.4 million driven by reduced expenses and FX impacts. £21 million of net underwriting profits received from the 2022 YOA in May 2025, with £14.3 million returned to shareholders. Total 2025 capital return of 20p per share confirmed, up 67% from 12p in 2024.
  • Lloyd's Market Pricing Strength: Despite significant cat losses in 2024 (Hurricanes Helene and Milton, Baltimore Bridge collapse, California wildfires totalling $40 billion in insured losses), the 2024 YOA profit forecast improved to 8.0% of capacity as at June 2025, demonstrating the underlying strength of pricing adequacy and Helios's portfolio resilience.
  • CEO Appointment (H1 2025): Louis Tucker appointed as Chief Executive Officer, bringing over two decades of experience including deep expertise in third-party capital. The appointment marks a strategic shift toward digitalisation and improved portfolio management capabilities.
  • Capital Markets Day (21 October 2025): Helios hosted its first Capital Markets Day, providing existing and prospective investors with detailed portfolio visibility and strategic direction. The event signalled a new commitment to investor communication and transparency.

Key Risks

  1. Catastrophe Loss Risk (HIGH): Helios's portfolio participates heavily in US and international property and casualty markets. Major hurricane seasons, earthquakes, or pandemic-level events could significantly impact syndicate results. While Lloyd's syndicates are diversified, a year with multiple large cat events (as seen in 2024 with $20 billion+ losses from individual hurricanes) will compress profitability across the portfolio. This is the primary external risk.
  2. Lloyd's Market Pricing Cycle (MEDIUM): The current favourable pricing environment has persisted for several years. The January 2026 YOA update noted that conditions are "starting to soften" following an "exceptionally strong" period. If rates normalise faster than expected, the profitability of recent YOAs will be compressed, impacting Helios's profit pipeline and NAV.
  3. Currency Risk (MEDIUM): A significant proportion of Helios's underwriting business is denominated in US dollars, while the company reports in sterling. The H1 2025 results showed a material FX impact on debt revaluation. A sustained weakening of the US dollar against sterling would reduce the sterling value of dollar-denominated underwriting profits and increase reported leverage.
  4. Liquidity and Capital Management (MEDIUM): As a small-cap AIM-listed company with a complex ownership structure (50+ subsidiary vehicles), Helios faces inherent challenges in investor communications and liquidity. The NAV discount to peer investment companies reflects some of this structural illiquidity. Large shareholders looking to exit face market impact costs that can perpetuate the discount.
  5. Leverage and Financing (MEDIUM): Helios carries net debt of approximately £5 million and has outstanding borrowings of around £53-55 million. The financing strategy involves managing excess of loss facilities and loan notes (9.5% unsecured loan note 2030). While the company generated strong operating cash flows in H1 2025 (£20.6 million net cash from operations), any deterioration in underwriting performance could pressure the capital position.

Investors seeking direct, listed exposure to the Lloyd's of London insurance market without the complexity of direct syndicate participation. Investors who understand the multi-year lag in Lloyd's profit recognition and can hold through the cycle. Those who recognise the scarcity value of a publicly listed Lloyd's investment vehicle. Income-focused investors attracted by the 20p per share capital return (2025) against a 198p share price. Investors willing to accept a NAV discount in exchange for a tangible profit distribution catalyst (the ~£40m 2023 YOA payment expected in 2026).

Should Avoid

Investors requiring high liquidity or who may need to exit small-cap positions on short notice. Those who are uncomfortable with the complexity of Lloyd's accounting conventions, IFRS 10 investment entity treatment, and the lagged profit recognition model. Investors who need transparent, quarterly revenue visibility - Helios's model means profits arrive in lumpy, multi-year tranches that are hard to forecast precisely. Those who are unwilling to accept the premium pricing required for micro-cap listed vehicles with limited analyst coverage.

Recommendation

BUY (strong) at 198.00 GBX. Conviction: 72/100.

Helios Underwriting PLC is one of the most differentiated small-cap investment companies on the London market. Its sole focus - providing publicly listed, limited-liability exposure to the Lloyd's of London insurance market - is structurally unique and, in our view, chronically undervalued by the market.

The investment case is supported by three converging factors. First, the shares trade at approximately 0.80x the last known NAV of £2.48 per share. This is a material discount that is not easily explained by liquidity alone. Second, the 2023 year of account is expected to deliver approximately £40 million in net underwriting profits in 2026 - the largest single profit distribution in Helios's history. This is a near-term catalyst with concrete timing. Third, the 2024 year of account - despite absorbing $20 billion+ in cat losses from hurricanes Helene and Milton - is still forecast to deliver 8.0% profit on capacity, demonstrating the resilience and pricing strength of the underlying portfolio.

The P/E of approximately 8x is attractive relative to the quality and visibility of the pipeline. The 20p per share capital return in 2025 (versus 12p in 2024) demonstrates a commitment to shareholder distributions that is credibly sustainable given the cash generation profile of the business.

The primary risk is that Lloyd's pricing normalises faster than expected, compressing future profit pipelines. This is a real risk and should be monitored. However, the current portfolio is well-diversified (75% established syndicates, 10 top syndicates representing 59% of capacity) and the increased freehold capacity ratio (up 35% to £218 million) signals a deliberate reduction of reinsurance-supported and therefore riskier capacity.

We assign a conviction score of 72 based on a weighted scenario analysis. The bull case (25% weight, 280p target) is supported by the near-term 2023 YOA distribution catalyst. The base case (55% weight, 240-270p target) assumes steady market conditions and narrowing of the NAV discount. The bear case (20% weight, 140-160p) assumes accelerated market softening and expansion of the NAV discount.

On a risk-adjusted basis, the current price of 198p represents an attractive entry point. We would be buyers on any further weakness below 180p and would consider the position fairly valued in the 260-280p range.
