Executive Summary

Sivers Semiconductors AB is a small-cap Swedish semiconductor company focused on two high-growth verticals: photonics (compound semiconductor lasers, external light sources, and co-packaged optics for AI data centre interconnects) and wireless mmWave technologies (5G Open RAN, SATCOM, fixed wireless access, radar, and defence electronics). The stock has been on a extraordinary run -- approximately 630% from its 52-week low of SEK 2.85 to the current SEK 20.78, driven by accelerating AI infrastructure buildout and a series of meaningful customer Validations in photonics. The April 15 announcement of a collaboration with Jabil on a 1.6 terabit pluggable optical transceiver -- claiming 2.5x lower energy consumption than current solutions -- was the most recent catalyst. At SEK 20.78 the stock sits within 7% of its 52-week high of SEK 22.30. With TTM revenue of approximately SEK 360 million, a market capitalisation of SEK 6.16 billion implies a price-to-sales multiple of approximately 17x -- a demanding valuation for a company that remains deeply loss-making on a reported basis. The investment case is binary: either the $453 million opportunity pipeline converts and the company reaches scale and profitability, or it does not.

Business Model

Sivers designs and manufactures compound semiconductor (III-V) photonic and RF solutions. Its two divisions serve distinct but complementary end markets.

Photonics: The higher-growth, higher-visibility segment. Sivers produces external cavity lasers, optical amplifiers, and related photonic integrated circuits targeting data centre and HPC interconnects. The core technical differentiator is energy efficiency -- specifically, the ability to deliver 1.6Tbps optical transceiver solutions at materially lower power draw than silicon photonics alternatives. The April 2026 Jabil collaboration is the most significant commercial validation to date of Sivers' 1.6T pluggable module roadmap. Partnerships with O-Net Technologies and Enablence Technologies expand the external light source and co-packaged optics portfolio. The POET Technologies partnership adds light engine delivery for AI data centres.

Wireless: RFICs, beamformers, and integrated modules for mmWave 5G Open RAN, fixed wireless access, satellite communications (SATCOM), and radar. Key programmes include the Daybreak 5G/6G IC family for emerging FR3 frequency bands, a $3 million production order from Tachyon Networks for 28GHz antenna modules, and a $1.5 million strategic partnership with Doosan for Ka-band SATCOM antenna panels. ALL.SPACE reached TRL-6 with the US Army -- a meaningful technical milestone for a niche defence technology provider.

Revenue is shifting toward products (modules and systems) from components, which improves the quality of revenue but increases the challenge of scaling manufacturing. The company is 130 employees and uses a partnership-led manufacturing model rather than owning fabrication capacity -- appropriate given its scale but introduces execution dependency on partners like Jabil, O-Net, and POET.

Recent Catalysts

Jabil 1.6T Optical Transceiver Collaboration (April 15, 2026): The most recent and highest-profile validation of Sivers' photonics roadmap. Jabil -- a major electronics manufacturing services provider -- is collaborating with Sivers on a 1.6T pluggable optical transceiver module claiming 2.5x lower energy consumption than current solutions. This is significant because power consumption is the binding constraint in AI data centre optical interconnects. The 1.6T speed grade is the next industry standard after 800G, targeting hyperscaler AI clusters. A manufacturing partner of Jabil's scale substantially de-risks the path from design to volume production.

LiDAR Production Ramp (Q4 2026): A strategic customer is ramping production using Sivers' lasers and optical amplifiers, with potential cumulative revenues of USD 28-53 million over the 2026-2030 period. This is the most concrete long-term revenue visibility Sivers has disclosed and suggests a durable, recurring customer relationship rather than a one-off order.

ALL.SPACE TRL-6 with US Army: Technical milestone achieved for Sivers' advanced non-terrestrial network technology. TRL-6 (System or subsystem model or prototype demonstration in a relevant environment) is a meaningful step toward field deployment and signals credible defence sector progress.

Tachyon Networks ($3m PO) and Doosan ($1.5m Partnership): Production order and strategic development contract respectively in the wireless segment, validating the fixed wireless access and SATCOM product lines.

$800K US Defence Contract: A strategic development award from a leading US defence contractor -- modest in value but validates the quality of Sivers' wireless and mmWave technology for defence applications.

$453 Million Opportunity Pipeline: The most important leading indicator. The pipeline grew 64% in 2025 to $453 million, encompassing both photonics and wireless opportunities. The conversion of this pipeline over the next 12-24 months will determine whether the investment thesis is realised or not. Key caveat: pipeline figures are not firm orders and include probabilistic opportunities at various stages of maturity.

Nasdaq Dual Listing Evaluation (April 2026): Sivers announced it is evaluating a potential dual listing on Nasdaq New York alongside its existing Nasdaq Stockholm primary listing, retaining Swedish domicile. No final decision made. If executed, this would improve access to US institutional capital and increase visibility among US growth-focused investors -- potentially supportive of valuation. Timeline uncertain if pursued; 6-18 months is typical for cross-border listings.

SEK 125 Million Private Placement (May 12 EGM): Concurrent with the dual listing announcement, Sivers launched a private placement of approximately SEK 125 million, subject to extraordinary general meeting approval on May 12, 2026. At current prices, this represents roughly 6% dilution to existing shareholders. Proceeds would fund operations and growth initiatives. The EGM vote is a concrete near-term catalyst to monitor.

Key Risks

  1. #1 SEK 125m Private Placement Dilution: The EGM on May 12, 2026 will vote on a proposed private placement of SEK 125 million. At current prices this adds approximately 6 million new shares -- roughly 6% dilution for existing holders. This is a direct and concrete near-term risk that the original report did not reflect.
  2. #2 Valuation disconnect from fundamentals: At SEK 20.78 the stock prices in substantial pipeline conversion and a successful transition to profitability. The price-to-sales multiple of approximately 17x on TTM revenue is extremely demanding for a company burning SEK 140+ million annually at the operating level. Any setback to pipeline conversion or customer momentum could trigger a sharp repricing.
  3. #3 Cash burn and capital structure: Annual operating cash burn of SEK 55-70 million against a cash position of SEK 43.5 million implies limited financial runway without new financing. The SEK 125m private placement, if approved on May 12, would extend runway by approximately 18-24 months at current burn rates -- a partial mitigation but not a solution. The company needs AEBITDA to turn sustainably positive or pipeline contracts to generate advance payments to avoid further dilution.
  4. #4 Execution in photonics against established players: Sivers is a 130-person company competing against Broadcom, Coherent, Lumentum, and Intel PFN in optical interconnects. The Jabil collaboration is an important de-risking step, but moving from prototype to high-volume production with a contract manufacturer is a fundamentally different challenge from developing a photonic chip. Execution failure at this stage would be difficult to recover from at the current valuation.
  5. #5 Pipeline conversion uncertainty: $453 million is a leading indicator, not a firm backlog. The weighted average probability of conversion across the pipeline is unknown and likely low. A pipeline of this size relative to current revenue (approximately 14x TTM revenue) implies the market is pricing in a very aggressive conversion rate.
  6. #6 Swedish krona exposure: Sivers reports in SEK while much of its revenue may be denominated in USD or EUR. SEK weakness suppresses reported revenue and margins when translated back. Given the current account is deeply loss-making, FX movements can amplify the loss or reduce the implied USD valuation for international investors.
  7. #7 Extreme price volatility: The stock has moved from SEK 2.85 to SEK 20.78 in 12 months -- a 630% move on fundamentally the same business. Small-cap, low-volume, Nasdaq Stockholm listing creates a structurally volatile equity that can move 20-30% in either direction on news flow.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Aggressive growth investors with a specific thesis on AI data centre optical infrastructure and a high risk tolerance. The photonics story has genuine substance -- the Jabil collaboration, POET/O-Net/Enablence partnerships, and the LiDAR production ramp are real proof points, not slideware. Position sizing of 1-3% maximum in a diversified portfolio with a wide trailing stop is essential.

Avoiders: Anyone requiring profitability, cash-flow visibility, or a rational fundamental support point for a valuation. The stock at SEK 20.78 is pricing in a version of the bull case that is already well advanced after a 630% run. Value-oriented investors, income seekers, and anyone who cannot absorb a 50-70% drawdown from current levels should not own this name.

Recommendation

HOLD -- 38/100. Sivers Semiconductors has transformed from a niche RF components supplier into a credible participant in two of the most compelling semiconductor end markets: AI photonics and advanced wireless. The April 2026 Jabil collaboration on 1.6T optical transceivers is the most significant commercial validation the company has achieved, and the $453 million opportunity pipeline provides meaningful visibility into future revenue potential. However, the stock has run 630% from its 52-week low in less than 12 months, and the current valuation of approximately 17x price-to-sales embeds assumptions about pipeline conversion and profitability that leave very little margin of safety at current levels.

The near-term catalyst is Q1 2026 earnings (expected around May 20, 2026) and any follow-through on the Jabil collaboration. Investors who held from the SEK 5-8 range have a substantial gain and can manage a trailing stop. New entrants at current levels face poor risk-reward: the upside to the bull case is meaningful but the downside from here is severe if pipeline conversion disappoints or the company needs to raise capital at distressed terms.

Entry framework: Not a BUY for new investors at current levels. Existing holders with wide cost basis may hold with a strict trailing stop at -30% from entry. The next meaningful entry point, if the thesis is compelling, would be on any significant pullback toward SEK 12-14 (approximately 40% below current levels) that offers some margin of safety relative to the pipeline and business quality. Reduce or exit on rallies above SEK 25-27 if the stock begins pricing in full bull-case execution before it is demonstrated.
