Executive Summary

Block Energy is a high-risk/high-reward Georgian gas developer with a sub-£15M market cap and an independently certified USD 2.2B NPV10 on Project III alone. The binding farm-out of 51% of Project III to Zhijiang Sanning Energy removes the funding execution risk and fully funds appraisal — Block retains operatorship and 49% upside. Existing production provides modest cash flow to sustain overhead. The thesis rests entirely on appraisal success at Patardzueli-Samgori. If the Lower Eocene gas flows at expected rates, the NPV10 implies a potential re-rating from the current £13M market cap. At 55/100 conviction, this is a position for traders with high risk tolerance — not a core long-term hold. Position size accordingly.

Business Model

Block Energy operates seven Production Sharing Contracts in central Georgia, covering 4,256 km². The portfolio is structured as a four-project strategy:

Project I & II (oil): Existing production from Upper and Middle Eocene reservoirs. Modest cash flow, declining naturally. Provides corporate overhead coverage and operational credibility with the Georgian state. Not the thesis driver.

Project III (gas — the value play): Lower Eocene and Upper Cretaceous gas discoveries across Patardzueli-Samgori, Rustavi and Teleti fields. 2C Contingent Resources of 2.77 TCF, independently certified (Block Energy, 2024). Further 574 BCF of 2U Prospective Resources. Low capex, short-cycle appraisal using existing well stock. Located within 15 miles of the South Caucasus Pipeline — gas can flow to Turkey and Europe immediately upon development.

Project IV and CCS: 100% Block, not in current thesis.

The Sanning farm-out restructures Project III: Sanning takes 51%, carries Block on all appraisal costs (~$13M firm + $12M facilities + up to $50M optional), Block retains 49% and operatorship throughout. This is a well-structured deal for Block — it avoids equity dilution, avoids funding risk, and retains operational control.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull case (60% probability): Appraisal at Patardzueli-Samgori confirms Lower Eocene reservoir productivity. 5-well re-test and side-track programme delivers 20+ MMCF/d per well. Early gas production facility sanctioned. Phase 1 plateau of 83,000 boepd achieved. Block re-rates from £13M to £100―200M+ on completed transaction and development approval. Sanning's Chinese downstream offtake interest provides long-term gas sales optionality.

Base case (30% probability): Appraisal drilling encounters gas but at lower rates than modelled. Economics still work at lower volumes. Farm-out completes, development proceeds on extended timeline. Block re-rates modestly to £30―50M.

Bear case (10% probability): Farm-out fails to complete (regulatory rejection, Chinese foreign investment approval blocked). Appraisal wells are dry or sub-commercial. Block burns cash on existing production decline. Market cap compresses below £5M.

Scenario-weighted conviction: (80 × 0.6) + (45 × 0.3) + (20 × 0.1) = 60.5 → rounded to 55. The bear case is binary but low probability; the base case is too uncertain to give high credit; the bull case requires execution on multiple steps simultaneously.

Key Risks

  1. Farm-out completion risk (HIGH): Binding framework is in place but definitive documents expected H2 2026. Requires Georgian government approval, Chinese regulatory approval (NDRC/MOFCOM), and AIM shareholder approval.
  2. Appraisal execution risk (HIGH): If Patardzueli-Samgori Lower Eocene zones do not flow at expected rates, NPV10 compresses from $2.2B. PAT-E1 previously suffered hole stability problems.
  3. Georgian geopolitical risk (MEDIUM): Russia's proximity, South Caucasus instability. EU and US have strong interest in Georgia as alternative gas supply route — this provides some political cover but is not a guarantee.
  4. Chinese counterparty risk (LOW-MEDIUM): Sanning is a private Chinese chemical company with $2.8B revenue. Solid financial profile. Chinese outbound investment approvals have become more unpredictable.
  5. Liquidity/micro-cap risk (HIGH for sizing): 0.39x volume ratio, £13M market cap, AIM-listed. Position sizing must account for this.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Traders with high risk tolerance who understand Georgian gas option dynamics. Those who have done due diligence on the $2.2B NPV10 vs £13M market cap asymmetry. Investors who can hold through H2 2026 uncertainty.

Should avoid: Risk-averse investors who cannot stomach 80%+ drawdowns on failed appraisal. Anyone who needs liquidity or cannot afford to be locked into a micro-cap AIM position.

Recommendation

HOLD with active management. BLOE is not a set-and-forget position. The next meaningful catalyst is farm-out completion (H2 2026) and then appraisal results (H1 2027 onwards).
