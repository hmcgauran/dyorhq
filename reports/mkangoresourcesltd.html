Executive Summary

Mkango is a Canadian-listed rare earths and recycling company with two core assets: the Songwe Hill rare earths project in Malawi and the Akokoma magnet recycling joint venture in Poland. The investment thesis rests on the secular demand growth for permanent magnets used in EV motors and wind turbines, combined with Mkango's position as one of the few potential near-term producers of separated magnet rare earth oxides outside China. At 35.0p, the stock has recovered from lows but is priced as a development story pending project advancement milestones.

The bull case is that Songwe moves toward development, the magnet recycling JV scales meaningfully, and Western demand for non-China rare earth supply chains creates a premium for Mkango's assets. The bear case is that project development requires more capital than the equity can access, dilution is severe, and the rare earths bear market of 2023-2024 structurally impairs the development economics.

Bottom line: HOLD - SPECULATIVE. The rare earths secular theme is real and the assets are genuine. But project financing risk, commodity price sensitivity, and dilution risk make this appropriate only for investors who understand development-stage mining equity sizing.

Business Model

Mkango operates across two platforms: (1) the Songwe Hill rare earths project in Malawi - a carbonatite deposit with high-grade rare earth mineralisation amenable to open-pit mining, currently in Bankable Feasibility Study stage; and (2) the Akokoma joint venture in Poland, focused on hydrometallurgical processing ofNdFeB (neodymium-iron-boron) magnet scrap into separated rare earth oxides for the European EV and wind turbine supply chains.

The strategic logic is compelling: Songwe provides the rare earth feedstock, Akokoma provides the downstream processing capability, and together they create a non-China rare earths supply chain for European strategic materials buyers. EU critical raw materials policy and UK critical minerals strategies are explicitly designed to support exactly this type of asset. The question is whether Mkango can access the capital to develop them on schedule.

Recent Catalysts

  • Akokoma magnet recycling JV: The Poland-based rare earth magnet recycling joint venture is the more near-term commercial opportunity. Akokoma processes end-of-life magnets and magnet production scrap into separated NdPr oxide - the key inputs for EV motor magnets. The EU's critical raw materials act creates demand for exactly this type of European processing capacity.
  • Bankable Feasibility Study for Songwe: The BFS is the key technical de-risking document that would support project financing. Completion and publication of a positive BFS is a major catalyst.
  • Rare earths market recovery: The NdPr oxide price cycle that crashed in 2022-2023 has partially recovered. Sustained higher prices make Songwe's development economics more attractive and reduce financing risk.
  • Strategic customer and offtake agreements: Any announcement of a binding offtake or strategic investment from a European automaker, wind turbine OEM, or specialty materials group would be a major re-rating event.
  • UK and EU critical materials policy: Both the UK's Critical Minerals Strategy and the EU's Critical Raw Materials Act create policy tailwinds for non-China rare earth supply chains. Mkango's geographic positioning (Malawi mine, Polish recycling) fits both frameworks.

Key Risks

  1. 1Project financing risk: The central risk is whether Mkango can access the capital required to develop Songwe at attractive terms. A large equity raise at a discount would be significantly dilutive. Watch the BFS results carefully for capital cost estimates.
  2. 2Rare earths commodity price sensitivity: NdPr oxide prices collapsed from $150/kg to $50/kg between 2022 and 2023, recovering partially since. Songwe's NPV is highly sensitive to long-term rare earths pricing assumptions.
  3. 3Dilution risk: Development-stage mining companies routinely require multiple equity raises. Each raise at a discount to the current price is directly dilutive to existing shareholders.
  4. 4Malawi country risk: Songwe is located in Malawi, which carries standard sovereign risk for a frontier market mining jurisdiction. Regulatory changes, tax disputes, or political instability are all possible.
