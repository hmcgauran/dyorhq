Executive Summary

Mkango Resources is a Canadian-listed rare earth elements explorer with a primary asset in Malawi, a UK-based processing technology subsidiary (Maginito), and a new acquisition in Germany through Hypromag. The rare earth secular demand story — driven by EVs, wind turbines, and defence — is genuine. The company has just closed a oversubscribed £12.5m fundraise to acquire and commission Hypromag Germany, a strategic expansion into rare earth magnet recycling that transforms the business from a pre-revenue explorer into an operating clean-tech materials company.

The share price has fallen from ~70p to ~42p following the equity raise — typical post-dilution weakness — but the oversubscription and the strategic rationale for Hypromag Germany are significant positives. The prior report (conviction 45) needs updating to reflect the operational expansion and current price context.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY. The Hypromag Germany acquisition is strategically sound — rare earth magnet recycling is a high-growth niche as EV and wind turbine end-of-life volumes begin to scale. Current price weakness is dilution-related, not thesis-breaking. BUY below 35p on post-raise weakness.

Business Model

Mkango operates across three pillars: (1) Songwe Hill rare earth project in Malawi (exploration/development stage); (2) Maginito UK — rare earth processing and refining technology, targeting magnet recycling; (3) Hypromag Germany — acquired via the £12.5m raise, targeting rare earth magnet recycling operations.

The strategic logic of Hypromag Germany is compelling: as EV and wind turbine installations from the 2015-2025 boom cycle reach end of life in the 2030s, magnet recycling will become a critical feedstock for Western-world REE supply chains. Hypromag Germany is positioned to capture this before Chinese players dominate the market.

First commissioning runs are underway at Hypromag Germany. A new UK collaboration has also been announced between Maginito and Mkango Rare Earths UK.

Key Risks

  1. Post-raise dilution (HIGH): The £12.5m raise has increased the share count meaningfully. The stock at 42p needs Hypromag Germany to generate meaningful revenue to justify the dilution.
  2. Execution risk at Hypromag Germany (MEDIUM): First commissioning runs are underway but commercial-scale operations are not yet proven. The recycling market is nascent — ramp-up could be slower than expected.
  3. REE price risk (MEDIUM): Neodymium-praseodymium prices are volatile and China-dominated. A sustained price decline would impair the economics of both Songwe Hill and recycling operations.
  4. Further capital raises (HIGH): The company is pre-revenue. Another equity raise in 12-18 months is likely if Songwe Hill advances to construction.
  5. Malawi political risk (LOW-MEDIUM): Government relations and the Mining Development Agreement remain key operational variables for Songwe Hill.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors who want early-stage exposure to the rare earth magnet recycling theme. Hypromag Germany is a genuine strategic move into a growing niche. The dilution-related price weakness creates a better entry than the 70p highs.

Should avoid: Investors who need revenue-generating assets or who cannot tolerate repeated equity dilution. This remains a pre-revenue story with meaningful capital requirements ahead.
