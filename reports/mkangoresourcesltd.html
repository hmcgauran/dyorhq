Executive Summary

Mkango Resources is a Canadian-listed rare earth elements (REE) explorer with a primary asset in Malawi and a UK-based processing technology subsidiary. The rare earth secular demand story -- driven by EVs, wind turbines, and defence applications -- is genuine, but Mkango remains pre-revenue, loss-making, and dependent on capital markets to fund development. At CAD 0.78, the stock sits well above its 52-week low but has not yet re-rated to levels that fully reflect development risk. HOLD. Conviction Score: 45/100 - HOLD.

Business Model

Mkango Resources Ltd is a mineral exploration and development company focused on rare earth elements in Malawi and the UK. The company's flagship asset is the Songwe Hill rare earth project in Malawi, a carbonate-hosted deposit containing neodymium, praseodymium, dysprosium, and terbium -- the critical magnet REEs that underpin the energy transition and electric vehicle supply chain. Mkango also operates through Maginito, its 90%-owned UK subsidiary, which is developing rare earth processing and refining technology targeting the magnet recycling and processing segment. The company generates no current revenue and is financed through equity raises and government agreements. Its strategy involves advancing Songwe Hill through feasibility and toward production, while developing the Maginito processing platform as a potential lower-capex entry point into the REE value chain.

Key Risks

  1. #1 Pre-revenue and cash-burn risk: Mkango has no operating income and is entirely dependent on equity capital markets to fund development. Any market dislocation or share price weakness could force a highly dilutive equity raise.
  2. #2 Commodity price risk: REE prices, particularly neodymium-praseodymium oxide, are volatile and heavily influenced by Chinese supply-demand dynamics. A sustained price depression would impair project economics at Songwe Hill.
  3. #3 Exploration and development risk: Songwe Hill has not yet reached bankable feasibility or production. Geological uncertainty, cost overruns, and processing recovery challenges are live risks through the development lifecycle.
  4. #4 Malawi sovereign and regulatory risk: The Mining Development Agreement has been subject to prolonged negotiations. Changes to the fiscal regime, royalty rates, or government equity requirements represent material project risk.
  5. #5 Execution and offtake risk: Without a committed offtake agreement, project financing will be extremely difficult to arrange. The strategic importance of magnet REEs creates a theoretical offtake market, but commercial terms remain to be proven.
