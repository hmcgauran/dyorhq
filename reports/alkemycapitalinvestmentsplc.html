Key Risks

  1. #1 Illiquidity and share dilution risk: As an investment company, Alkemy may issue new shares to fund portfolio companies, causing dilution to existing holders. Thin daily volume amplifies price volatility on any news flow.
  2. #2 No disclosed portfolio transparency: Without detailed, independently verified portfolio disclosures, investors cannot accurately assess NAV or the true risk profile. This opacity is a structural discount factor.
  3. #3 Interest rate and risk appetite sensitivity: Early-stage and growth-sector investments are disproportionately harmed by higher discount rates. A sustained higher-rate environment could permanently impair portfolio company valuations.
  4. #4 Concentration and exit risk: Small investment companies frequently have concentrated portfolios. A failed or impaired investee company can materially damage overall performance, with limited diversification benefit.
  5. #5 Governance and management alignment: Without evidence of co-investment by management alongside shareholders, incentive alignment is imperfect. A small-cap investment company with limited analyst coverage also faces heightened agency risk.
