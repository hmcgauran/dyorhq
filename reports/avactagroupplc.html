Executive Summary

Avacta Group is a UK-listed biotech company developing a pipeline of cancer therapeutics based on its proprietary Affimer scaffold platform and the pre|CISION tumour-activated chemotherapy technology. The investment case rests on two pillars: the scientific validation of the FAP-targeting pre|CISION mechanism, which is supported by peer-reviewed literature and is genuinely differentiated from conventional chemotherapy, and the near-term catalysts from the ongoing Phase 1 trial of faridaksorubicin (FA) and the upcoming AACR 2026 conference presentations (17-22 April 2026). The shares trade at a significant discount to the implicit value of a successful partnership or NASDAQ listing, both of which management is actively exploring. With the AACR conference underway and Phase 1 data expected in H1 2026, the risk-reward is compelling at current levels. BUY (STRONG).

Business Model

Avacta operates a pure-play biotech model: develop proprietary therapeutic candidates through early clinical validation, then out-license or partner with larger pharmaceutical companies for late-stage development and commercialisation. The company retains rights for the US market in certain structures, providing optionality for a NASDAQ listing or direct US partnership.

The Affimer platform is Avacta's core technology engine. Affimers are small (~12kDa), cysteine-free scaffold proteins engineered to bind selectively to specific disease targets. Compared to monoclonal antibodies, Affimers are smaller, cheaper to manufacture, and offer potential advantages in tumour penetration and immunogenicity. The platform has been validated in collaborations with major pharmaceutical companies including AstraZeneca and others.

The pre|CISION technology is the proprietary tumour-targeting layer. The platform uses a peptide linker that is selectively cleaved by fibroblast activation protein (FAP), an enzyme highly expressed in the tumour stroma of over 90% of epithelial cancers but largely absent from normal tissue. By linking doxorubicin to this FAP-cleavable linker, pre|CISION prodrugs release the active drug preferentially within the tumour microenvironment, dramatically reducing systemic toxicity. This is the scientific foundation for both the faridaksorubicin (FA) and AVA6000 (Doxorubicin prodrug) programmes.

Revenue model: No current revenue. The value creation path is through out-licensing deals (upfront + milestone + royalty) or a corporate transaction (partnership, acquisition, NASDAQ listing). The £9.45M oversubscribed fundraising completed in early 2026 provides cash runway through key catalysts without excessive dilution.
