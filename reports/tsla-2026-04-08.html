Executive Summary

Tesla is the world's largest electric vehicle company by market capitalisation, also operating an energy storage business (Megapack), a solar business, and an autonomous driving / robotics programme (Optimus). The company sells cars at multiple price points globally, competes directly with BYD, and is facing meaningful competitive pressure in China and Europe while maintaining a dominant position in the US premium EV market.

At $348.09, the stock has de-rated from its 2024 highs but remains expensive on any conventional metric. The P/E of 343x is not meaningful — the company is priced for an autonomous taxi future and sustained 30%+ revenue growth that requires flawless execution.

Bottom line: REDUCE. Tesla is a high-quality business in a competitive market. The autonomous driving / robotics premium is not warranted by current evidence. Conviction: 48/100.

Business Model

Tesla manufactures and sells electric vehicles (Model 3, Y, S, X, Cybertruck) and energy products (Megapack battery storage, Solar). Revenue is approximately 85% automotive, 10% energy storage, 5% services and other. The automotive business benefits from direct sales, proprietary charging network, and vertically integrated manufacturing.

Full Self-Driving (FSD) is a software product sold to consumers that provides driver-assist capabilities. The robotaxi ambition — autonomous ride-hailing without a driver — is the most speculative part of the bull case and requires regulatory approval that has not yet materialised.
