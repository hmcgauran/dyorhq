Executive Summary

Deere & Co sits at the intersection of agricultural structural demand and precision farming technology adoption, offering a compelling combination of earnings durability and long-term growth optionality at a reasonable valuation, and is rated BUY.

Business Model

Deere is the world's largest manufacturer of agricultural machinery and a major player in construction equipment. Its business model spans the full equipment lifecycle -- manufacture, financing, and after-market parts and services. Key segments:

Production & Precision Agriculture~55% of segment revenue; large & small farm equipment, precision ag tech
Small Agriculture & Turf~15% of segment revenue; compact tractors, consumer & commercial turf equipment
Construction & Forestry~25% of segment revenue; excavators, loaders, crawlers
Financial Services~5% of revenue; equipment financing, captive finance arm

Deere's services and parts revenue provides recurring, high-margin earnings (~35% of total profits). The precision agriculture platform -- including GPS-guided autosteer, variable-rate seeding, and See & Spray A.I. technology -- creates a recurring software and subscription revenue opportunity that structurally differentiates Deere from pure equipment manufacturers.

Recent Catalysts (Last 3-6 Months)

See & Spray Ultimate Expansion: Deere's AI-powered See & Spray herbicide reduction technology reached commercial scale in FY2025 and is being rolled out across additional tractor and sprayer lines in 2026, with strong farmer adoption driven by input cost savings.

Agricultural Income Resilience: Despite grain price volatility, US farm income in 2025 remained near record levels, supporting equipment replacement demand and dealer order books extending into FY2026.

Infrastructure Spending: The US Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act continues to drive demand for Deere's construction and forestry equipment, providing diversification and counterbalancing any softness in agricultural equipment demand.

Labour Cost Inflation: Persistent farm labour shortages are accelerating automation and robotics investments, including Deere's autonomous tractor programmes, which have entered pilot programmes in the US Midwest.
