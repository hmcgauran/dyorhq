Executive Summary

The Cigna Group's combination of Evernorth health services and disciplined Medicare Advantage growth supports a mid-60s conviction score, but medical cost uncertainty and integration execution risk keep it in hold territory.

Business Model

The Cigna Group operates two divisions: (1) Evernorth Health Services (pharmacy benefit management through Express Scripts; health benefits administration; behavioural health; diagnostic and wellness services) serving ~115 million people; and (2) Cigna Healthcare (medical, dental, and behavioural health insurance for employers and individuals, covering ~17 million medical members in the US). The Evernorth PBM is the third-largest in the US and is strategically important because it steers patients toward Cigna-owned pharmacy networks, generating rebate income and data advantages. The company's strategy emphasises stop-loss and large-group coverage rather than individual ACA exchanges, providing a more predictable medical loss ratio.

Recent Catalysts

  • Evernorth PBM renegotiated rebate contracts with three major pharma manufacturers in H2 2025, improving per-prescription economics.
  • Medicare Advantage membership grew 9% YoY in 2025 open enrollment, ahead of industry average, supporting premium revenue growth.
  • Separation from the CHS (Cigna Healthcare) real estate joint venture completed Q1 2026, unlocking ~$2.1bn in capital.
  • Initiative to integrate behavioural health services into primary care through Evernorth MD platform, improving chronic condition management.
  • Share repurchase programme of $3.2bn authorised in February 2026, representing ~4.5% of market capitalisation.

Thesis Evaluation

Cigna is a structurally well-positioned health services and insurance hybrid. The Evernorth PBM provides recurring revenue with significant moat (switching costs for employer clients are high), while the insurance book generates premium income with disciplined MLR management. The valuation (P/E 12.2x) appears inexpensive relative to peers, but this partly reflects MLR uncertainty in Medicare Advantage and concern about medical cost trend normalisation post-COVID. The pending Humira biosimilar transition and potential Star rating headwinds in 2026 are the key near-term variables.
