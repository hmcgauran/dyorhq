Executive Summary

Oklo is one of the cleanest listed ways to express the view that AI-era electricity demand will force the market to take advanced nuclear seriously. The company’s appeal is not just “nuclear”, it is power for compute. That positioning matters because it links Oklo directly to one of the strongest structural demand stories in the market. Business Model

Oklo is developing advanced microreactors designed to supply reliable, low-carbon power where uptime and energy density matter. The commercial target set is attractive: data centres, industrial sites, remote applications and other environments where firm power can command premium economics. The company’s strength is narrative alignment. If hyperscalers, data-centre developers or industrial power buyers start securing future dedicated nuclear capacity, Oklo is positioned to benefit. The weakness is that this still needs to move from strategic interest to deployable, financeable commercial reality.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull (30%) Oklo secures stronger counterparties, continues to clear milestones and becomes one of the market’s preferred listed beneficiaries of AI-linked power demand. Base (45%) The story remains attractive, but progress is gradual and the stock continues to trade on thematic sentiment more than on current economics. Bear (25%) Commercial adoption takes longer than hoped, regulatory timelines stretch and the market cuts the premium attached to the AI-power nuclear theme. Oklo does not need to be profitable tomorrow to work as a stock, but it does need to keep investors convinced that it can become strategically important before capital patience fades.
