Executive Summary

Kraken Robotics filed record annual results on 16 April 2026: CAD 102.2 million revenue and CAD 25.0 million adjusted EBITDA, both records for the company. The defence and commercial subsea technology business is firing on all cylinders, with gross margins expanding to 62.1 per cent and a $615 million transformational acquisition of UK-headquartered Covelya Group in the pipeline. A CAD 402.5 million equity raise funds most of the deal. Management guided to CAD 165-175 million revenue and CAD 40-50 million adjusted EBITDA for 2026, representing over 60 per cent top-line growth. The stock trades at a punishing P/E of 141 times reported EPS, but the true earnings power sits in adjusted EBITDA, which was CAD 25 million in 2025 on a base of CAD 102 million revenue. The stock has recovered strongly from its 52-week low of CAD 2.15 but remains below the 52-week high of CAD 10.72. Covelya closing in Q2 2026 is the next binary catalyst.

Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY at CAD 8.68. The fundamentals are exceptional, the growth pipeline is deep, and the Covelya acquisition transforms the addressable market. The valuation is demanding but the execution track record justifies a position for investors with a 12-18 month horizon.

Business Model

Kraken operates two divisions — products and services — serving both defence and commercial subsea customers globally. Products include SeaPower subsea batteries (the fast-growing segment), KATFISH towed synthetic aperture sonar (SAS) systems, and seabed imaging sensors. Services provides subsea survey, inspection, and LiDAR services primarily to offshore energy operators. The Covelya acquisition brings six subsidiary businesses including Sonardyne International (navigation and positioning), EIVA (subsea survey software), and Wavefront Systems (counter-drone sonar), materially expanding Kraken's addressable market.

The company benefits from the global push toward unmanned underwater vehicles (UUVs) for both military and commercial applications. Kraken's batteries are a key bottleneck product — energy density and reliability in deep-sea conditions are critical, and Kraken has built a meaningful technical lead. The 60,000 square foot Nova Scotia facility expansion completed in 2025 addresses the manufacturing constraint that limited growth in prior years.

Key Risks

  1. Acquisition integration risk: Six acquired businesses with different cultures, systems, and customer bases. Coordination cost and customer disruption are real risks in the first 12 months post-close.
  2. Dilution from equity raise: CAD 402.5M raise at CAD 8.50 was dilutive. Shares outstanding increased materially. EPS recovery depends on acquisition delivering synergies.
  3. P/E is meaningless: Reported EPS is CAD 0.01 due to acquisition costs, restructuring, and deferred tax accounting. Investors focused on P/E will miss the real story in EBITDA.
  4. Defence budget dependency: Significant portion of revenue comes from NATO and allied defence customers. A shift in defence spending priorities or a peace dividend scenario would reduce demand.
  5. Small-cap liquidity: TSX-V has limited analyst coverage and institutional investor participation. Large positions may face exit challenges.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors with a 12-24 month horizon who understand defence technology investing and can tolerate binary event risk. The subsea unmanned systems theme is durable and Kraken is one of the few listed plays on it.

Should avoid: Investors who require earnings visibility, those uncomfortable with elevated valuation metrics, or those needing a liquid mid-cap name for large position sizing.
