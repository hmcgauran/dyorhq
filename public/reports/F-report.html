Executive Summary

Business Model

Ford operates across three primary vehicle segments following its organisational restructuring: Ford Blue (ICE): Traditional internal combustion engine vehicles — F-Series trucks, Mustang, Explorer, Bronco — the cash-generating backbone of the company. Ford Blue generates the profits and free cash flow that funds the EV transition. The segment is in structural long-term decline as consumer preferences shift. Ford Model e (EVs): Electric vehicles including the F-150 Lightning (electric truck), Mustang Mach-E, and E-Transit van. This segment is currently loss-making and is expected to remain so until EV scale reaches the level required to achieve manufacturing cost parity with ICE vehicles. Ford Pro (Commercial): A dedicated commercial vehicle and fleet services business selling ICE and EV commercial vehicles globally, alongside telematics and service contracts. This is a high-margin, sticky business that benefits from the transition to electric commercial fleets. Ford Credit: The captive finance arm provides auto financing and leasing to Ford customers and dealers. It provides a steady earnings contribution and helps support vehicle sales through promotional leasing programmes. Ford's core strategic challenge is managing the simultaneous decline of its ICE cash cow and the losses of its EV growth engine — a transition that has proven far more difficult and expensive than originally modelled.
