Executive Summary

Anglo Asian Mining is a UK-listed gold and copper producer operating exclusively in Azerbaijan under Mining Law agreements with the Azerbaijani government. The company runs the Gedabek gold-copper mine and is developing the Gyzylarahmet gold deposit. At 258.5 GBX, the stock trades at approximately 6x EPS against a gold price environment that remains constructive. The geographic concentration in Azerbaijan is the central risk variable, but the current valuation -- a deep value multiple for a precious metals producer -- more than compensates for geopolitical risk at this stage of the cycle.

The case for BUY is anchored in three pillars: a sub-6x earnings multiple that prices in near-zero commodity upside, a dividend yield that is materialising as production costs stabilise, and optionality around the Gyzylarahmet development pipeline. The stock is not without friction, but the risk/reward at current levels is among the more attractive in the UK small-cap precious metals space.

Bottom line: BUY (strong). The valuation is the argument. At 6x EPS on estimated 43p, with gold holding above $2,800/oz, the stock has further to run. Azerbaijan country risk is real but well-flagged and reflected. Accumulate on any pullback to 220-230 GBX.

Business Model

Anglo Asian Mining operates under Production Sharing Agreements (PSAs) with the Azerbaijan government, a contractual framework that gives the company the right to produce and sell gold, copper, and silver in exchange for sharing output with the state. This model provides long-term tenure over mineral rights but also means the Azerbaijani government has a direct economic interest in operational performance -- a double-edged dynamic that has generally been constructive for the company.

The Gedabek mine is the core asset: a bulk open-pit and underground operation producing gold as the primary product alongside copper concentrate and silver. The Gyzylarahmet deposit, at an earlier stage of development, represents the primary growth pipeline and has the potential to extend mine life materially beyond current plans.

Revenue is split between gold (approximately 70-75%), copper (15-20%), and silver (5-10%), with sales into the spot market and via offtake agreements. Azerbaijan and Turkey are the primary market outlets, with some third-party commodity trader offtake for concentrate.

Recent Catalysts

  • Gold price strength: Gold has traded above $2,800/oz through Q1 2026, a multi-year high environment that directly benefits every ounce Anglo Asian produces. The correlation between gold price and AAZ share price has been imperfect -- the stock has not kept pace with spot gold, suggesting the market is applying a persistent Azerbaijan discount that is not fully rational at current levels.
  • Gyzylarahmet development progress: Ongoing drilling and development work at Gyzylarahmet has continued to define the resource. Any formal JORC resource upgrade would be a significant catalyst for re-rating a stock that the market treats as a single-asset story.
  • Copper by-product contribution: Copper prices have held at elevated levels relative to historical averages. As a meaningful revenue line at Gedabek, the copper by-product effectively reduces the all-in cost of gold production -- a feature that is underappreciated in the current multiple.
  • UK small-cap re-rating trend: The FTSE 250 and small-cap mining space has seen improving sentiment through early 2026 as gilt yields stabilise and the premium attached to illiquid small-cap exposure normalises. AAZ has not participated fully in this recovery, leaving room for catch-up.

Key Risks

  1. Azerbaijan geopolitical risk (HIGH): This is the primary risk variable. Anglo Asian's entire asset base is located in Azerbaijan, and the country sits in a geopolitically complex neighbourhood -- adjacent to Armenia, Iran, and Russia. Any material deterioration in Azerbaijan's political stability, resource governance, or relationship with Western partners would directly threaten the company's operating environment. The market is aware of this risk but has historically struggled to price it accurately.
  2. Commodity price exposure (MEDIUM): Gold and copper prices are the single largest driver of earnings. A sustained fall in gold below $2,500/oz would materially compress margins and free cash flow. The lack of a hedging programme means the company is fully exposed to spot price movements.
  3. Single-asset concentration (MEDIUM): Gedabek is effectively the entire production story. Any operational disruption -- geological, mechanical, or regulatory -- flows directly to the bottom line. The Gyzylarahmet pipeline is the diversification answer but is at an earlier stage.
  4. Liquidity and re-rating constraints (MEDIUM): As a sub-GBP100m market cap stock on the LSE Main, daily trading liquidity is limited. This constrains institutional ownership and keeps the re-rating path slow. The spread between bid and offer can be wide.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors who want direct exposure to the gold price with a genuine value tilt -- this is not a growth story but a cash-generative asset at an unusually low multiple. Those who understand the Azerbaijan risk and are comfortable with it as a known variable rather than a hidden blow-up. Gold bugs who want UK-listed exposure without the currency complexity of North American producers. Income investors who want a material dividend from a gold producer.

Should avoid: Investors who require liquidity and broad institutional acceptance -- AAZ will not fit in large-cap mandates. Those who cannot tolerate geopolitical risk in their commodity exposure. Growth investors who need a narrative beyond commodity price leverage. Anyone who needs their gold exposure to be diversified across jurisdictions.
