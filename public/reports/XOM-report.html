Executive Summary

Business Model

Exxon Mobil is an integrated oil and gas company operating across the full petroleum value chain: Upstream Exploration & Production: Crude oil and natural gas production across five major producing regions: US Permian Basin, Guyana (Stabroek block), Brazil, Middle East, and Australia. Exxon has one of the lowest-breakeven production portfolios in the industry with significant Permian and deepwater barrels. Downstream Refining & Chemicals: One of the world's largest refiners with ~4 million barrels per day of refining capacity across 21 refineries globally. The chemicals segment (ExxonMobil Chemical) is integrated with the refineries and focuses on high-margin specialty chemicals and polyethylene. Low-Carbon Solutions: Carbon capture and storage (CCS), hydrogen, and biofuels investments — still nascent but growing. The strategy is to leverage existing operational capabilities into emerging energy transition markets without significant capital at risk. Permian Basin Leadership: Exxon is the largest acreage holder in the US Permian Basin following the Pioneer Natural Resources acquisition, giving it a dominant position in the lowest-cost US production region. Exxon's integrated model provides a natural hedge — when upstream revenues are pressured by low oil prices, the downstream refining business typically benefits from processing margins. The Permian consolidation and Guyana deepwater discoveries have significantly strengthened the upstream portfolio over the past five years.
