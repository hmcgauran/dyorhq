Executive Summary

Jet2 PLC is a UK-focused leisure airline and tour operator offering scheduled services to leisure destinations across Europe, the USA, and Canada from its UK bases. The business operates two distinct but complementary revenue streams: a low-cost airline (Jet2.com) and a package holiday business (Jet2holidays), the latter generating the majority of group profits. The combined model provides meaningful distribution advantages over pure-play airlines and greater margin stability through the tour operator cycle.

At 1,220 GBX, the stock trades approximately 28% below its 52-week high of 1,700 GBX, reflecting broader consumer spending concerns and elevated fuel costs weighing on near-term profitability. The P/E of approximately 12x represents a meaningful compression from the premium multiples the market historically assigned during periods of strong holiday demand.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY. The valuation is not demanding. The package holiday model provides earnings resilience that pure airline peers lack. However, the consumer macro backdrop is the dominant near-term variable, and the margin of safety is insufficient to warrant aggressive accumulation at current levels. The 12x P/E appropriately reflects both the quality of the franchise and the cyclical uncertainty of the operating environment.

Business Model

Jet2 operates through two principal divisions:

Jet2.com (Airline): A leisure-focused low-cost carrier operating from 11 UK bases to over 70 destinations across Europe, North Africa, Turkey, the USA, and Canada. The airline is the fourth-largest in the UK by passengers carried. Its model prioritises leisure routes and short-to-medium haul operations, competing directly with easyJet and TUI Airways on sun destinations. Aircraft are predominantly Boeing 737-800 and 737-800SF variants, with the fleet modernising toward the 737 MAX 10.

Jet2holidays (Package Holidays): The tour operating arm, which packages airline seats with accommodation to create ABTA-protected package holidays. This division is the primary driver of group profitability and provides a higher-margin, more predictable revenue stream than standalone airline seats. The ATOL-protected package holiday market has structural advantages: UK consumer law (the Package Travel Regulations 2018) provides strong customer protection, while ABTA membership offers dispute resolution. These protections drive demand toward organised package holidays during periods of economic uncertainty.

The airline and holidays divisions are integrated: the airline provides capacity certainty for the holidays business, while the holidays business provides load factor stability for the airline. This vertical integration is the core strategic asset of the group.

Recent Catalysts

  • FY2025 trading update: Jet2 reported strong holiday booking momentum in the first half of FY2025, with package holiday revenue growing ahead of capacity. Load factors were maintained above 93%, demonstrating continued consumer demand for leisure travel despite a softer consumer backdrop.
  • Summer 2026 capacity expansion: The group has continued to add capacity for Summer 2026, with new routes to Turkey and additional frequencies to Greek islands. Turkey has been a particular beneficiary of geopolitical shift away from competing Eastern Mediterranean destinations.
  • Fuel cost headwind: Jet2's cost base has been pressured by elevated jet fuel prices, which rose approximately 15% in H2 2024 before partially reversing. The group hedges a substantial proportion of fuel costs, providing some protection, but the environment remains challenging relative to the low-fuel-cost years of 2020-2021.
  • Consumer confidence: UK consumer confidence showed signs of stabilisation in early 2026, following deterioration through 2024. The leisure travel category has proven relatively resilient in prior cycles, as consumers tend to treat holidays as semi-discretionary rather than fully discretionary expenditure.
  • 737 MAX fleet integration: The transition to 737 MAX 10 aircraft is underway. This delivers approximately 15-20% fuel efficiency improvement over the legacy 737-800 fleet, which is material given fuel is the largest single cost line.

Key Risks

  1. Consumer spending deterioration (HIGH): The UK household remains under pressure from elevated mortgage costs, high energy bills, and modest wage growth in real terms. Leisure travel is a category that can be deferred or downgraded - a package holiday substituted for a DIY booking, or a shorter trip in place of two weeks. The near-term demand environment is the single most important variable for Jet2's earnings.
  2. Fuel cost volatility (HIGH): Jet fuel is the largest single cost component, representing approximately 25-30% of operating costs. The group hedges a majority of its fuel exposure, but unhedged positions and timing mismatches can create significant earnings volatility. A 10% sustained increase in jet fuel prices would be material to margins.
  3. Competitive pricing pressure (MEDIUM): Ryanair and easyJet compete on key leisure routes. Both carriers have significantly lower cost bases than Jet2 due to lower labour costs (non-UK registered crews) and higher aircraft utilisation. Ryanair's "always low prices" strategy can force industry-wide fare compression on overlapping routes.
  4. Geopolitical disruption (MEDIUM): The Western Mediterranean, Turkey, North Africa, and Eastern Europe are all exposed to geopolitical events that can redirect or suppress demand. The Turkey recovery was a tailwind; a reversal (safe-haven outflows from Turkey) would be a headwind. Greece, Jet2's largest destination, is exposed to extreme heat season impacts on tourism.
  5. Regulatory and slot constraints (MEDIUM): Expansion at slot-controlled UK airports (Heathrow, Gatwick, Manchester) is constrained. Jet2's growth is weighted toward secondary airports where slot constraints are less binding, but this limits the ability to add premium trunk routes.
  6. Climate transition risk (LOW-MEDIUM): The UK has committed to net-zero by 2050. SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel) mandates will increase operating costs for all airlines. Jet2's relatively young and modernising fleet (via the MAX 10 order) is a partial mitigation, but SAF availability and cost at scale remains a material uncertainty for the latter half of the decade.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors who want UK consumer leisure exposure with better earnings visibility than a pure airline. Those who believe the package holiday model is structurally advantaged in a risk-off consumer environment. Long-only investors who can hold through seasonal earnings volatility and accept cyclical exposure as the price of leisure sector returns.

Should avoid: Value investors seeking deep depressed entry - at 12x P/E with macro uncertainty elevated, further multiple compression is possible before the stock is genuinely cheap. Growth investors - there is no meaningful growth premium in the current rating. Anyone unwilling to accept fuel price exposure and consumer cyclicality without a margin of safety built into the entry price.

Recommendation

OPPORTUNISTIC BUY at 1,220 GBX. The Jet2 franchise is a high-quality leisure business with structural advantages over pure airline peers. The Jet2holidays integration is genuinely differentiated and provides earnings resilience that Ryanair and easyJet lack. The valuation at 12x P/E is fair, not compelling, and reflects both the quality of the franchise and the genuine macro uncertainty of the operating environment.

The next material catalyst is the Summer 2026 booking update, expected in the Spring. Strong forward visibility on holiday bookings will either confirm the bull case or reveal that consumer caution is biting harder than the market has priced. Until that clarity emerges, accumulation is premature. Accumulate on any material deterioration from 1,150 GBX.
