Executive Summary

Interglobe Aviation Ltd is the holding company of IndiGo, India's largest airline by fleet size and passenger market share. The airline operates a predominantly domestic point-to-point network with a growing international footprint, and is the defining price competitor in Indian aviation. IndiGo commands a dominant domestic market share of approximately 55-60% and has a young, fuel-efficient Airbus A320neo family fleet. At a P/E of approximately 25x and a price of INR 4,620, the stock sits below its 52-week high, offering a reasonable entry point for a high-quality aviation compounder riding structural Indian aviation growth. The thesis is balanced: structural demand tailwinds are compelling, but fuel cost pressures, aggressive competition from Tata-backed Air India, and a stretched valuation relative to global LCC peers warrant a HOLD at this level. Conviction Score: 72/100 - HOLD

Business Model

Interglobe Aviation Ltd (IndiGo) is India's dominant low-cost carrier, operating a fleet of Airbus A320neo family aircraft (A319, A320neo, A321neo) on a predominantly point-to-point network. IndiGo is the largest airline in India by fleet size and passenger volume, with a domestic market share in the region of 55-60%. The group's model is structurally similar to other LCC leaders: high aircraft utilisation, rapid turnaround, a single aircraft type for maintenance simplicity, and competitive pricing to drive volume. Revenue is derived from passenger fares and ancillary revenues (baggage fees, seat selection, priority boarding, cargo). IndiGo has expanded aggressively into international routes (Middle East, South-East Asia, Gulf) where it competes with full-service carriers on price. The group is a significant employer and a key infrastructure player in Indian aviation. Geographic exposure is heavily India-weighted, with international routes providing diversification.

Recent Catalysts

IndiGo has been executing a fleet expansion programme using the fuel-efficient Airbus A320neo family, which provides a meaningful cost-per-seat-mile advantage over older narrowbody fleets. Strong domestic Indian aviation demand -- driven by rising middle-class disposable incomes, urbanisation, and low base penetration -- underpins load factors. The airline has been selectively adding international routes, particularly to the Gulf and South-East Asia, where yield discipline has been reasonable. Fuel price moderation in 2025-2026 has eased margin pressure relative to the prior cycle peak. The Indian aviation market is widely viewed as a long-term structural growth story, with India projected to become the third-largest aviation market by passenger volume within the next decade. Tata Group's consolidation of Air India has created a more formidable competitor, and this competitive dynamic bears watching.

Key Risks

  1. #1 Fuel price exposure: Fuel is IndiGo's largest cost line item. A sustained crude oil price spike directly compresses margins and is the single most material short-term risk to earnings.
  2. #2 Competitive intensification: The Tata Group's consolidation of Air India has created a significantly stronger competitor. Aggressive pricing or capacity expansion by Air India could pressure IndiGo's yields and load factors.
  3. #3 Regulatory and slot constraints: Indian aviation regulation, airport slot allocations at congested airports (Delhi, Mumbai), and periodic disputes with airport operators can constrain growth and increase costs.
  4. #4 Macroeconomic sensitivity: Indian domestic air travel demand is correlated with economic growth, disposable income trends, and rural economic conditions. A growth slowdown could reduce passenger volume growth.
  5. #5 Aircraft availability and engine reliability: IndiGo's fleet is predominantly Airbus A320neo family. Pratt & Whitney PW1100G-JM engine durability issues have affected some operators in this family; any fleet availability constraints would directly impact capacity.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Own: Investors seeking exposure to India's structural aviation growth through a dominant, cost-efficient operator with a strong balance sheet and proven execution track record. IndiGo is the closest thing to a blue-chip Indian aviation compounder. Long-term infrastructure-oriented investors with a 3-5 year horizon will find the story compelling. Avoid: Investors who are concerned about valuation or who prefer airlines with greater diversification beyond domestic India. Those seeking near-term re-rating catalyst will find limited support at 25x P/E. Investors who require dividends or buybacks as a component of total return should note Interglobe Aviation's capital allocation priorities favouring fleet investment.
