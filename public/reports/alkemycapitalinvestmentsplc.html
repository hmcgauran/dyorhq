Executive Summary

Alkemy Capital Investments PLC is a development-stage investment company focused on critical minerals and battery materials infrastructure. The core asset is the Tavasciute lithium project in Lithuania — a hard rock lithium project with a completed Definitive Feasibility Study and a binding Glencore offtake agreement covering 40% of projected production at competitive pricing. Wates Group has been appointed as the construction partner, FEED is complete, and the project is awaiting Final Investment Decision (FID).

The shares have pulled back sharply from a February 2026 high of ~466p to ~301p — a ~35% decline that appears driven by general small-cap lithium sector weakness and broader market conditions, not project-specific deterioration. The binding Glencore offtake remains in place. The FID timing remains the primary near-term binary catalyst.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY. Conviction: 62/100. The pullback from 466p to 301p is thesis-neutral — the project fundamentals are intact. The entry point is now materially better than it was two months ago. FID is the only thing that matters in the near term.

Business Model

Alkemy Capital is an investment vehicle structured to develop and operate the Tavasciute lithium project in Lithuania. The model is to advance the project through financing to construction, then operation, generating revenue from long-term battery-quality lithium concentrate sales into the European market.

The Glencore offtake agreement is the commercial anchor — 40% of projected production is committed at competitive pricing, de-risking the revenue model significantly. Wates Group as construction partner adds execution credibility. Ara Partners has made a non-binding equity commitment of approximately US$100m, contingent on FID.

  • Wates Group construction partner appointment: Wates, a major UK construction and infrastructure group, has been appointed as the construction partner for Tavasciute. This is an execution de-risking step — a credible contractor reduces the key-man and delivery risk that typically plagues development-stage mining projects.
  • FEED study complete: Front-End Engineering and Design is complete. This was a prerequisite for FID and for finalising the project financing structure.
  • Glencore binding offtake (40% of production): The commercial anchor remains in place. Glencore's offtake for 40% of production at competitive pricing validates the project economics.
  • Ara Partners ~US$100m equity commitment: Non-binding, contingent on FID. This is a meaningful signal of institutional confidence but needs to convert to binding before it provides full financing comfort.
  • Share price pullback: 466p to 301p: The stock has given back ~35% from its February 2026 high. No project-specific bad news has emerged. This appears to be general small-cap lithium sector de-rating and broader market conditions.
  • Q1 2026 FID deadline MISSED (16 April 2026): The chairman-stated Q1 2026 FID deadline has passed without an FID announcement. This is a yellow flag on execution timing. FID is not yet confirmed and the precise timeline for the financing package is now uncertain. This does not break the thesis but the binary catalyst has been delayed.

Key Risks

  1. FID timing (HIGH): The FID is the binary near-term catalyst. Any slippage in the FID timeline — particularly beyond Q2 2026 — would put pressure on the share price and the Ara Partners commitment.
  2. Ara Partners commitment is non-binding (HIGH): Until the FID is declared and the equity commitment converts to binding, there is meaningful execution risk. A walk-away by Ara Partners would be thesis-breaking.
  3. Lithium price risk (MEDIUM): Tavasciute economics are sensitive to lithium concentrate prices. A sustained decline would impair project returns but the Glencore offtake provides a floor.
  4. Financing risk (MEDIUM): A significant equity raise at current prices (post the 35% pullback) would be dilutive. The timing and pricing of the financing is a key variable.
  5. Lithuanian government risk (LOW-MEDIUM): The Tavasciute project requires ongoing government cooperation. Any change in the political environment could affect permitting or the Mining Development Agreement.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors who want pure-play exposure to the European lithium supply chain theme with a near-term FID catalyst. The Wates appointment and Glencore offtake are genuine de-risking milestones. The pullback to 301p is a better entry than the 466p highs.

Should avoid: Investors who cannot tolerate binary event risk. FID is the make-or-break catalyst in the next 3-6 months.
