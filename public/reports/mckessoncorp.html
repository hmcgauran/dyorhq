Executive Summary

McKesson's dominant position as the largest US pharmaceutical distributor, combined with accelerating specialty pharmacy growth and robust essential medicines demand, supports a solid hold in a defensive sector with limited downside.

Business Model

McKesson is the largest pharmaceutical distributor in North America and globally, handling approximately 40% of all pharmaceuticals prescribed in the United States. The company operates across two primary segments: (1) US Pharmacy Solutions (dispenses and distributes prescription drugs, over-the-counter products, and health and beauty items to retail chains, independent pharmacies, and health systems); and (2) Medical-Surgical Solutions (distributes medical-surgical supplies, equipment, and gloves to physicians, laboratories, and hospitals). A key growth driver is its Oncology Services platform (US Oncology Network) and specialty pharmacy capabilities, which handle high-value, high-margin biologics and gene therapies. McKesson also owns a controlling interest in Clearroad logistics (consumer health).

Recent Catalysts

  • Specialty pharmacy revenue grew 16% YoY in FY2025 (ended March 2025), driven by oncology and immunology biologics handling growth.
  • US Oncology Network now covers 4,800 oncologists across 40 states, providing recurring revenue from practice management services.
  • WIN rs (Weighing Interests in Pharma) contract renegotiations concluded favourably in FY2025, improving per-prescription economics.
  • FDA approval pipeline for specialty biologics expected to be the highest on record in 2026-27, directly expanding McKesson's handling revenue.
  • Controlled substance (Schedule II) distribution security contracts renewed with major health systems through FY2028.

Thesis Evaluation

McKesson is a high-quality essential-services business with significant barriers to entry (licensing, infrastructure, scale). The specialty pharmacy transition is the most important long-term variable - as more drugs migrate from retail to specialty (particularly oncology and rare disease), McKesson's handling fees and associated services revenue should grow well above GDP. The P/E of 24.9x is reasonable for the defensive nature of the business and consistent mid-teens earnings growth.
