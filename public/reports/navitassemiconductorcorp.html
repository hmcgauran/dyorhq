Executive Summary

Navitas is a power semiconductor company focused on gallium nitride and silicon carbide technologies, so the story depends on whether next-generation materials win enough sockets in chargers, data centres, solar, storage and EV systems to justify the current strategic excitement. The technology case is interesting. The investment case is harder because commercial ramp timing and competition can remain uncertain for longer than investors expect.

Business Model

The company designs power semiconductors intended to improve efficiency, power density and thermal performance relative to conventional silicon solutions. Its attraction lies in enabling faster charging and better power conversion in applications where efficiency matters. It is still a small semiconductor vendor, however, so design wins, customer concentration and the pace of end-market adoption are more important than broad thematic narratives.

Key Risks

  1. Design wins do not always translate into large or timely production revenue.
  2. Competition is intense across power semiconductors, including from larger established players.
  3. A small customer base can create revenue volatility.
  4. End-market ramps in EV, solar and industrial power can be delayed.
  5. The valuation can remain fragile without clear progress towards scale and profitability.
