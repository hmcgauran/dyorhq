Business Model

Vast Resources PLC is a junior mining explorer and developer operating in Zambia, with historic interests in Romania and broader African exploration activity. The group's primary focus has been the advancement of its copper and base metal projects through to production. As a junior miner, Vast's business model is dependent on equity issuance, joint venture arrangements, and asset-level financing to fund exploration and development expenditure. The company has no meaningful operating revenue at present, making it reliant on capital markets for continued funding. The shares trade on the London Stock Exchange Main Market, providing some liquidity versus OTC alternatives, though daily volume is thin and the register is likely dominated by retail and specialist resource investors. Key Risks

  1. #1 Funding risk: Vast has no operating revenue and is entirely dependent on equity capital markets for survival. Any sustained deterioration in appetite for junior mining equities could prevent the company from raising necessary capital.
  2. #2 Dilution risk: Historical financings have been highly dilutive. Further placings at sub-0.20 GBX levels would be materially accretive to the share count, creating persistent overhang on the share price.
  3. #3 Commodity price exposure: Vast's Zambian copper, zinc, and lead assets are economically sensitive to base metal prices. A sustained bear market in copper would impair the investment case even if the project advances technically.
  4. #4 Jurisdiction risk: Zambia, while a established mining jurisdiction, carries country risk including regulatory change, tax policy shifts, and potential resource nationalism as metal prices rise.
  5. #5 Liquidity and market risk: With a market capitalisation of approximately GBP 6 million and negligible daily volume, the shares are extremely illiquid. Large positions cannot be exited without significant price impact.
