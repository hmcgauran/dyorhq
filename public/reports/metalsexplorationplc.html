Executive Summary

Metals Exploration Plc is a UK-listed AIM company focused on the exploration and development of the Los Domingo copper-gold project in the Dominican Republic. At 15.00 GBX and a market capitalisation of approximately GBP40m, the company is a speculative early-stage explorer with no producing assets and no meaningful revenue. The entire investment case rests on the geological potential of Los Domingo and the ability of management to advance the project through exploration, feasibility, and ultimately into development -- a pathway that will require significant additional capital and carry a material risk of failure at each stage.

The copper and gold commodity backdrop is constructive. Copper is a beneficiary of the global energy transition and electrification theme; gold has traded at multi-year highs through 2025-2026. These macro tailwinds provide a favourable setting for a junior explorer seeking to advance a copper-gold asset. However, macro tailwinds do not change the fundamental reality that Los Domingo remains an early-stage exploration asset with no confirmed economic resource, and the path from current exploration status to a producing mine is long, capital-intensive, and uncertain.

The HOLD thesis reflects this balance: the commodity backdrop and early-stage exploration upside are real, but the speculative nature of the asset, the absence of a resource definition, the likely requirement for dilutive capital raises, and the execution risk inherent in Dominican Republic mining make this an inappropriate vehicle for investors who cannot afford a total loss of capital. Position sizing is critical.

Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY at 15.00 GBX. The speculative nature of the Los Domingo project is appropriate for investors with a genuine speculative allocation who understand the binary risk profile of early-stage mining exploration. Copper and gold macro tailwinds provide a constructive setting. Do not build a meaningful position unless you have a high conviction on the geological upside and can stomach the probability-weighted return.

Business Model

Metals Exploration Plc operates as a classic junior mining explorer: the business model is to identify, explore, and advance mineral resource projects through to a stage where a larger mining company will acquire the asset or fund its development. The single material asset is the Los Domingo project, a copper-gold exploration property located in the Dominican Republic.

The Dominican Republic has a mixed history with foreign mining investment. The country hosts several active mining operations, including the Pueblo Viejo gold mine (a world-class asset operated by Newmont and Barrick Gold), which provides a positive data point for large-scale mining in the jurisdiction. However, regulatory and permitting environments in the Caribbean and Latin America can be unpredictable, and changes to royalty regimes, environmental requirements, or resource ownership rules represent ongoing variables that investors must monitor.

Metals Exploration's value creation model is entirely dependent on drilling results and resource definition at Los Domingo. Positive drill intercepts and an increasing resource base are the primary value drivers. The company does not have a producing asset, which means there is no operational cash flow to fund exploration activity. As a result, the company is dependent on capital markets -- equity raises, potential joint venture arrangements, or asset-level debt -- to fund the continued advancement of Los Domingo. Each capital raise is dilutive to existing shareholders.

The copper-gold commodity mix is strategically relevant. Copper provides exposure to the electrification and energy transition theme; gold provides the traditional safe-haven and hard-asset hedge. This combination makes the project attractive to a broader pool of potential acquirers or development partners than a single-commodity explorer would be. However, the relative weighting of copper versus gold in the resource will determine which strategic players are most interested, and that has not yet been established with any precision.

Copper above USD10,000/tonne elevates the headline valuation of any copper exploration project and improves the perceived economics of Los Domingo should a resource be defined.
  • Gold at multi-year highs: Gold has traded firmly through Q1 2026, with spot prices holding above USD3,200/oz. A gold price at these levels improves the economics of any gold-dominant deposit and broadens the universe of strategic buyers and development partners who might consider Los Domingo.
  • Exploration programme advancement: Any positive newsflow from the Los Domingo drilling programme -- new intercepts, expanded mineralised zones, or improved geological modelling -- would be a direct re-rating catalyst. The market is pricing the asset close to the speculative floor; positive drill results could generate meaningful upside.
  • Dominican Republic mining climate: The Pueblo Viejo operation (Newmont/Barrick) continues to operate productively in the Dominican Republic, which provides an ongoing proof of concept for large-scale mining in the country. A stable operating environment for Pueblo Viejo is a positive signal for Los Domingo's long-term viability.

Key Risks

  1. Exploration binary risk (VERY HIGH): The fundamental value of Metals Exploration is entirely dependent on the geological potential of Los Domingo. If drilling does not confirm a meaningful copper-gold resource, the asset has limited to no value. Exploration is inherently probabilistic -- even geologically promising targets regularly fail to deliver economic resources. Investors must be comfortable with a high probability of total capital loss on this specific investment and size accordingly.
  2. No revenue or cash flow (VERY HIGH): The company has no producing assets. All exploration activity is funded by capital that has been or will be raised from shareholders. Each equity raise is dilutive. Without a resource definition that attracts a joint venture partner or strategic acquiror, the company will continue to burn cash with no operational income. The valuation is entirely dependent on market expectations for the exploration programme and the underlying commodity price environment.
  3. Capital markets dependency and dilution (HIGH): With a market cap of GBP40m and no cash flow, the company will require additional capital to advance Los Domingo. The quantum and timing of dilution are uncertain, but it is a near-certainty. Investors who buy at current levels will be diluted unless and until a non-dilutive funding arrangement is secured. The cost of capital for a pre-resource AIM junior is high, which compounds the dilution risk.
  4. Dominican Republic jurisdiction risk (MEDIUM-HIGH): The Dominican Republic is a developing-market jurisdiction with a mining regulatory framework that has evolved over time. While Pueblo Viejo demonstrates that large-scale foreign mining investment can work in the country, changes to royalty rates, environmental requirements, permitting timelines, or resource ownership rules could affect the viability of Los Domingo. Caribbean and Latin American politics can be volatile, and a shift in government could bring a more resource-nationalist posture.
  5. Commodity price exposure (MEDIUM): Copper and gold are the two commodity price variables that most directly affect the Los Domingo investment case. Copper prices above USD10,000/tonne improve the apparent economics of any defined resource; prices below USD8,000/tonne could render a marginal resource uneconomic. Gold prices affect the valuation of any gold-dominant component. The company does not hedge, meaning full spot price exposure in both directions.
  6. AIM liquidity and shareholder base (MEDIUM): AIM-listed junior miners typically have limited daily trading liquidity. The shareholder base skews toward retail and specialist mining investors rather than institutional generalists. Bid-offer spreads can be wide. Large positions are difficult to build or exit without material price impact. This constrains the addressable investor base and is a structural feature of the MTL register.
