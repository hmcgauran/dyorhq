Executive Summary

Polarean Imaging is a UK-headquartered medical device company that has developed a proprietary Xenon MRI imaging platform for visualising lung function. The technology enables clinicians to see ventilation defects in the lungs of patients with cystic fibrosis, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and asthma — conditions affecting hundreds of millions of patients globally. Business Model

Polarean's model involves selling or leasing Xenon gas delivery equipment to hospital radiology departments, combined with proprietary pulse sequence software for existing MRI scanners. The consumables revenue comes from the ongoing supply of isotopically enriched Xenon-129 gas to imaging centres. The company works with major MRI OEM partners to integrate its technology into existing scanner platforms. The key commercial milestones are: (1) gaining coverage in major US hospital systems, (2) securing CPT reimbursement codes that allow hospitals to bill for Xenon MRI procedures, and (3) establishing clinical partnerships that generate published evidence for the technology's diagnostic value in CF, COPD, and asthma. Without adequate reimbursement, hospital adoption will remain slow regardless of clinical merit. The technology has a genuine scientific moat — Xenon MRI for lung ventilation imaging is technically difficult and Polarean holds key patents and know-how in gas administration, pulse sequence design, and image processing. However, a scientific moat does not automatically translate to a commercial one.
