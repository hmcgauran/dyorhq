Executive Summary

Arteche is a Spanish industrial champion that has quietly compounded earnings at pace in a niche corner of electrical equipment and defence electronics. Net profit surged 93 per cent to EUR 45.3 million in fiscal year 2025, with sales reaching EUR 508.4 million, reflecting strong demand in energy infrastructure and railway signalling. At EUR 28.10 and a market capitalisation of approximately EUR 1.6 billion, the stock trades on 51 times last year's earnings — elevated, but justified if growth continues. The 52-week range of EUR 7.30 to EUR 30.00 reflects the recovery story from a low base.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY at EUR 28.10. The numbers are strong and the niche is defensible, but the valuation requires continued execution.

Business Model

Arteche operates three core divisions. Its electrical equipment division supplies measurement and protection devices for electricity transmission and distribution networks — a recurring revenue stream tied to grid modernisation cycles across Spain, Portugal, and Latin America. Its railway signalling division provides control systems and components to rail operators, a segment with long contract tails and high barrier to entry. Its defence electronics division manufactures electronic warfare and communication systems for military customers, predominantly in Europe and Latin America.

The group has particular depth in Spanish-speaking markets, where it competes against global giants like ABB and Siemens on smaller, more localised projects where service and domain knowledge matter more than scale. Approximately 45 per cent of revenues are recurring or contract-based, providing reasonable earnings visibility.

Recent Catalysts

  • FY2025 results (26 February 2026): Net profit EUR 45.3 million, up 93.4 per cent year-on-year. Sales EUR 508.4 million. Strong beat versus prior year driven by energy transition CapEx and defence contract timing.
  • European energy transition spending: Grid operators across Spain and the EU are accelerating investment in transmission infrastructure to support renewable integration. Arteche is a direct beneficiary of this multi-year CapEx cycle.
  • Defence spending acceleration: NATO members increasing defence budgets post-2022 have supported demand for signalling and electronic warfare components. Arteche's exposure to Spanish and Latin American defence markets positions it to capture contract wins.
  • Latin America growth: Infrastructure investment in Brazil, Mexico, and Chile provides geographic diversification beyond European core markets.

Key Risks

  1. Valuation is not cheap: At 51 times earnings, the stock prices in a fair amount of success. Any profit disappointment will cause disproportionate damage.
  2. Latin American currency risk: A significant portion of revenues is denominated in Latin American pesos and reais. Depreciation could reduce reported earnings in EUR terms.
  3. Grid CapEx cyclically: Energy infrastructure spending is lumpy. A political or regulatory delay in grid investment could reduce order intake.
  4. Small-cap liquidity: BME is not a highly liquid exchange for international investors. Bid-ask spreads and execution risk are material for larger positions.
  5. Defence contract concentration: Some large contracts can dominate a year's revenue. Timing variability can create earnings volatility.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: European infrastructure investors, defence sector specialists, and anyone looking for exposure to the energy transition CapEx cycle with a differentiated mid-cap name.

Should avoid: Investors needing earnings growth clarity beyond 12 months, or those uncomfortable with single-name currency risk and mid-cap liquidity.
