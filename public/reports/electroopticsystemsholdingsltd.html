Executive Summary

Electro Optic Systems is an Australian defence technology company that has staged a remarkable recovery from AUD 1.10 at the start of 2025 to AUD 10.70 currently, a near 10-bagger in 15 months. The gain reflects a combination of contract momentum, satellite customer demand, and a broader re-rating of defence technology names. However, the stock now trades at 9.7 times 2025 revenue (AUD 44.1M in H1 2025 alone) and the 52-week high of AUD 11.80 is within reach. The company is not yet profitable on a reported basis, with EPS of AUD -0.40. FY2025 full-year results were released on 23 February 2026, and FY2025 full-year revenue should be assessed against the H1 base. The key question is whether the contract backlog can sustain revenue growth of the pace required to justify the current multiple.

Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY at AUD 10.70. The momentum is real and the addressable market is growing, but the valuation is full. Investors entering here should accept that significant further upside requires continued positive announcements and margin expansion. The risk-reward is no longer generous at this price.

Business Model

EOS designs and manufactures electro-optic systems for military and law enforcement customers. Key products include remote weapon systems, fire control systems, electronic warfare equipment, and satellite communications systems. The company operates across three segments: land systems (weapons, sights, fire control), space systems (satellite communications and tracking), and security/counter-drone systems. Customers include the Australian Defence Force, NATO member governments, and allied law enforcement agencies.

The counter-drone segment has attracted particular investor interest given global demand for anti-UAV systems following the Ukraine conflict and Middle East instability. EOS's Vigilant Forward Looking Sonar (FLS) and Sentinel Intruder Detection system serve both the maritime and land-based counter-drone markets.

Key Risks

  1. Valuation is stretched: At 9.7 times H1 annualised revenue, the stock is pricing in substantial growth. Any guidance miss or contract delay could cause significant multiple compression.
  2. Not yet profitable: Negative EPS means the company has no earnings cushion. Revenue must continue to grow to justify the multiple. Cash burn or working capital build could require future capital raises.
  3. Highly volatile: The stock has gone from AUD 1.10 to AUD 10.70 in under 15 months. The magnitude of the move creates its own risk as early investors look to take profit.
  4. ASX liquidity and coverage: Limited sell-side analyst coverage and retail-dominated shareholder register can cause sharp moves on relatively small trading volumes.

Should avoid: General investors, those requiring profitability or earnings visibility, and anyone who cannot tolerate the volatility inherent in a near-10-bagger that is approaching 52-week highs.
