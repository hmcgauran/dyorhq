Executive Summary

NuScale is one of the most recognisable listed names in advanced nuclear because it carries a real regulatory credential, the first U.S. NRC-approved SMR design. That matters. It gives the company a level of technical and policy credibility many earlier-stage peers do not have.

But the equity is telling a harsher story. The live price is $9.16, down 84.0% from the 52 week high of $57.42 and sitting effectively on the 52 week low. This is still a loss-making business with EPS -2.17 and a market cap of around $3.10bn. Investors are no longer rewarding the company for being strategically interesting. They want evidence that its commercial model can work at scale.

Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY. NuScale remains relevant to the nuclear theme, but the equity is still too dependent on future commercial proof rather than present operating strength.

Business Model

NuScale is developing small modular reactor systems for utility, industrial and large-load customers. The commercial opportunity is substantial if customers adopt SMRs as part of future energy infrastructure. Revenue should ultimately come from design licensing, engineering services, project participation and long-cycle deployment support.

The challenge is that nuclear businesses do not get paid for technical elegance alone. They need financeable projects, committed customers, manageable timelines and confidence that build economics will hold together under real-world conditions.

Key Risks

  1. Commercial execution risk: projects may remain conceptual rather than contractual.
  2. Funding risk: prolonged losses may force future dilution.
  3. Timeline risk: nuclear projects are highly vulnerable to slippage.
  4. Competitive risk: stronger players elsewhere in the supply chain may capture more of the eventual value.
  5. Narrative risk: if AI-power enthusiasm fades, NuScale may lose a major sentiment support.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: investors who want a small, high-risk option on the SMR theme and are comfortable waiting through volatility and execution uncertainty.

Should avoid: anyone who wants current profitability, predictable compounding or a cleaner nuclear exposure.

Recommendation

REDUCE.

NuScale still matters strategically, but the stock is not yet earning a high-conviction place in a serious portfolio. The risk is not that the technology is irrelevant. The risk is that commercial traction arrives too slowly and the capital structure absorbs the pain first.
