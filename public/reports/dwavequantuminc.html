Executive Summary

D-Wave Quantum is the oldest listed quantum computing company, the only one with both annealing and gate-model quantum systems, and — following the acquisition of Quantum Circuits Inc (QCI) — now a full-stack quantum provider. The April 9 report (conviction 44, REDUCE, $14.56) was a reasonable assessment of an overvalued quantum story. That view now requires material revision.

Revenue growth of 179% year-on-year is not a showcase partnership — it is a company that is demonstrably commercialising. Fortune 100 customers are signing QCaaS contracts. The CEO is talking publicly about quantum advantage being real and measurable. The Nvidia quantum rally in April 2026 has amplified the move, but underneath it there is genuine business progress. The analyst consensus price target of $36-38 implies 73-83% further upside from $20.81.

The stock is not cheap. At $20.81 the market is pricing in significant quantum commercialisation. But unlike most quantum peers, D-Wave now has the revenue growth to back up some of that narrative. The upgrade from REDUCE to HOLD is warranted — though not a BUY at current levels given the speculative premium and quantum sector volatility.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY at $20.81. 179% revenue growth, Fortune 100 contracts, full-stack quantum, and analyst PT $36-38 are genuine upgrades to the thesis. The $20.81 entry is not a bargain but it is not a REDUCE either. BUY on pullback to $15-17 on any quantum sector cooling.

Business Model

D-Wave sells quantum computing access via its Leap cloud platform (QCaaS — Quantum Computing as a Service), sells quantum systems hardware outright, and provides professional services. Unlike annealing-only competitors, D-Wave expanded into gate-model quantum computing through the QCI acquisition, giving it the only commercially available hybrid quantum annealing/gate-model system — the Advantage2 prototype.

The QCaaS model provides recurring revenue through subscriptions and usage-based pricing, which is more scalable than one-off hardware sales. Fortune 100 customers using Leap for optimisation problems in logistics, drug discovery, and financial modelling represent the credible commercial use case that quantum bulls have been promising for years.

The relocation to Florida's "Quantum Coast" is a strategic positioning play — Florida has been actively recruiting quantum and AI companies, suggesting state-level incentives and talent attraction are part of the rationale.

Key Risks

  1. Valuation is demanding (HIGH): At $20.81 with 179% YoY growth the revenue multiple is still very high. Even at $4bn market cap, D-Wave needs to show it can grow to $500M+ revenue to justify the valuation — that is still a 8x multiple from current revenue levels.
  2. Nvidia quantum rally is amplifying the move (HIGH): A significant portion of the +22.6% session is sector momentum and short-covering, not new fundamental buying. If the broader quantum trade cools, QBTS will give back a meaningful portion of this rally.
  3. Quantum sector competition (MEDIUM): IBM and Google are building gate-model systems. IonQ is the competing pure-play. The hybrid annealing + gate-model approach is theoretically differentiated but requires commercial validation.
  4. Still deeply unprofitable (MEDIUM): EPS of approximately -$1.11 means the company is burning cash. The path to profitability in quantum computing is not yet clear. Cash runway and dilution risk remain.
  5. High volatility, binary outcomes (HIGH): Quantum computing remains a speculative sector. The distance from 52w high of $46.75 means the stock has already experienced an 80% drawdown from peak. These are the characteristics of a binary, high-volatility spec stock.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Speculative investors who want quantum exposure with a demonstrable commercial ramp. Unlike IONQ or Rigetti, D-Wave's Fortune 100 contracts and 179% revenue growth provide a grounded bull case rather than pure narrative.

Should avoid: Risk-averse investors. The valuation is demanding and the stock's 52w range ($5.97 to $46.75) reflects the extreme volatility of the quantum sector. Any misstep in earnings or guidance will cause severe pain.
