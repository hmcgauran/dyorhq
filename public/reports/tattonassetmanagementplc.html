Business Model

Tatton Asset Management PLC is a specialist UK wealth management group. Its core activity is the provision of model portfolio services (MPS) -- a range of multi-asset, risk-profiled investment solutions -- delivered predominantly through the UK's financial adviser channel. Advisers use Tatton's model portfolios as the underlying investment wrapper for their private client portfolios. Tatton also manages its own range of multi-asset funds. The business model is fee-based, generating recurring income from funds under management (FUM) with no direct product manufacturing risk and no reliance on transaction commissions. The adviser distribution model gives Tatton access to a broad client base without the cost of a direct-to-consumer proposition. Geographic exposure is exclusively UK, with all FUM invested in globally diversified underlying assets. This is a capital-light, scalable platform business with strong operating leverage as FUM grows.

Key Risks

  1. #1 UK equity market risk: Tatton's FUM and revenues are directly exposed to UK and global equity market movements. A sustained bear market would reduce fee income materially.
  2. #2 Adviser channel concentration: The business depends on the financial adviser distribution network. Reduced IFA numbers through ongoing consolidation would impair growth and fee revenue.
  3. #3 Regulatory risk: UK financial services regulation (FCA) imposes conduct and capital requirements on investment managers. Any change to adviser duty obligations or MPS regulatory frameworks could affect the business model.
  4. #4 Competitive pressure in MPS: The model portfolio services market is becoming increasingly crowded with both standalone providers and vertically integrated competitors offering similar solutions at lower cost.
  5. #5 Liquidity and exit risk: As a mid-cap UK listed company, Tatton's liquidity is limited. A large seller would move the market materially. This is a consideration for institutional holders.
