Executive Summary

Business Model

Goldman Sachs operates across four primary business segments: Investment Banking: Advisory (M&A, divestitures, restructurings) and equity/debt underwriting. Goldman is consistently ranked among the top three global M&A advisors and is particularly strong in high-profile, large-cap transactions. Fee revenue is highly cyclical and tied to global M&A and IPO activity levels. FICC and Equities Trading (Global Markets): Fixed-income, currencies, commodities (FICC), and equities trading for institutional clients. This is the highest-revenue segment in absolute terms and provides significant earnings stability through market cycles, though it is sensitive to volatility levels and client activity. Asset and Wealth Management: Traditional and alternative asset management (private equity, hedge funds, real estate, credit) alongside private wealth management for UHNW and HNW individuals. This segment has grown significantly and provides recurring fee revenue with some performance fee upside. Consumer & Wealth Management (Marcus / Personal Finance): The Marcus by Goldman Sachs digital banking platform and personal finance products. After the Apple Card partnership restructuring and exit from Marcus UK, the strategic scope of this segment is narrower than originally envisioned. Goldman's brand, talent density, and client relationships represent a durable competitive moat in investment banking and trading. The franchise's reputation for advising on the most consequential transactions globally is a self-reinforcing competitive advantage.
