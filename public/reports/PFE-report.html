Executive Summary

Pfizer's post-COVID normalisation and patent cliff create a structural earnings headwind that the current valuation of 19.85x P/E does not adequately discount — the balance of risk and reward argues for REDUCE.

Business Model

Pfizer discovers, develops and commercialises pharmaceuticals and vaccines. Its portfolio spans oncology, immunology, cardiology, rare diseases and vaccines. Key products include the mRNA-based Comirnaty vaccine (Covid-19) and Paxlovid antiviral, both of which generated extraordinary revenues in 2021–2022. The pipeline includes oncology candidates (amcenestrant, sigvastat), a next-generation pneumococcal vaccine (PCV20) and gene therapies. The company funds R&D through internal discovery and bolt-on licensing deals.

Recent Catalysts

PaxlovidScript volume has normalised sharply from its 2022 peak. The FDA declined to grant full approval for tanezumab (chronic pain) in 2025, a setback for the partnered asset. Positive Phase III data for PCV20 in adults strengthens theVaccines franchise ahead of a 2026 regulatory filing. Pfizer has announced a multi-year cost restructuring programme targeting $3.5 billion in savings by 2028. The board has maintained the dividend, though free cash flow coverage has tightened.

Key Risks

  • 1COVID revenue normalisation: Comirnaty and Paxlovid generated $56 billion combined in 2022. The normalisation to a seasonal, low-single-digit baseline leaves a significant and permanent revenue hole.
  • 2Patent cliff on key drugs: Several major products face generic or biosimilar entry over the 2026–2028 horizon, including Xeljanz (JAK inhibitor, FDA black-box warning weighing on volumes) and Ibrance (palbociclib) facing CDK4/6 competitive pressure.
  • 3R&D productivity: Pfizer's pipeline, while broad, has not produced a comparable mega-blockbuster replacement for the COVID franchise. Multiple high-profile Phase III setbacks in recent years suggest execution risk.
  • 4Valuation does not reflect structural headwinds: At 19.85x P/E, the market appears to be pricing a recovery that requires consistent pipeline successes that are not yet demonstrated.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Recommendation

REDUCE. Pfizer faces a genuine structural challenge: the extraordinary COVID era revenues are not returning, and replacing that revenue base requires consistent pipeline execution that has not yet been demonstrated at scale. The P/E of 19.85x is not cheap relative to the earnings trajectory. Entry framework: BUY below $22 (provides a margin of safety on base-case EPS of $1.60–$1.85, roughly 16–18x normalised earnings). Reduce above $29 (prices in pipeline success that is not yet assured).
