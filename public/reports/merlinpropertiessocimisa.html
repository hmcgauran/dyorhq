Executive Summary

MERLIN Properties SOCIMI SA is Spain's largest internally managed REIT by portfolio value, with a focused portfolio of commercial real estate across Spain and Portugal. The company benefits from the SOCIMI tax-efficient structure, delivering a normalised FFO yield that is attractive relative to the broader European property sector. At a P/E of 10.89x and a dividend yield north of 5%, the stock offers a compelling combination of income and capital appreciation potential in a quality Spanish real estate asset base -- BUY. Conviction Score: 68/100 - BUY

Business Model

MERLIN Properties SOCIMI SA is a publicly listed Spanish Real Estate Investment Trust (SOCIMI) operating under the favourable tax treatment of Spain's SOCIMI regime, which levies a 0% corporate tax rate on qualifying rental income and unrealised gains, provided a minimum 80% of income is distributed as dividends and certain asset holding conditions are met. MERLIN's portfolio is concentrated in four asset classes: offices (principally in Madrid and Barcelona CBD and business parks), retail (shopping centres and high-street assets), logistics (warehouse and distribution centres), and to a lesser extent data centres and other specialised use assets. The business is structured to generate stable, recurring rental income from a tenant base that spans major Spanish and multinational corporates, financial institutions, and government entities. MERLIN is one of the largest real estate owners in the Iberian Peninsula, with a portfolio gross asset value exceeding EUR 15bn. The company maintains an active asset management strategy -- leasing, repositioning, and development Extensions within its existing assets -- to drive organic rental growth and sustain occupancy rates. The SOCIMI structure, combined with the scale and quality of the underlying portfolio, forms the core investment proposition.

Recent Catalysts

The Spanish commercial property market has shown signs of stabilisation following a prolonged repricing cycle that compressed valuations significantly from the 2022 peaks. Office vacancy rates in Madrid and Barcelona have improved marginally as new supply tightens and demand from financial services and technology tenants stabilises. Logistics demand remains supported by structural e-commerce growth and near-shoring trends in the Iberian supply chain. MERLIN has continued to execute its asset management programme, signing new leases and indexation clauses that partially protect rental income against inflation. The ECB's rate-cutting cycle is a direct tailwind for REIT valuations, reducing the discount rate applied to future rental cash flows and easing debt refinancing costs. In Q1 2026, Spanish REIT indices have outperformed the broader European property sector, reflecting improved sentiment toward domestic real estate. No material RNS since the H1 2025 results; next material update likely at the full-year results in February/June cycle.

Key Risks

  1. #1 Office vacancy and rental pressure: MERLIN's portfolio retains significant exposure to Madrid and Barcelona office markets, where vacancy rates remain elevated and rental re-pricing could compress income if demand weakens further.
  2. #2 Interest rate and refinancing risk: Although the ECB is in a cutting cycle, rising rates globally or any stall in the easing path could increase MERLIN's debt refinancing costs and compress FFO, while also impacting property cap rate valuations.
  3. #3 Concentration in Spanish market: Unlike pan-European REIT peers, MERLIN's portfolio is overwhelmingly concentrated in Spain, creating idiosyncratic country risk around Spanish economic growth, fiscal policy, and property market dynamics.
  4. #4 Asset valuation sensitivity: Property REITs are exposed to mark-to-market valuations that can fluctuate significantly with cap rate changes; a 50bp expansion in cap rates could materially reduce reported NAV and trigger asset impairment charges.
  5. #5 Tenant concentration risk: A small number of large tenants account for a material portion of rental income; any significant tenant default or consolidation could create income disruption in specific assets.
