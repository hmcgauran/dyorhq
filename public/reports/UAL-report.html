Executive Summary

United Airlines is a high-quality global network carrier with a compelling international growth trajectory and a P/E under 10x that significantly undervalues its earnings power and asset base, and is rated HOLD.

Business Model

United is one of the world's largest airlines, operating a comprehensive global network from its hubs at major US cities. Following the 2022 announcement of "United Next", the carrier has committed to a significant fleet transformation aimed at improving unit economics and competitive positioning: Mainline Passenger Revenue~85% of revenue; domestic and international scheduled service Cargo~5% of revenue; belly cargo on passenger aircraft and freighter operations Mileage Plus (Loyalty)~7% of revenue; co-brand credit card partnership and retail monetisation ancillary / Other~3%; ground handling, maintenance services United's "United Next" plan involves retiring older narrowbody aircraft and replacing them with a large order of Boeing 737 MAX 10s and Airbus A321neos, which offer significantly better fuel efficiency and passenger experience. The international network -- particularly the Pacific and Atlantic routes -- is United's key differentiator versus low-cost carriers and a major driver of premium revenue.
