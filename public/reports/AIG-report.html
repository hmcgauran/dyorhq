Executive Summary

AIG is a complex, restructuring multi-line insurer that has made meaningful progress simplifying its operations and strengthening its balance sheet, but the valuation at low-14x earnings is not compelling given persistent catastrophe volatility and execution uncertainty, warranting a REDUCE.

Business Model

AIG is one of the largest insurance organisations in the world, operating through two core divisions following its multi-year restructuring: General Insurance (Commercial & Personal Lines)~65% of pre-tax income; commercial P&C, specialty lines, A&H, personal auto and homeowners Life & Retirement~35% of pre-tax income; individual life, group benefits, retirement accounts AIG's commercial insurance operations serve large multinational corporations, middle-market businesses, and specialty niche markets including political risk, cyber liability, and marine insurance. The personal lines business has been significantly scaled back following the 2021 Strawberry Hill reinsurance transaction. AIG earns revenues primarily through insurance premiums and investment income on the float generated by its insurance liabilities.
