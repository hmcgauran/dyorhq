Executive Summary

Pantheon Resources is a NASDAQ-listed (and LSE cross-listed) oil and gas exploration and production company focused on the Alaskan North Slope. The company holds significant acreage in the prolific Piatan and North Slope basins, with confirmed resource estimates in the multibillion-barrel range across multiple projects. Pantheon is currently loss-making, pre-revenue, and dependent on financing to progress its development projects through to first oil. The cross-listed structure and North Slope positioning give it a distinct speculative profile. HOLD. Conviction Score: 45/100 - HOLD

Business Model

Pantheon Resources Plc is an independent oil and gas exploration and production company incorporated in the United Kingdom and listed on the NASDAQ (ticker: PANR) with a standard listing on the London Stock Exchange. The company's core asset is its 100% working interest in the Piatan and North Slope acreage on Alaska's North Slope. Pantheon has delineated multiple prospects across its acreage position, with confirmed aggregate resource estimates exceeding several hundred million barrels of oil equivalent (mmboe) at the project level. The company's projects are at various stages of development -- from early-stage exploration through to contingent resources awaiting development approval. Pantheon does not yet have commercial production and is pre-revenue, financing its activities through equity raises and farm-out agreements. The business model requires successful fundraising, regulatory approvals, and partner engagement to progress assets through to commercial production -- creating significant execution risk alongside the undoubted geological potential.

Recent Catalysts

Pantheon has been advancing permitting for its North Slope projects, with the goal of reaching final investment decision (FID) on its most advanced assets. The company has sought farm-down partners to reduce its capital burden and bring external expertise to project development. Alaskan North Slope infrastructure development continues to attract attention as transport costs and LNG export potential improve project economics. Oil price recovery from 2020-2021 lows has improved the implicit valuation of North Slope barrels. Pantheon has been active in investor relations, targeting both US and UK institutional audiences given its cross-listing. Capital raise activity has been a persistent feature given the pre-revenue status -- dilution risk is an ongoing consideration. Recent operational updates have focused on well results and resource certification, with the company targeting reserve upgrades at its key prospects.

Key Risks

  1. #1 Financing and dilution risk: Pantheon is pre-revenue and will need to raise significant capital to develop its assets. Ongoing equity raises will dilute existing shareholders and create overhang until production cash flows materialise.
  2. #2 Project execution and permitting risk: Alaskan North Slope development requires complex regulatory approvals, environmental permits, and infrastructure agreements. Delays at any stage can materially impact project timelines and economics.
  3. #3 Oil price sensitivity: Project economics are directly tied to oil prices. A sustained decline below $50-60/bbl could render some development scenarios uneconomic and impair the company's ability to attract farm-in partners.
  4. #4 Farm-out and partnership risk: Pantheon is seeking to reduce its capital exposure through farm-outs. Failure to secure suitable partners on reasonable terms would force the company to slow development or accept less favourable terms.
  5. #5 Resource estimation uncertainty: The company's published resource estimates are based on drilling data and probabilistic modelling. Downward revisions at any stage, or disappointing well results, would have a significant negative impact on the valuation.
