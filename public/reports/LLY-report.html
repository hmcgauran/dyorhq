Executive Summary

Business Model

Eli Lilly discovers, develops, and commercialises pharmaceutical products across several key therapeutic areas: GLP-1 Agonists (Mounjaro/GIP, Zepbound/GIP-GLP1, Wegovy/GLP1): The crown jewel of the portfolio. Tirzepatide (Mounjaro for diabetes, Zepbound for obesity) and semaglutide (Wegovy) are driving extraordinary revenue growth in what is rapidly becoming the largest pharmaceutical category by revenue globally. Manufacturing scale and pipeline depth are the key competitive moats. Oncology: A growing portfolio including Verzenio (breast cancer), Jaypirca (leukaemia/lymphoma), and several earlier-stage assets. The oncology business is a key earnings growth driver independent of the GLP-1 franchise. Immunology: IL-17 and IL-23 inhibitors (Mirikizumab, Omvoh) competing in immunology, with potential for market share gains in psoriasis and Crohn's disease. Diabetes (beyond GLP-1): Trulicity (dulaglutide), Humalog (insulin), and other legacy diabetes products provide a stable foundation even as GLP-1s capture incremental market share within the diabetes category. Neuroscience: An emerging pipeline in Alzheimer's disease (donanemab under FDA review) and potential psychiatric indications represent meaningful future optionality. Lilly's model combines an exceptional internal discovery engine with a disciplined approach to lifecycle management — extending patent cliffs through formulation improvements and combination therapies.
