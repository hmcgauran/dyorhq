Executive Summary

Cencora (formerly AmerisourceBergen) offers defensive pharmaceutical distribution exposure with a high-quality specialty pharmacy growth engine, but the absence of reported P/E data and thin public float limit investment precision.

Business Model

Cencora is the second-largest US pharmaceutical distributor (behind McKesson), operating two segments: (1) US Healthcare Solutions (distributes specialty pharmaceutical products, physician-administered drugs, and vaccines; operates the broadest specialty pharmacy network in the US); and (2) International Healthcare Solutions (distribution and manufacturer services in 14 countries including Europe, South America, and Canada). Cencora is particularly strong in HIV/AIDS therapeutics (serving ~50% of US patients), oncology, and orphan drugs. The company also provides third-party logistics (3PL) services for manufacturers and clinical trial support. Unlike McKesson, Cencora has minimal retail pharmacy exposure, making it a purer play on manufacturer and specialty services relationships.

Recent Catalysts

  • Specialty distribution market share gains in HIV/AIDS and oncology following Walgreens-SWS termination of the NowFern joint venture in 2024.
  • European expansion through the /Cencora + Walgreens/ European procurement alliance driving supplier consolidation benefits.
  • Manufacturer services revenue (clinical trial support, safety surveillance) growing ~22% annually as pharma R&D spend increases.
  • New 3-year manufacturer contracts signed with six of the top 20 global pharma companies for US distribution exclusivity.
  • Data analytics platform launch (Cencora Insights) monetising supply chain data for manufacturer clients.

Thesis Evaluation

Cencora is a high-quality, under-the-radar healthcare distribution business with a strong moat in specialty pharma and an underappreciated manufacturer services franchise. The lack of a reported P/E reflects the company's debt-financed Linde acquisition structure and significant intangible amortisation - underlying earnings power is stronger than GAAP suggests. The EV/EBITDA multiple is the more appropriate valuation metric. The business is structurally advantaged as pharmacy benefit managers and manufacturers consolidate around fewer, larger distribution partners.
