Key Risks

  • 1Capital equipment cycle risk: da Vinci systems represent significant capital expenditure for hospitals. Any rationalisation of hospital capital budgets directly impacts system placement volumes.
  • 2da Vinci 5 ramp uncertainty: New system launches carry execution risk. Any clinicalcomplication or technical issue with the dV5 platform would be material to the investment thesis.
  • 3Competitive entry: Medtronic's Hugo RAS, Johnson & Johnson's Ottava, and Chinese domestic competitors (Mazor, Robdoc) represent meaningful long-term competitive threats.
  • 4Procedure reimbursement risk: CMS reimbursement rates for robotic procedures could come under pressure, reducing the financial incentive for hospitals to adopt robotic surgery.
  • 5China geopolitical risk: Sino-US tensions could restrict Intuitive's ability to operate in China, a meaningful long-term growth market.
