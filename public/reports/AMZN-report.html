Executive Summary

Amazon is the world's largest e-commerce marketplace and cloud infrastructure provider, operating a network effect business with two dominant platforms — marketplace and AWS — that reinforce each other. AWS is the leading cloud platform, generating $100B+ in annual revenue with strong growth and significant AI opportunity through Bedrock. Business Model

Amazon operates three core businesses: North America e-commerce (approximately 45% of revenue), International e-commerce (approximately 25%), and Amazon Web Services (approximately 25%). The Advertising segment is a high-margin overlay on all three that has become a significant profit driver in its own right.AWS is the dominant cloud platform, competing with Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud. AWS's AI strategy centres on Bedrock — a managed service for building generative AI applications — and Trainium/Inferentia custom AI chips that compete with NVIDIA GPUs. The AI opportunity for AWS is not just compute but the full stack of enterprise AI adoption.The e-commerce marketplace is transitioning from a logistics company that happens to sell goods to a logistics infrastructure provider that monetises its fulfilment network through third-party seller services. This shift is improving margins structurally.

Key Risks

  • 1Azure competition (MEDIUM): Microsoft Azure's integration with OpenAI and Microsoft 365 is a meaningful competitive advantage in enterprise AI adoption. AWS must demonstrate that Bedrock's model choice and enterprise features can compete effectively.
  • 2E-commerce competition (MEDIUM): Temu and Shein are taking share in ultra-fast fashion and general merchandise. If they expand categories and logistics capability, they become a structural headwind for Amazon's marketplace.
  • 3AI capex pressure (MEDIUM): Amazon is investing heavily in custom AI chips and data centre infrastructure. The ROI of this investment is uncertain — if AI adoption is slower than expected, the capex burden will weigh on free cash flow.
