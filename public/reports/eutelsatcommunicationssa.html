Executive Summary

Eutelsat Communications SA is one of the world's oldest and largest satellite operators, providing fixed and broadcast satellite services across Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and the Americas. The group operates a fleet of Geostationary (GEO) satellites and holds a controlling interest in OneWeb, the Low Earth Orbit (LEO) broadband constellation. The company is currently loss-making following heavy investment in the OneWeb LEO buildout and faces structural headwinds in its core video broadcast business from OTT disruption. The stock trades near its 52-week low, reflecting these challenges. The strategic case rests on whether OneWeb can achieve commercial scale before GEO revenues deteriorate further. HOLD Conviction Score: 38/100 - HOLD

Business Model

Eutelsat Communications SA is a Paris-listed satellite operator founded in 1977. The group operates two distinct platforms: a fleet of Geostationary (GEO) satellites providing video broadcasting, data services, and government connectivity; and a controlling stake in OneWeb, a Low Earth Orbit (LEO) constellation designed for broadband internet access. The GEO business is the near-term cash engine: Eutelsat's 35 GEO satellites serve broadcast customers (TV channels, platform operators), telecom operators requiring backhaul, and government/defence contracts. The OneWeb LEO constellation, now substantially deployed, is intended to address the growing market for high-throughput, low-latency broadband, particularly in underserved and remote regions. Revenue is split across Video (the largest segment), Data Services, Government Services, and Connectivity (OneWeb). The group is headquartered in Paris and operates across 150+ countries.

Recent Catalysts

OneWeb's constellation reached near-global coverage in 2023-2024, enabling the group to launch commercial broadband services globally. Eutelsat has continued to consolidate OneWeb's commercial operations, with early enterprise and government contracts beginning to contribute. The GEO video business, however, continues to face secular pressure as European broadcasters migrate to OTT platforms, reducing transponder utilisation on key satellites. No major RNS has been published since the last reporting period. The group is in a transition phase: GEO cash flows funding the LEO growth story, with the market yet to assign meaningful credit to OneWeb's commercial traction.

Key Risks

  1. #1 LEO execution risk: OneWeb must achieve commercial scale against well-funded and entrenched competition from Starlink and the incoming Amazon Kuiper constellation. Failure to gain market share would render the LEO investment value-destructive.
  2. #2 GEO revenue secular decline: Broadcast video revenues face structural headwinds from OTT platform migration. If the decline accelerates, GEO cash generation may be insufficient to fund the LEO buildout and debt servicing.
  3. #3 Balance sheet and leverage: The combined Eutelsat-OneWeb entity carries significant debt. With the company currently loss-making, financial flexibility is constrained and any revenue shortfall has limited buffer.
  4. #4 Regulatory and spectrum risk: LEO constellations require coordination with national regulators and ITU spectrum allocations. Changes to licensing terms or spectrum priority rules could impair OneWeb's competitive position.
  5. #5 Geopolitical risk: Satellite operators with global footprints face exposure to geopolitical tensions, sanctions, and restrictions on cross-border service delivery in sensitive regions.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Own: Speculative investors with a high risk tolerance who believe in the long-term LEO satellite broadband thesis and want exposure to OneWeb's commercial trajectory at a distressed entry point. Avoid: Conservative income investors (the dividend is at risk and likely suspended). ESG-focused investors with concerns about satellite debris and orbital sustainability. Investors who prefer near-term cash generative businesses with visible earnings trajectories.
