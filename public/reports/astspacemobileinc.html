Executive Summary

AST SpaceMobile is a direct-to-device satellite broadband company that connects standard mobile handsets to its space-based network without requiring specialised hardware. The technology is genuinely differentiated and the MNO partnerships (AT&T, Verizon, Vodafone, TELUS, Saudi Telecom) are high-quality. The company has over $1 billion in contracted revenue commitments. The near-term catalyst is the BlueBird 7 satellite launch on New Glenn (~April 19, 2026) — the most important near-term binary event in the portfolio.

The Amazon Globalstar $11.6 billion deal has introduced a significant new competitive dynamic: a "three-way race" between AST, Iridium, and Amazon/Globalstar has been flagged by the FCC. This is new competitive context that was not present in the prior report (8 Apr 2026, conviction 49, REDUCE).

The stock has pulled back ~10% since the prior report ($96 to $87), partly on the Amazon news. Analyst average targets of $86-90 suggest current levels are fairly valued absent a positive launch catalyst.

Bottom line: OPPORTUNISTIC BUY. BB7 launch (~April 19) is the binary near-term catalyst. Current price at ~$87 reflects the post-Amazon-news uncertainty. BUY below $75 on pre-launch weakness. Upgrade to BUY-SPEKULATIVE if BB7 launches successfully and early data is positive.

Business Model

AST SpaceMobile operates a constellation of low-earth orbit satellites that connect directly to standard mobile handsets. The key differentiator is that no new hardware is required on the consumer side — existing smartphones connect to AST's network in the same way they connect to terrestrial cell towers.

The MNO partnerships are the commercial anchor: AT&T, Verizon, Vodafone, TELUS, and Saudi Telecom have signed on. These MNOs effectively white-label AST's satellite coverage as an extension of their own networks, particularly in rural and coverage-gap areas.

The $1B+ revenue backlog is the financial embodiment of these partnerships. The 2025 full-year revenue was $70.9M — the path to $150-200M in 2026 is aggressive but reflects the manufacturing and deployment ramp underway.

Key Risks

  1. BB7 launch (~April 19) — binary (HIGH): This is the immediate near-term binary. Success unlocks the path to commercial service; failure or delay adds to the cumulative launch risk that has plagued AST's timeline repeatedly.
  2. Amazon/Globalstar competition (HIGH): The $11.6B Amazon/Globalstar deal is a new and serious competitive threat. FCC has flagged the "three-way race" dynamic. AST's first-mover advantage is not as large as previously assumed.
  3. Manufacturing ramp (MEDIUM-HIGH): 45-60 satellites by end-2026 is an aggressive target. Manufacturing at this scale has not been proven.
  4. Capital needs (MEDIUM): The company is pre-profit and needs continued capital deployment. Further equity dilution is likely if revenue growth lags.
  5. Launch cadence (MEDIUM): New Glenn is a new rocket with limited flight history. Any launch failure or delay cascades into the deployment timeline.
