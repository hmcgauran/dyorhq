Business Model

Eli Lilly discovers, develops, and commercialises pharmaceutical products across several key therapeutic areas:

  • GLP-1 Franchise (Mounjaro / Zepbound / Foundayo): The crown jewel. Tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist, marketed as Mounjaro for diabetes and Zepbound for obesity) delivered over $36 billion in combined sales in 2025. The April 2026 FDA approval of oral orforglipron (branded Foundayo) adds a convenience layer to the obesity portfolio, competing directly with Novo Nordisk's oral Wegovy. Retatrutide, a triple-agonist obesity candidate, demonstrated up to 24 per cent body weight reduction in Phase 3 — reinforcing Lilly's pipeline superiority.
  • Oncology: A growing portfolio including Verzenio (breast cancer), Jaypirca (leukaemia/lymphoma), and earlier-stage assets. A key earnings growth driver independent of GLP-1s.
  • Immunology: IL-17 and IL-23 inhibitors (Mirikizumab, Omvoh) competing in psoriasis and Crohn's disease, with potential for market share gains.
  • Neuroscience: Donanemab under FDA review for Alzheimer's disease; represents meaningful future optionality if approved and launched successfully.
  • Diabetes (beyond GLP-1): Trulicity (dulaglutide), Humalog (insulin), and legacy products provide a stable revenue base even as GLP-1s capture incremental share within the category.

Lilly's model combines an exceptional internal discovery engine with aggressive lifecycle management and an unprecedented $10 billion+ manufacturing expansion programme — the most ambitious capacity build in the company's history.

Key Risks

1GLP-1 safety signals: Long-term safety data for tirzepatide in broad obesity populations is still accumulating. Rare but serious adverse events (pancreatitis, thyroid cancer signals, GI risks) could trigger black box warnings or regulatory restrictions that impair the commercial trajectory. The Foundayo preprint on lean-mass loss is a early signal of potential efficacy differentiation risk.
2Competitive GLP-1 entrants: Novo Nordisk's oral Wegovy (launched December 2025), Amgen's MariTide with durable weight loss and less frequent dosing, Pfizer's oral GLP-1, and Viking Therapeutics' dual agonist are all advancing rapidly. A superior or cheaper competitor could structurally impair Lilly's market share and pricing power.
3Payer reimbursement headwinds: The cost of GLP-1 medications to health systems and insurers is substantial. Medicare drug price negotiation under the Inflation Reduction Act is a genuine risk. Pushback from pharmacy benefit managers and international health systems on coverage criteria and pricing could significantly limit the addressable market, particularly for the obesity indication.
4Manufacturing execution risk: Lilly's $10 billion+ manufacturing investment is unprecedented. Construction delays, regulatory approval of new facilities, and quality control issues at new plants represent meaningful execution risk that could constrain supply and hand market share to Novo Nordisk at a critical moment in the competitive cycle.
5Valuation embeddedness: The P/E of approximately 40x prices in near-perfect execution. Any quarter of disappointing revenue, a clinical failure, or safety signal could trigger a sharp de-rating given how much positive news is already embedded in the current price. The 40 per cent drawdown from 52-week highs reflects the market's sensitivity to competitive and clinical newsflow.
