Executive Summary

Business Model

Dell operates across two primary business segments following the VMware spin-off: Infrastructure Solutions Group (ISG): Servers, storage, networking, and data-centre infrastructure. This is the highest-margin and highest-growth segment, particularly driven by AI-optimised server demand (PowerEdge with Nvidia GPU integrations) and enterprise storage arrays. This is the most important segment for the investment thesis. Client Solutions Group (CSG): Commercial and consumer PCs, laptops, and peripherals. A mature, cyclical business with commodity pricing dynamics. Commercial PCs (vs consumer) offer better margin and contract stability through Dell's relationships with large enterprise IT departments. Connected Base Services: After-market services, warranties, and deployment/installation services that provide recurring, high-margin revenue tied to the installed hardware base. The Dell investment case rests heavily on ISG's AI server momentum and whether the company can sustain margin recovery in a competitive infrastructure market against pure-play competitors like HPE and hyperscaler custom silicon.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case 72 / 100 AI server orders continue to accelerate as enterprises beyond hyperscalers (sovereign AI, enterprise on-premise AI) build out inference and training infrastructure. ISG revenue grows 15–20 per cent in fiscal 2027 and margins expand to 18–20 per cent. The P/E re-rates to 25×+ as the AI infrastructure narrative gains further traction. Weight: 30% Base Case 58 / 100 AI server momentum continues at a steady pace but not at the explosive rate currently implied by the backlog. ISG grows at high-single to low-double digits. CSG stabilises after the Windows 10 refresh tailwind fades. EPS grows at 8–12 per cent annually. Shares drift towards $190–210 as fundamentals improve gradually. Weight: 50% Bear Case 40 / 100 AI infrastructure spending slows as enterprises and hyperscalers absorb existing GPU capacity. Custom silicon (Nvidia Grace, Google TPU, Amazon Trainium) increasingly replaces commodity x86 servers in AI workloads. CSG revenue declines as the commercial PC refresh cycle completes.
