Executive Summary

Deere is the world's dominant agricultural and construction equipment manufacturer, benefiting from precision farming technology adoption and infrastructure spending; the stock's valuation near 52-week highs reflects this, leaving limited near-term upside; HOLD at $605.00.

Business Model

Deere operates two core divisions with distinct end markets. Key revenue streams: Production & Precision Ag: Tractors, combines, sprayers, and seeding equipment; the world's largest producer. Revenue driven by farm income, commodity prices, and fleet replacement cycles. Small Agriculture & Turf: Compact tractors and grounds maintenance equipment for residential, commercial, and municipal use. Construction & Forestry: Excavators, bulldozers, loaders, and forestry equipment; sales track infrastructure spending and housing activity. Financial Services: Captive financing arm providing loans for equipment purchases; generates ~15% of group net income and stabilises group cash flows. Deere's moat is its combination of brand, distribution network, and — increasingly — proprietary precision agriculture technology (SeeScan, ActiveVision) that creates recurring software and service revenue from an installed base of equipment.

Key Risks

  • 1Commodity price sensitivity: Farm equipment purchases are directly driven by farm income, which is a function of commodity prices; a corn price collapse is the single largest demand risk.
  • 2Interest rate exposure: Both customers' equipment financing costs and DE's own Financial Services business are sensitive to the rate environment; a rate-cutting cycle creates NIM pressure on captive finance.
  • 3China agricultural demand: Chinese soybean and corn purchasing decisions directly affect global commodity prices and thus Deere's primary end market.
  • 4Supply chain and tariffs: Steel input costs, component availability, and tariff exposure (particularly on imports from Mexico and China) remain ongoing margin risks.
  • 5Labour disputes: UAW strikes (2023 demonstrated) can halt production at major US manufacturing facilities; costs and reputational damage are significant.
