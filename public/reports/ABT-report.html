Executive Summary

Abbott's diversified healthcare franchise — spanning diagnostics, medical devices, nutrition and established pharmaceuticals — is well-positioned to deliver mid-to-high single-digit EPS growth, but the shares are fully valued at the top of their 52-week range — HOLD is the weighted recommendation.

Business Model

Abbott operates across four principal divisions. Medical Devices covers cardiovascular stents, rhythm management, neuromodulation and Alinity diagnostics platforms. Diagnostics — a legacy COVID-19 strength — includes point-of-care and laboratory-based testing. Nutrition (Similac, Ensure, Pedialyte) serves maternal, infant and adult nutritional needs globally. Established Pharmaceuticals sells generic branded medicines in emerging markets. The diversity of revenue streams is Abbott's structural advantage: no single product or therapy represents an existential dependency. The FreeStyle Libre continuous glucose monitor is the primary growth accelerant within Medical Devices, having displaced traditional fingerstick monitoring in type 2 diabetes management across major markets. Abbott's emerging-market exposure (Established Pharmaceuticals and Nutrition) provides long-term demographic tailwinds, though near-term currency and macroeconomic volatility are headwinds.

Recent Catalysts

Abbott has continued to expand Libre's indication set, most recently securing additional type 1 diabetes approvals in key European markets and advancing its bidirectional glucose-insulin integration device programme. The AVEIR dual-chamber leadless pacemaker has gained regulatory approvals and is ramping commercially. In nutrition, US market share stabilised following supply disruptions in 2022–2023. The company's 2025 full-year guidance pointed to high-single-digit EPS growth, supported by margin expansion in Medical Devices.

Key Risks

  • 1FreeStyle Libre competitive intensity: Dexcom's G7 and next-generation platforms are competitive. Pricing pressure or market share loss in Abbott's most important growth product would be material.
  • 2Elective procedure volumes: Cardiovascular implant procedures are sensitive to healthcare system capacity, reimbursement rates and patient co-pays. A recession-driven reduction in non-urgent procedures would hit device revenues.
  • 3Currency and emerging market risk: Approximately 40% of revenues are international. Emerging market currency depreciation, economic instability or geopolitical disruption (Latin America, Africa, Asia) could suppress reported growth.
  • 4COVID-19 test revenue normalisation: Although largely normalized, residual rapid-test inventory builds in prior years created tough comps; the base effect can produce misleading near-term reported growth figures.
  • 5Supply chain and manufacturing concentration: Abbott's manufacturing footprint is concentrated in the US, Ireland and certain emerging market facilities; disruption at key sites could affect multiple product lines simultaneously.
