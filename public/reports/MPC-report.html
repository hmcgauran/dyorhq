Executive Summary

Marathon Petroleum is the largest US refiner by throughput and a dominant player in Midwest and Gulf Coast refining, generating significant free cash flow at current crude prices — the shares offer a reasonable entry point at 16.9x trailing earnings with a ~2% dividend yield and aggressive buybacks, but the bull case is constrained by structural EV-driven petrol demand erosion over the medium term, making HOLD the appropriate stance.

Business Model

Marathon Petroleum is the largest US refiner by crude processing capacity, operating 13 refineries with a combined capacity of approximately 3.3 million barrels per day. The asset base is heavily concentrated in the US Midwest and Gulf Coast — the mostsignificant crude oil production regions in North America — giving Marathon a significant feedstock cost advantage relative to coastal competitors. The core model is crude oil refining into petrol, diesel, jet fuel, and asphalt. The midcontinent refining system is advantaged by its proximity to Bakken and Permian Basin crude production, enabling the company to purchase discounted domestic crude and sell refined products at regional crack spreads that can be structurally superior to coastal spreads when logistics are tight. The Midstream segment operatespipelines, terminals, and gathering systems through a combination of wholly-owned assets and the Marathon Petroleum Corp LP (MPC LP) master limited partnership. The Speedway retail network (approximately 3,200 convenience stores) was sold to 7-Eleven in 2022 for $22 billion — this disposal significantly strengthened the balance sheet and provided capital for buybacks and debt reduction. Capital allocation has been shareholder-friendly: following the Speedway sale, MPC has been aggressively buying back shares and maintaining a base dividend with supplementary quarterly dividends tied to performance. At current crude prices, the business generates very substantial free cash flow.

Recent Catalysts

Crude oil prices in the $65-$80 range have been broadly supportive for refinery economics — Marathon's Midwest assets have benefited from discounted Bakken crude and strong regional diesel/jet fuel crack spreads, particularly given logistics constraints that keep Midcontinent products firm relative to imports. Aggressive buyback programme: MPC has repurchased approximately $5 billion in shares in 2024-2025, representing over 7% of shares outstanding. At current prices, this is accretive and mathematically improving per-share metrics. Trump administration energy policy has broadly supported domestic refining, with tariff discussions on imported petroleum products providing a modestly constructive backdrop for crack spreads on domestically advantaged assets. Marathon's Permian Basin joint venture with Lindsay Corporation and select midstream acquisitions have been announced in 2025, expanding the midstream business — this diversifies away from pure refining and potentially provides higher-multiple earnings. Dividend of approximately $4.40/share plus supplementary quarterly payments represents a total dividend yield of approximately 2% at current prices. The balance sheet is strong post-Speedway sale, with leverage at low single digits.

Key Risks

Refining margin cyclicality: The most important near-term risk. US refinery crack spreads are highly cyclical and sensitive to global supply-demand balances, imports, and inventory cycles. The extraor

  • 1Refining margin cyclicality: The most important near-term risk. US refinery crack spreads are highly cyclical and sensitive to global supply-demand balances, imports, and inventory cycles. The extraordinary margins of 2022-2023 have normalised and any return to those levels requires genuine supply disruptions or demand surprises.
  • 2Medium-term petrol demand destruction from EV penetration: Electric vehicles represent a structural headwind for petrol demand. Marathon's Midwest assets face lower immediate EV penetration risk than coastal California assets, but over a 5-10 year horizon this is a real headwind. Marathon's large asset base is less flexible than some competitors in switching to renewable diesel or sustainable aviation fuel.
  • 3Crude oil price volatility: Marathon's assets are configured for specific crude slates (Bakken, Permian, Canadian heavy). A sudden shift in crude price dynamics or logistics can change feedstock economics rapidly.
  • 4Larger asset base limits optionality: With 3.3 million bpd of refining capacity, Marathon has limited ability to meaningfully curtail operations in a downturn without taking significant writedowns. This makes the company more exposed to trough-margin environments than smaller, more flexible regional refiners.
  • 5Geopolitical tariff risk: Any significant disruption to North American trade flows from tariff escalation could create crack spread volatility — particularly in the Midwest where pipeline connectivity to the USGC can constrain the ability to redirect product flows.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Value-oriented energy investors who want the largest US refiner with a strong buyback track record; those seeking commodity-linked total return in a crude $65-$80 environment; investors who believe current crack spreads are sustainable and want to capture the high free cash flow generation. Cyclical investors who want to hold through the energy cycle. Avoiders: Growth investors; those concerned about long-term petrol demand destruction; investors who want exposure to the energy transition; anyone unwilling to hold through refining margin cyclicality. ESG-focused investors who want no direct exposure to coal, oil sands, or conventional refinery assets.

Recommendation

HOLD — Conviction Score: 62/100 Marathon Petroleum is the highest-quality pure-play US refiner, with the largest asset base, strong free cash flow generation, and a shareholder-friendly capital allocation policy. The 16.9x trailing P/E on $13.21 EPS is reasonable for a business generating that level of earnings power. The buyback programme is mathematically accretive at current prices. However, with no structural growth driver and the specter of medium-term petrol demand destruction from EV penetration, the upside case is capped. HOLD is the appropriate recommendation. Entry framework: BUY below $170 (approximately 12-13x normalised EPS), which represents genuine undervaluation relative to replacement cost of the asset base. BUY on a sustained break above $250, which would signal crack spread re-acceleration and momentum. Base-case price target $240 over 12 months assuming $13 EPS and a 17-18x multiple — modest upside from $222. Stop-loss: A sustained break below $165 would suggest crack spread deterioration beyond what is priced in and would be a signal to reassess the investment thesis. This is the level at which the dividend yield becomes the primary attraction.
