Key Risks

  1. EV transformation cost drag: GM is investing $35bn+ in EVs through 2025. Ultium battery platform losses, charging infrastructure and EV manufacturing ramp create persistent margin headwinds.
  2. Full-size truck pricing cyclicality: GM's most profitable segment is sensitive to economic cycles, competitor discounting behaviour and commodity cost inflation.
  3. UAW labour cost headwinds: The 2023 UAW strike resulted in significant wage increases and restored defined-benefit pensions. Labour costs are structurally higher than peers.
  4. Chinese EV import risk: Chinese EV manufacturers (BYD, NIO, XPeng) could pursue US market entry, creating a competitive shock in the mass-market EV segment.
  5. Cruise robotaxi commercial failure: The 2023 pedestrian incident accelerated regulatory scrutiny. Restarting commercial service is taking longer and costing more than initially modelled.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Contrarian value investors who believe in the ICE truck cash flow underwriting the EV transformation. Not suitable as a pure EV or growth story.

Avoiders: ESG-focused investors seeking clean transportation pure-plays. Investors seeking consistent earnings growth without the structural transformation risk. Tesla-focused investors will be disappointed.
