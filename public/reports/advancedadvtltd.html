Executive Summary

AdvancedAdvT Limited is a sub-£60M market cap AIM-listed software company operating in the digital transformation and membership management space. The FY26 trading update (year ending 28 February 2026) confirmed revenue of approximately £53 million and adjusted EBITDA of not less than £14.4 million — ahead of market consensus. Adjusted EBITDA margins exceeded 27%, underpinned by approximately 80% recurring revenue. A share buyback programme launched in March 2026 signals board confidence, with nearly two million shares now held in treasury. The company remains pre-profitable on a reported EPS basis (-0.08) and carries the governance and liquidity constraints typical of the micro-cap AIM peer group. At 165p with improving margins and an active buyback, the risk-reward is acceptable in the SPECULATIVE BUY range for investors with high risk tolerance and a multi-year horizon.

Business Model

AdvancedAdvT provides digital transformation software solutions with a focus on membership management, subscription billing, and customer engagement tools for B2B clients in the UK and Europe. Revenue is derived from SaaS subscriptions, implementation fees, and transaction processing. Key customers span UK local government, membership organisations, and not-for-profit sectors.

The subscription model provides predictable recurring revenue, while implementation fees add near-term cash generation. The FY26 update confirmed approximately 80% recurring revenue as a proportion of total revenues — a composition that provides meaningful revenue visibility. The shift in revenue mix toward SaaS subscriptions is the primary margin leverage driver as cohort-based retention improves and customer LTV rises. The company serves a niche but defensible position in the membership management stack, though competitive pressure from larger SaaS platforms is a standing risk. Analyst coverage is limited and the stock has low visibility — a characteristic that keeps the discount to intrinsic value wider than it would be for a larger-cap peer.

The buyback signals board-level confidence in intrinsic value and is directly accretive to remaining shareholders. Sustained purchase activity reduces the free-float voting pool, which could provide ongoing price support.

Adjusted EBITDA margins exceeded 27%, underpinned by approximately 80% recurring revenue. This is a materially stronger set of numbers than the current report had embedded, with recurring revenue growth and margin expansion occurring simultaneously — uncommon at this stage in a small-cap SaaS evolution and the core bull case catalyst.

Key Risks

  1. AIM liquidity constraints: At approximately £55M market cap, daily traded volume is insufficient for larger institutional positions without material price impact. This is a structural discount factor for any public-market investor.
  2. Limited broker coverage and low visibility: Minimal sell-side research coverage limits price discovery and investor awareness. The stock trades on thin information flow — a characteristic that creates opportunity but also risk of mispricing in either direction.
  3. Pre-profitability on reported basis: Despite positive adjusted EBITDA (£14.4M), reported EPS is negative (-0.08). Investors requiring earnings visibility through a traditional P/E lens will find this unsuitable.
  4. Small-cap governance standards: AIM listing entails reduced disclosure and governance requirements relative to the MAIN Market, increasing information asymmetry for investors.
  5. Customer concentration in UK public sector: Significant revenue exposure to local government and public sector budgets creates cyclical vulnerability to spending cuts, delayed payment cycles, and procurement delays.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It
