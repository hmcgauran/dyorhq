Executive Summary

Business Model

Home Depot operates the world's largest home improvement retail chain, serving three primary customer types: Do-It-Yourself (DIY) Consumers: Individual homeowners purchasing tools, materials, and equipment for personal home improvement projects. This is the historical core customer base, though as the housing stock ages and DIY skills decline in younger generations, this segment is evolving. Pro Customers: Professional contractors, tradespeople, and construction companies purchasing building materials, tools, and supplies at scale. Home Depot has invested heavily in Pro-focused services (HD Pro Checkout, dedicated sales teams, inventory management tools) to capture this high-volume, relationship-driven segment. Multi-Family Housing / Rental: Property managers and landlords maintaining rental units. This is a growing segment as institutional ownership of single-family rental properties expands. Home Depot's competitive moat rests on scale advantages in procurement, an unmatched product range, and the density of its store network. The Pro customer segment is the highest-priority growth area — Pro customers spend significantly more per transaction and have higher retention rates than DIY customers.
