Executive Summary

VEQT is a passive, all-equity index fund-of-funds ETF that holds a diversified portfolio of Vanguard's underlying regional equity ETFs — covering Canada, the US, Europe, Asia, and emerging markets. It offers 100% equity exposure with 13,000+ stocks globally, at a confirmed 0.00% management expense ratio (MER). There is no investment thesis here beyond the long-term equity risk premium: buy global equities cheaply, hold forever, benefit

Business Model

VEQT is a fund-of-funds ETF. It does not hold individual stocks — it holds other Vanguard ETFs, which in turn hold individual securities. The portfolio is weighted approximately: ~45% North America — VCN (Canada), VSP (US), VUN (US), VGG (US growth) ~25% Europe — VEF (Europe), VDU (UK), VIU (Europe ex-UK) ~15% Asia Pacific — VRE (Real estate), VA (Australia), VEE (Emerging markets Asia) ~15% Emerging Markets — VEF, VEE, VWO-weighted exposure The fund rebalances quarterly. It holds 13,000+ individual stocks across 40+ countries. It is a complete, self-contained global equity portfolio in a single ticker. The 0.00% MER is the headline — it is effectively free to hold from a fee perspective. Vanguard achieves this partly through fee waiver programmes that may have an end date; investors should confirm the current fee structure is still honoured. There is no active management, no stock selection, no market timing. The fund owns the market. That is the entire proposition.
