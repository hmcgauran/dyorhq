Executive Summary

Mkango is a Canadian-listed rare earths and recycling company with two core assets: the Songwe Hill rare earths project in Malawi and the Akokoma magnet recycling joint venture in Poland. The investment thesis rests on the secular demand growth for permanent magnets used in EV motors and wind turbines, combined with Mkango's position as one of the few potential near-term producers of separated magnet rare earth oxides outside China. Business Model

Mkango operates across two platforms: (1) the Songwe Hill rare earths project in Malawi — a carbonatite deposit with high-grade rare earth mineralisation amenable to open-pit mining, currently in Bankable Feasibility Study stage; and (2) the Akokoma joint venture in Poland, focused on hydrometallurgical processing ofNdFeB (neodymium-iron-boron) magnet scrap into separated rare earth oxides for the European EV and wind turbine supply chains. The strategic logic is compelling: Songwe provides the rare earth feedstock, Akokoma provides the downstream processing capability, and together they create a non-China rare earths supply chain for European strategic materials buyers. EU critical raw materials policy and UK critical minerals strategies are explicitly designed to support exactly this type of asset. The question is whether Mkango can access the capital to develop them on schedule.

Recent Catalysts

Akokoma magnet recycling JV: The Poland-based rare earth magnet recycling joint venture is the more near-term commercial opportunity. Akokoma processes end-of-life magnets and magnet production scrap into separated NdPr oxide — the key inputs for EV motor magnets. The EU's critical raw materials act creates demand for exactly this type of European processing capacity. Bankable Feasibility Study for Songwe: The BFS is the key technical de-risking document that would support project financing. Completion and publication of a positive BFS is a major catalyst. Rare earths market recovery: The NdPr oxide price cycle that crashed in 2022–2023 has partially recovered. Sustained higher prices make Songwe's development economics more attractive and reduce financing risk. Strategic customer and offtake agreements: Any announcement of a binding offtake or strategic investment from a European automaker, wind turbine OEM, or specialty materials group would be a major re-rating event. UK and EU critical materials policy: Both the UK's Critical Minerals Strategy and the EU's Critical Raw Materials Act create policy tailwinds for non-China rare earth supply chains. Mkango's geographic positioning (Malawi mine, Polish recycling) fits both frameworks.
