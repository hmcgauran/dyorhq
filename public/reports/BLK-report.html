Executive Summary

BlackRock's unassailable position as the world's largest asset manager — with structural tailwinds from ETF secularisation and growing Aladdin platform revenues — is reflected in a 29x P/E, leaving limited near-term upside but a durable long-term compounding franchise — HOLD is the weighted recommendation.

Business Model

BlackRock manages $10.5 trillion+ in assets under management (AUM) across equities, fixed income, multi-asset, alternatives and cash. The business model generates revenues from advisory fees (basis points on AUM), securities lending, and the Aladdin risk-management technology platform, which is licensed to institutional investors and other asset managers globally. The secular shift from active to passive (index and ETF) investing has been a structural tailwind for BlackRock, as it is the dominant global ETF provider (iShares franchise). iShares alone accounts for over $3 trillion in AUM and generates highly predictable fee revenues. The Aladdin platform is a high-margin, recurring-revenue technology business that represents BlackRock's answer to the question of how it monetises its institutional relationships beyond AUM fees. The firm has also expanded into private markets (infrastructure, private credit, real assets) to diversify revenue streams as passive fee compression intensifies.

Recent Catalysts

BlackRock's 2024 full-year results showed AUM reaching record levels, driven by strong ETF inflows and market appreciation. The acquisition of Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP) for $12.5 billion added significant private markets capabilities, particularly in infrastructure. The First Republic FDIC-assisted transaction and BAI (Barclays Africa) partial stake have been successfully integrated. Aladdin revenues have grown double digits as more institutions adopt the platform for portfolio risk management. However, the stock has pulled back from 52-week highs as markets have questioned whether the elevated fee-income cycle has peaked alongside equity markets.

Key Risks

  • 1Market depreciation AUM risk: BlackRock's earnings are highly correlated with equity market levels. A sustained 20%+ market correction would reduce AUM, fee revenues and EPS simultaneously, and could trigger redemption spirals in active funds.
  • 2Passive fee compression: While ETF market share continues to grow, fee compression is a secular headwind. As average ETF fees drift toward zero (or negative in some European markets), BlackRock's revenue per dollar of AUM declines even if AUM grows.
  • 3Regulatory risk in index licensing: Regulators in the EU, UK and increasingly the US have scrutinised index provider practices and potential conflicts of interest. Structural remedies could affect iShares' European market share.
  • 4Integration execution risk on GIP: The $12.5 billion GIP acquisition adds meaningful private markets scale but integration complexity and infrastructure market cycle risk could produce disappointing returns on the investment.
  • 5Alternatives competition: The private markets space is crowded with well-capitalised competitors (Apollo, Blackstone, KKR). Generating differentiated returns in infrastructure and private credit at scale is increasingly challenging.
