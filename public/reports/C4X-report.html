Executive Summary

Business Model

C4X Discovery operates a drug discovery and early development model. Its core asset is the 4D chematics computational platform — a molecular modelling technology that simulates drug-target interactions across four dimensions (structure, dynamics, energetics, and solvation), enabling the identification of novel compounds with higher precision than conventional high-throughput screening. The platform's value lies in de-risking early-stage discovery and attracting licensing deals with larger pharmaceutical companies. The most significant corporate event was the out-licensing of the Nrf2 activator programme to AstraZeneca for the treatment of COPD and related inflammatory respiratory diseases. This deal provides C4X with upfront and milestone payments, plus tiered royalties on commercial sales — a classic biotech licensing model. The Nrf2-KEAP1 pathway is biologically validated (NRF2 activation is protective against oxidative stress and inflammation), but AstraZeneca's development of the licensed programme is entirely outside C4X's control. The company also maintains a proprietary pipeline including early-stage programmes targeting the NRF2-KEAP1 pathway in neurodegeneration (Parkinson's) and other inflammatory indications. These are pre-clinical and will require either significant cash investment or additional out-licensing to progress. Revenues, if any, are entirely dependent on milestone payments from existing or future licensing agreements. There is no marketed product and no near-term commercial revenue. The burn rate relative to cash resources is the primary financial constraint.
