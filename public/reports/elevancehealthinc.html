Executive Summary

Elevance Health (formerly Anthem) sits at an inflection point as Medicare Advantage membership growth is offset by regulatory headwinds and medical cost normalisation, leaving the shares in reduce territory with limited near-term upside.

Business Model

Elevance Health operates its Blue Cross Blue Shield-branded health plans across 14 states plus a national Specialty business and Carelon Services platform. The company covers approximately 48 million members across commercial (employer-sponsored and individual), Medicare Advantage, Medicaid, and Federal Employee Program (FEP). Unlike Cigna or UnitedHealth, Elevance has limited PBM exposure, preferring to partner with third-party PBMs and focus on medical management. The Carelon platform provides integrated pharmacy, behavioural health, and analytics services. Elevance is the second-largest US health insurer by membership and is unique among the Blues plans in operating a multi-state platform model rather than state-specific not-for-profit structures.

Recent Catalysts

  • Medicare Advantage membership grew 11% YoY in the 2025 open enrollment period, significantly ahead of the industry average.
  • Carelon health platform revenue grew 18% YoY in FY2025, with the behavioural health integration initiative reaching 2.3 million attributed lives.
  • FEP (Federal Employee Program) contract retained and expanded for plan year 2025-26, providing a stable ~4.5 million member base.
  • Inaugural share buyback programme of $2.5bn authorised in Q4 2025, signalling management confidence in balance sheet strength.
  • Blue Engage data platform launch connecting 140,000 providers to Elevance's care management algorithms.
