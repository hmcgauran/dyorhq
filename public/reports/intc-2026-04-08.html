Executive Summary

Intel is a US semiconductor company in structural decline. Its manufacturing competitiveness has eroded versus TSMC, its data centre CPU market share is being lost to AMD, and its foundry ambitions (Intel Foundry Services) require multi-year capital investment that is diluting shareholders without yet generating meaningful revenue. The company is burning cash and has suspended its dividend.

The bull case — IFS becoming a credible alternative to TSMC for US-based chip manufacturing — has a positive NPV but the timeline is uncertain and the capital requirements are enormous. This is a restructuring story, not a growth story.

Bottom line: SELL. Intel is burning cash, losing market share, and has a dividend cut. The turnaround story is real but distant and uncertain. There are better places to allocate capital in semiconductors. Conviction: 28/100.

Business Model

Intel designs and manufactures CPUs, GPUs, and FPGA chips. Its Client Computing business (laptop and desktop CPUs) is the primary revenue source. The Data Centre and AI business competes with AMD EPYC and NVIDIA GPUs. Intel Foundry Services (IFS) is the company's attempt to become a contract chip manufacturer like TSMC — a multi-decade ambition that requires $100B+ in capex.
