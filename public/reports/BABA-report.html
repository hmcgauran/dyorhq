Executive Summary

Alibaba is a high-quality e-commerce and cloud infrastructure company that has been systematically mispriced by Western markets due to geopolitical risk premium, regulatory overhang from the PRC's tech crackdowns, and US-traded ADR uncertainty. Business Model

Alibaba operates three core businesses: Taobao/Tmall e-commerce platforms (the dominant China e-commerce marketplace), Alibaba Cloud (the leading cloud infrastructure provider in China, #2 globally), and Cainiao logistics (supply chain and logistics infrastructure). International commerce (AliExpress, Lazada, Trendyol) is a growing but loss-making growth vector.The e-commerce moat is built on network effects: merchants attract buyers, buyers attract merchants, and the resulting data advantage allows personalised recommendations and advertising that compounds the flywheel. Alibaba Cloud is the most credible domestic alternative to foreign cloud providers in China and is expanding internationally.

Key Risks

  • 1PRC regulatory interference (HIGH): The PRC government has demonstrated willingness to fundamentally reshape the technology sector through regulation. Alibaba's dominant market position makes it a natural target for antitrust action. The $2.8B antitrust fine from 2021 is the precedent; a repeat or escalation is possible.
  • 2US ADR delisting risk (HIGH): The Holding Foreign Companies Accountable Act creates a real risk of US-traded ADRs being delisted. The ADR-to-Hong Kong conversion process is complex and not guaranteed to protect all shareholders equally.
  • 3Chinese consumer demand weakness (MEDIUM): Consumer confidence in China is at multi-decade lows. A sustained recovery is essential for Alibaba's core e-commerce business to stabilise. Without it, cloud growth alone may not be sufficient to drive the bull case.
