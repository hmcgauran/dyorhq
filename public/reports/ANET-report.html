Executive Summary

Arista is one of the cleanest infrastructure beneficiaries of the AI datacentre build-out. Its high-performance cloud networking kit sits directly in the path of hyperscaler capex, and its software-led switching architecture has allowed it to take share from legacy vendors for years. At $145.26, just 0.6% below the 52-week high, the business quality is obvious but so is the valuation.The stock is not cheap at 51.6x trailing earnings, but the market is paying for premium execution, hyperscaler ex

Business Model

Arista sells high-speed cloud networking equipment and network operating software to hyperscalers, cloud providers, and large enterprises. The core moat is EOS, its software layer, combined with strong positioning in high-bandwidth switching for AI clusters and modern datacentres.The business is tied to datacentre architecture spending rather than end-user hardware cycles. That gives it direct leverage to AI infrastructure deployment, but also creates customer concentration risk because a handful of hyperscalers drive a large share of revenue.
