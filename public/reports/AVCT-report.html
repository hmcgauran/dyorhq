Business Model

Avacta's value is entirely in its pre|CISION platform and the clinical pipeline it generates. pre|CISION activates drugs specifically in the tumour microenvironment using Fibroblast Activation Protein (FAP), which is highly expressed in the stroma of most epithelial tumours. The FAP-activated prodrug approach is scientifically credible and has a mechanistic rationale that addresses a genuine clinical need — reducing the systemic toxicity of potent chemotherapies. FAP is expressed on cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs) across a broad range of solid tumours — not on the tumour cells themselves, but in the tumour stroma. This is the correct mechanistic interpretation: the pre|CISION platform targets the FAP enzymatic activity at the tumour-stroma interface. Primary literature supports FAP as a "promising pan-tumour target" with expression confirmed in multiple epithelial cancer types. [1,2] Exatecan — used as a pre|CISION payload in AVA6000's next generation — is a potent topoisomerase-1 (Topo-1) inhibitor with single-dose efficacy confirmed in primary literature. [3] The "most potent Topo-1 in clinical use" claim requires specific comparative data not yet confirmed in available literature; the general potency claim is well-supported. The commercial model will be either a partnership deal with a global pharmaceutical company (preferred, as it provides upfront cash and derisks development) or a US listing to access deeper capital markets. A NASDAQ dual listing or US partnership is the stated strategic direction and the £9.45M oversubscribed raise completed in early 2026 was structured to support this. The pipeline: farudodistat (AVA6000) is the most advanced pre|CISION asset — Phase I data expected H1 2026. AVA6200 (gemcitabine pre|CISION) is in IND/CTA-enabling studies targeting 2026 clinical entry. AVA7200 (immune checkpoint inhibitor pre|CISION) is in discovery. The business is essentially a platform validated by clinical data — each clinical readout de-risks or validates the platform thesis.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull case support (primary literature): FAP as pan-tumour target: FAP is confirmed expressed in the stroma of multiple epithelial cancer types. The platform's tumour-agnostic claim is mechanistically justified. [1,2] Cardiotoxicity TAM expansion: This is the strongest literature finding. DOX cardiotoxicity is mechanistically linked to FAP activation in cardiac fibroblasts — FAP PET signal is significantly elevated from 2 weeks post-DOX, versus 10 weeks for standard echocardiography detection. If pre|CISION reduces systemic DOX exposure, it could reduce this cardiotoxicity mechanism, enabling higher/frequent dosing. [4] Exatecan potency: A potent Topo-1 payload with confirmed single-agent efficacy and synergy with DDR inhibitors (ATR, PARP). [3] Bear case support (primary literature): FAP is a stromal, not tumour-cell target: The pre|CISION mechanism depends on stromal penetration and FAP activity at the tumour interface — this is scientifically sound but harder to optimise than direct tumour-cell targeting. Clinical validation is required. "Most potent Topo-1" claim: Specific comparative data (Exatecan vs Irinotecan/Topotecan in patients) not confirmed in available literature — this specific claim should be treated as unvalidated.
