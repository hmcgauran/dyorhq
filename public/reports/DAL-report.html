Executive Summary

Delta Air Lines offers an attractive combination of strong pricing power, disciplined capacity management, and an improving balance sheet, trading at a significant discount to intrinsic value on a P/E of under 10x, and is rated HOLD.

Business Model

Delta is the largest US airline by market capitalisation and a leading global carrier. Its revenue is derived from scheduled passenger service, cargo transportation, and ancillary services including loyalty programme monetisation. The business model has structurally shifted post-pandemic toward capacity discipline and premium revenue mix: Mainline Passenger Revenue~85% of total revenue; basic economy to premium delta one Cargo~3% of revenue; freight and mail Loyalty Programme (SkyMiles)~8% of revenue; co-brand credit card and retail partnerships Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul (MRO)~4% of revenue; delta techops serves third-party airlines Delta's competitive moat lies in its premium route network, superior operational reliability, and the SkyMiles loyalty programme, which is one of the most valuable co-brand relationships in US consumer finance. The MRO business (Delta TechOps) is a high-margin, underappreciated earnings contributor.
