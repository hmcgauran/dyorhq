Executive Summary

Thor Explorations Ltd is a West African gold producer operating the Sabiensola gold mine in Senegal, with development optionality at the Makabtoun and Kouloum projects. The company is currently loss-making at the operational level but benefits from exposure to a rising gold price environment and significant oxide ore reserve potential. At 81.5 GBX, the stock trades near the lower end of its 52-week range, providing an interesting entry point for investors with tolerance for commodity price risk and single-asset concentration. The near-term catalyst is the Phase 2 expansion study and a recovery in gold price sentiment. BUY on weakness. Conviction Score: 65/100 - BUY

Business Model

Thor Explorations Ltd is a Canadian-incorporated gold mining company focused on West Africa. The primary asset is the Sabiensola gold mine in Senegal, a producing operation that generates gold revenue from oxide ore processing via heap leaching. The company also holds early-stage exploration assets in Senegal and Burkina Faso, including the Makabtoun and Kouloum projects. Thor's business model is straightforward: extract and process oxide ore, sell gold into the spot market, and use proceeds to fund exploration and development. The key variables are gold price, ore grade, and recovery rates. With no hedging programme of note, Thor is essentially a leveraged play on the spot gold price. Geographic exposure is West Africa, a region with meaningful political and operational risk versus established mining jurisdictions.

Key Risks

  1. #1 Gold price sensitivity: Thor has minimal hedging exposure. A sustained fall in the gold price directly impairs revenue, cash flow, and the viability of the Phase 2 expansion -- the single largest risk to the investment case.
  2. #2 Single-asset concentration: Sabiensola is effectively the entire production base. Any operational disruption -- equipment failure, processing issues, weather, or regulatory stoppage -- removes all revenue. The company lacks the operational diversity of larger peers.
  3. #3 Political and regulatory risk in Senegal: West African mining jurisdictions carry meaningful political risk. Changes to royalty rates, environmental regulations, or the social licence to operate could materially affect economics.
  4. #4 Funding and dilution risk: With negative operating cash flow and no current profitability, Thor may need to access capital markets to fund Phase 2 development. An equity raise at distressed share prices would be significantly dilutive to existing holders.
  5. #5 Exploration stage assets carry no near-term value: The Makabtoun and Kouloum projects are early-stage and offer no near-term cash flow. Capital allocated to exploration is essentially a call option with a high probability of expiring worthless.
