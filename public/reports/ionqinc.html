Executive Summary

IonQ is a pure-play quantum computing company trading on the NYSE. The prior report (8 Apr 2026, conviction 47, REDUCE) was written when the stock was at $29.55 — a thoughtful assessment of an overvalued, pre-revenue story. That characterisation is now materially incomplete. The stock has surged over 20% in a single session to $43.25, driven by a photonic interconnect breakthrough, DARPA HARQ program selection, and sector-wide momentum from Nvidia's quantum announcements.

Business Model

IonQ builds trapped-ion quantum computing systems and sells access through cloud platforms (Amazon Braket, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud — the only quantum company on all three major clouds), direct enterprise engagements, and government contracts. The company is the only pure-play quantum computing stock in the US, which gives it a structural premium and scarcity value.

The photonic interconnect breakthrough announced in April 2026 demonstrates entanglement-based quantum networking — a significant technical milestone that extends IonQ's lead in networked quantum systems. The DARPA HARQ program selection is government validation of IonQ's technical approach.

The 256-qubit system pipeline for 2026 represents the next generation of hardware, targeting improved error rates and larger problem sets than current systems.

Key Risks

  1. Valuation is demanding (HIGH): At $43.25 with $235M midpoint 2026 guidance, IonQ trades at approximately 67x forward revenue. This leaves essentially no margin for error if revenue growth decelerates or a major enterprise contract falls through.
  2. Commercialisation risk (HIGH): $130M in revenue with 73-88% growth guidance is impressive, but quantum computing is still in its infancy. The jump from $130M to a billion-dollar revenue business is where the hard work happens.
  3. Quantum architecture competition (MEDIUM): IonQ's trapped-ion approach competes with superconducting (IBM, Google) and photonic (PsiQuantum) architectures. No guarantee trapped-ion wins the commercial race.
  4. Short squeeze reversal (MEDIUM): A significant portion of the recent +20% move is short-covering rather than new fundamental buying. If momentum stalls, the short squeeze can reverse violently.
  5. Highly speculative (MEDIUM): Quantum computing remains pre-profit and dependent on continued investor appetite for long-duration tech themes. Any rotation out of speculative tech would hit IonQ disproportionately.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Speculative investors who want pure-play quantum exposure with demonstrable revenue growth. The $3.3bn cash balance removes the immediate bankruptcy/dilution risk that typically makes pre-revenue quantum stocks uninvestable. The analyst PT of ~$67 represents genuine upside.

Should avoid: Risk-averse or valuation-sensitive investors. At 67x forward revenue, the stock requires everything to go right. Any revenue miss or quantum winter narrative would cause severe pain.
