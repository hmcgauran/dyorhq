Executive Summary

Bank of Ireland is another core domestic lender whose earnings are currently flattered by higher rates, but the longer-term investment case rests on deposit stickiness, credit discipline and the ability to return capital without inviting a harsher regulatory tone. The bank has benefited from consolidation in the Irish market, though investors still need to separate cyclical earnings strength from truly durable franchise improvement.

Business Model

The group serves retail, SME, corporate and wealth customers, with mortgages and deposits central to the model. Irish banking has become more concentrated, which helps pricing discipline, but it remains a politically visible sector and therefore a difficult one to rate as a carefree compounder. Like AIB, Bank of Ireland is judged on asset quality, funding profile and capital return credibility more than on ambitious growth narratives.
