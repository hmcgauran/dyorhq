Executive Summary

NuScale is one of the most recognisable listed names in advanced nuclear because it carries a real regulatory credential, the first U.S. NRC-approved SMR design. That matters. It gives the company a level of technical and policy credibility many earlier-stage peers do not have. But the equity is telling a harsher story. Business Model

NuScale is developing small modular reactor systems for utility, industrial and large-load customers. The commercial opportunity is substantial if customers adopt SMRs as part of future energy infrastructure. Revenue should ultimately come from design licensing, engineering services, project participation and long-cycle deployment support. The challenge is that nuclear businesses do not get paid for technical elegance alone. They need financeable projects, committed customers, manageable timelines and confidence that build economics will hold together under real-world conditions.
