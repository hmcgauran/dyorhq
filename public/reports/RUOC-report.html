Executive Summary

Ocean Power Technologies is a pre-revenue marine energy and autonomy company that has just started converting a record backlog into delivery. The stock has been obliterated — down ~61% from its 52-week high of €0.769 to €0.2976 — largely on cash burn fears and liquidity concerns. That selloff looks overdone relative to the actual contract trajectory. The company announced a record $19.9M backlog as of January 2026

Business Model

Ocean Power Technologies designs and deploys PowerBuoy® systems — autonomous offshore platforms that generate power from wave energy and can operate as persistent, unattended marine infrastructure. They also produce WAM-V (Wave Adaptive Modular Vessel) unmanned surface vehicles (USVs) for defence and research customers. Revenue streams: PowerBuoy systems — sold or leased to defence, security, and offshore energy customers WAM-V USVs — sold to defence/research clients (Nordic order March 2026) MERROWS® payload — persistent maritime surveillance payload integrated onto PowerBuoy (DHS/Anduril contract) Data/monitoring services — power + data-as-a-service from deployed buoys Economic moat: early mover in US government-approved wave energy; Anduril partnership provides credible defence prime integration; growing patent portfolio in marine autonomous power. The defence market for persistent offshore maritime awareness is genuine and growing (US Coast Guard, DHS, NATO allies). Key vulnerability: pre-revenue. No significant commercial revenue yet. Backlog conversion is everything.
