Executive Summary

Burford Capital is the world's largest litigation finance company, providing capital to law firms and corporate clients engaged in commercial legal disputes. It operates a portfolio of legal finance investments, earning management fees, performance fees, and returns on its own deployed capital. The business is structurally long legal market dysfunction and benefits from a growing pipeline of commercial disputes as litigation volumes and values increase globally.

The investment case rests on three pillars: Burford's dominant market position as the scale leader in a nascent industry, the high-return nature of litigation finance as an asset class, and the portfolio maturity inflection as older cases resolve. At 348.20 GBX, the stock trades at approximately 15x trailing EPS with a market capitalisation of roughly £760m - a meaningful discount to the estimated net asset value of its portfolio.

Bottom line: BUY (strong). Burford is a high-conviction position for investors willing to tolerate NAV opacity and case-level timing uncertainty in exchange for exposure to a structural-growth alternative asset business at a material NAV discount.

Business Model

Burford operates two distinct businesses within its capital-light / capital-recovery framework:

Capital segment: Burford deploys its own balance sheet capital into litigation investments, earning returns directly from case outcomes. This is the core portfolio business and the primary driver of NAV. Returns are asymmetric - cases either win and generate multiples on capital, or lose and write off the position - and timing is uncertain and lumpy.

Solutions segment (previously called "OCM"): Burford provides management services to external capital (including the Burford Capital Primary Fund and other vehicles), earning management fees and performance fees. This segment is growing and more recurring in nature, providing fee income that partially hedges portfolio volatility.

Revenue recognition is conservative: Burford only recognises gains from case outcomes when cash is received or reasonably assured. This means reported income understates the true economic earnings of maturing portfolios. Net assets are stated at fair value, but NAV methodology involves manager judgement on case-by-case probability-weighted outcomes, which creates legitimate opacity.

The investment thesis is that Burford's portfolio is maturing - a large number of older cases are in advanced resolution stages - which should drive significant NAV growth and realised gains over the next 18-36 months. This, combined with the Solutions segment's growing fee base, creates a compounding earnings trajectory that the current multiple does not reflect.

Recent Catalysts

  • H1 2025 results (Jul 2025): Burford reported strong portfolio performance with material realisations from older cases. Realised gains were ahead of expectations. NAV per share grew. Solutions segment revenue expanded meaningfully, demonstrating the capital-light model's scalability.
  • Portfolio maturity inflection: Management has consistently flagged that a large cohort of portfolio positions are in late-stage resolution. Several significant cases moved to conclusion in H2 2025 and into 2026, generating realisations that should accelerate reported earnings.
  • NAV discount narrowing: The stock has re-rated from its 2024 lows as investors have grown more comfortable with portfolio transparency initiatives. Burford has improved disclosure around portfolio composition and case-stage distribution, reducing the discount at which shares trade to reported NAV.
  • Solutions segment expansion: The fund management business is growing its external AUM, adding fee income that is less volatile than direct portfolio returns. Third-party capital now manages a meaningful portion of total capital under management, improving Burford's capital efficiency and fee revenue.
  • Market share position: Burford remains the clear market leader in commercial litigation finance, a category with high barriers to entry (reputation, track record, legal expertise, capital depth). No competitor has achieved comparable scale or diversification.

Key Risks

  1. Case-level outcome uncertainty (HIGH): Litigation finance is inherently binary at the individual case level. A small number of large positions can dominate portfolio returns. Adverse outcomes on key cases would be significant and would not be diversified away given the nature of the business.
  2. NAV opacity and discount (MEDIUM): Burford's NAV involves significant management judgement. The market historically applied a large discount to NAV, partly due to this opacity and partly due to the lumpiness of realisations. Improved disclosure has narrowed the discount, but it remains a structural risk.
  3. Timing uncertainty (MEDIUM): Litigation is slow and unpredictable. Case resolution timelines can extend well beyond initial expectations due to court scheduling, appeals, and procedural complexity. This creates earnings volatility that is difficult to forecast and that can frustrate shareholders expecting near-term catalysts.
  4. Concentration risk (MEDIUM): The portfolio is concentrated in a relatively small number of large positions. If several large cases resolve adversely simultaneously, the impact on reported earnings and NAV would be severe. Diversification across cases and jurisdictions provides some mitigation but not complete protection.
  5. Regulatory and reputational risk (LOW-MEDIUM): Litigation finance is subject to evolving regulatory scrutiny in key jurisdictions including the US and UK. Adverse regulation could restrict portfolio growth or increase costs. Reputational associations with plaintiff-side financing also carry political risk in certain contexts.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors with a genuine long-term horizon (3-5 years minimum) who understand and can tolerate the NAV opacity, timing uncertainty, and binary case-level outcomes inherent in litigation finance. Those who want differentiated exposure to an alternative asset class with structural growth characteristics. Investors who accept that the NAV discount represents opportunity rather than warning.

Should avoid: Short-term investors who need earnings visibility and regular news flow - Burford will disappoint on timing expectations repeatedly. Value investors looking for conventional metrics - P/E and EPS are distorted by conservative revenue recognition. Anyone uncomfortable with binary outcomes on concentrated positions. Investors who need liquidity from their portfolio holdings.

Recommendation

BUY (strong) at 348.20 GBX with 72/100 conviction. Burford Capital trades at a material discount to estimated NAV and benefits from a portfolio maturity inflection that should drive increasing realisations over the next 18-36 months. The Solutions segment provides growing fee income that partially offsets portfolio volatility. The market leadership position in litigation finance is durable and self-reinforcing.

The primary bull catalyst is a series of large case resolutions that demonstrate the portfolio's quality and force the market to close the NAV discount. This is not a question of if but when. The risk/reward at 348.20 GBX is attractive given the asymmetry between NAV backing and market price.
