Executive Summary

Coinbase is the dominant US cryptocurrency exchange by registered users and volume, operating a regulated platform for retail and institutional crypto trading, custody, and staking. At $178.90, the stock is 5.6% below its 52-week high of $189.00 — a modest pullback from Bitcoin ETF enthusiasm peaks. The investment case is straightforward: Coinbase is a leveraged play on crypto asset prices and trading volumes. When Bitcoin rises, Coinbase earnings and sentiment rise. When it falls, they fall. Business Model

Coinbase earns revenue from four streams: (1) retail trading fees (1.5–4% depending on transaction size, higher than most competitors), (2) institutional trading fees (lower per-transaction but higher volume), (3) subscription services including Coinbase One membership and staking, and (4) interest income on customer cash held on platform. The Bitcoin ETF custody business is the structural highlight: Coinbase earns recurring fees as the custodian for major institutional ETF products — this is institutional-quality, recurring revenue that is not directly tied to crypto market volatility. The competitive position is strong in the US (regulatory moat from SEC and state-by-state licensing), but internationally Binance, Kraken, and other global exchanges compete aggressively. The regulatory environment for crypto exchanges in the US remains uncertain — the SEC has pursued enforcement actions against Coinbase, and the broader regulatory framework for digital assets is not yet settled law.

Recent Catalysts

Bitcoin ETF custody revenues: Coinbase earns recurring custody fees as the institutional custodian for BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) and Fidelity's Bitcoin ETF products. These fees are recurring, institutional-quality revenue that does not depend on retail trading volumes. This is the most important structural development in Coinbase's financial model in years. Bitcoin price trajectory: Bitcoin's performance dominates Coinbase's earnings trajectory. At $178.90, Coinbase is effectively pricing in continued Bitcoin strength — a sustained correction would compress trading revenues and custody fee valuations simultaneously. SEC regulatory risk: The SEC has filed charges against Coinbase for operating as an unregistered securities exchange. A resolution of this case (either through settlement or litigation outcome) would remove a major regulatory overhang. Product expansion: Coinbase has been expanding its product suite to include more DeFi access, stablecoin infrastructure, and blockchain-based financial services. These are meaningful optionality bets but do not yet contribute materially to revenue.
