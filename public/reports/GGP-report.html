Business Model

Greatland Resources acquires, explores, and develops gold and base metal properties in Australia, primarily in the Pilbara craton — one of the world's most geologically prospective greenstone belt regions. The company's strategy is to make early-stage discoveries and bring them to a point where a major miner (like Newmont) will earn into or acquire the asset. This is a classic junior miner model: farm-in agreements transfer development capital burden to the major while Greatland retains exposure to upside. Revenue is entirely pre-production. The company is cash flow negative and will remain so until Blackhill or another asset reaches commercial production. The value driver is the valuation of in-ground resources relative to the cost of discovery and development — measured in ounces per share, not earnings multiples. The Newmont partnership is the central pillar. Newmont's ratification of the Blackhill mining lease is a signal that the geological work has cleared a major hurdle. Newmont's technical and financial backing substantially reduces the binary risk that typically kills micro-cap explorers.
