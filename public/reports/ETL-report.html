Business Model

Eutelsat is a satellite operator — it owns and operates a fleet of geostationary communications satellites providing video, data, and internet connectivity services to broadcast customers, telecom operators, and enterprise clients globally. Revenue streams: Video & Broadcast (~40%): Satellite capacity for TV broadcasting — the legacy cash cow. Under pressure as viewers migrate to streaming. Government & Enterprise (~35%): Secure communications, military/government contracts, maritime and aviation connectivity. Mobile Connectivity (~15%): Backhaul for mobile networks, aviation and maritime connectivity. OneWeb LEO (~10% and growing): Low Earth Orbit constellation for broadband — the strategic bet. Operational but pre-commercial scale. Economic moat: orbital slots are scarce resources — once Eutelsat holds a geostationary slot, competitors cannot easily replicate it. Long-term contracts with broadcast clients provide revenue visibility. The moat is eroding as LEO constellations (Starlink, OneWeb) offer lower-latency alternatives.

Key Risks

Debt burden and refinancing risk (HIGH): The €1.5B refinancing addressed near-term maturities but the debt load remains enormous for a declining-revenue business. If EBITDA erodes faster than modelled

  • 1Debt burden and refinancing risk (HIGH): The €1.5B refinancing addressed near-term maturities but the debt load remains enormous for a declining-revenue business. If EBITDA erodes faster than modelled, covenant breaches become a real possibility. This is the primary equity risk.
  • 2Revenue decline acceleration (HIGH): Video broadcast is structural decline, not cyclical. Every year, more viewers leave satellite for streaming. Eutelsat's addressable market is shrinking. The pace of decline will determine whether the debt trajectory is manageable.
  • 3OneWeb execution risk (MEDIUM–HIGH): OneWeb is the only meaningful growth lever. It is capital-intensive, pre-revenue at scale, and faces direct competition from Starlink. French export credit financing is helpful but does not guarantee commercial success.
  • 4Geopolitical risk (MEDIUM): The Express AT1/AT2 terminations (Russian sanctions) illustrate this. Eutelsat has exposure to markets with geopolitical tension. European government contracts partially offset this.
  • 5LEO constellation competition (MEDIUM): Starlink has dramatically more satellites deployed and a functioning commercial product. OneWeb is behind and needs to scale fast to be relevant in connectivity.
  • 6Liquidity / equity raise risk (MEDIUM): A €1.1B market cap and €1.5B+ debt means the equity is thin. If the business needs more capital, an equity raise at these levels would be massively dilutive to existing holders.
