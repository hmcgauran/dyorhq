Executive Summary

Prospex Energy PLC is a micro-cap oil and gas exploration and production company focused on Western Europe. The company operates producing assets in Spain (Viura/Selva) and Italy (Podere Maiar), and is expanding into Poland through a new licence award. At 3.38p, the stock is near the lower end of its 52-week range but has been supported by recent positive news flow — including a San Licence award in Poland (April 2026), an oversubscribed convertible loan note raise, and broker commentary flagging significantly increased cash generation from producing assets.

The prior report (conviction 32) was written before the San Licence award and the CLN oversubscription. The H&P broker note targets 21p — a 6x upside from current levels — though this target requires successful execution and sustained gas prices. The risk/reward at 3.38p has improved materially.

Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY. The San Licence award and CLN oversubscription are incremental positives. The H&P 21p target is compelling but requires near-term catalysts to be confirmed. BUY below 2.5p on execution risk. The European gas macro remains supportive given geopolitical factors.

Business Model

Prospex Energy operates a portfolio of oil and gas assets across Western Europe: producing fields in Spain (Viura/Selva) generating cash from gas sales to industrial offtakers; an Italian joint venture (Podere Maiar) selling natural gas to Hera; and new Polish exploration licences awarded in April 2026.

The company is directly exposed to European natural gas prices — a macro tailwind given the Russia-Ukraine crisis and ongoing sanctions on Russian energy. Higher European gas prices improve the economics of all producing assets.

Key Risks

  1. Broker target unverified (HIGH): The H&P 21p price target is cited in sentiment analysis but has not been independently verified. If this target is based on aggressive assumptions or is outdated, the risk/reward case weakens significantly.
  2. Polish licence execution (HIGH): The San Licence award is an option, not a proven discovery. Exploration risk is high. The licence must be progressed through seismic, drilling, and appraisal before any production can materialise — a 3-5 year timeline at minimum.
  3. Gas price sensitivity (MEDIUM): Prospex's cash flows are directly correlated to European gas prices. A sustained decline in gas prices would impair asset economics.
  4. Micro-cap liquidity (HIGH): At ~£14m market cap, Prospex is an AIM micro-cap. Bid/ask spreads are wide and selling pressure can cause outsized price moves.
  5. CLN dilution (MEDIUM): The £2m CLN converts to equity at maturity — if the stock does not re-rate, the conversion price may be significantly dilutive.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Investors who want micro-cap European gas exposure with a speculative upside case. The San Licence award and broker 21p target are genuine positives. Position sizing must be tiny — this is an AIM micro-cap.
