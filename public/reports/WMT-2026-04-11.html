1. Executive Summary

Walmart's relentless omnichannel expansion and membership traction underpin a solid if richly valued base case, with tariff sensitivity and US consumer stress the primary downside vectors.

2. Business Model

Walmart operates across three segments: US Walmart (general merchandise, groceries, sundries across ~4,600 stores); Sam's Club (membership-only warehouse clubs); and Walmart International (operations in 19 countries including Flipkart in India and Asda in the UK). Revenue is generated through retail sales, e-commerce marketplace fees, advertising income, and membership subscriptions (Walmart+ at $12.95/month). Grocery is the anchor category, representing approximately 60% of US net sales, providing defensive, high-frequency traffic that drives basket completion in higher-margin general merchandise.

4. Recent Catalysts

  • Walmart+ membership surpassed 50 million members in Q4 FY2025 (ended Jan 2025), driving recurring subscription revenue and higher basket frequency.
  • E-commerce and curbside pickup growth of approximately 23% YoY in FY2025, with marketplace third-party seller mix expanding margins.
  • Acquisition of Vizio completed in FY2025, adding advertising platform revenue (Walmart Connect) with high-margin programmatic ad dollars.
  • India operations (Flipkart) reached profitability for the first time in FY2025, unlocking significant embedded optionality.
  • US consumer shift from traditional grocery toward Walmart's value proposition accelerated in Q1 2026 amid tariff-driven inflation.

6. Key Risks (Ranked)

1
Tariff-driven cost inflation squeezes supplier margins and could force price increases that alienate value-sensitive shoppers, hitting same-store sales.
2
US consumer stress deepens as excess savings deplete and credit delinquencies rise, disproportionately impacting Walmart's core low-income shopper base.
3
Intense competition from Target, Amazon, and dollar-format retailers (Dollar General, Dollar Tree) for the value-conscious consumer.
4
Labour cost escalation from federal minimum wage pressure and unionisation campaigns in California and other states.
5
International operations in volatile markets (Argentina, India, South Africa) create currency and regulatory exposure.

7. Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Ideal for: Long-term income and stability investors seeking defensive retail exposure; ESG-mandated institutional holders; inflation-hedging portfolios.

Avoid if: You require deep value (P/E of 46x is elevated for retail); you want pure e-commerce exposure without legacy store drag; you are near retirement and cannot tolerate drawdowns.
