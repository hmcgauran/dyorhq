Executive Summary

Alkemy Capital Investments is a development-stage investment company focused on critical minerals and battery materials infrastructure. The core asset is the Tavasciute lithium project in Lithuania — a hard rock lithium project with a completed Definitive Feasibility Study and a binding Glencore offtake agreement covering 40% of projected production at competitive pricing. At 334.0p, the stock has recovered from distressed levels but remains fully priced as a development story pending Final Investment Decision.

The thesis is straightforward: Europe needs domestic lithium supply for EV batteries, Alkemy has a shovel-ready project with offtake signed, and the equity is dramatically undervalued versus the NPV of the project. The bear case is that project financing remains outstanding, geopolitical risk around Lithuania may complicate equity or debt financing, and FID timing is uncertain.

Bottom line: HOLD — SPECULATIVE. The asymmetric upside is real if financing closes. The binary risk is that it doesn't. Position sizing should reflect that this is a development-stage project, not an operating company.

Business Model

Alkemy Capital is an investment vehicle structured to develop and operate the Tavasciute lithium project in Lithuania. The model is to advance the project through financing to construction, then operation, generating revenue from long-term battery-quality lithium concentrate sales into the European market. The Glencore offtake agreement is the commercial anchor — it de-risks the revenue model significantly by providing a credible, creditworthy buyer at fixed pricing for a material portion of production.

The investment case is fundamentally different from a mining equity: the value creation happens as the project moves through financing, construction, and ramp-up milestones. The listed equity is the junior financing layer — it benefits from project success but is exposed to dilution risk if equity financing is required at unfavourable prices.

Recent Catalysts

  • Binding Glencore offtake (January 2026): The 40% TVL offtake agreement with Glencore is the commercial anchor. Glencore's involvement as both offtake customer and ~US$100M equity investor (via Ara Partners) provides significant third-party validation of project economics.
  • Wates Group as construction partner: The appointment of Wates Group as the construction partner for the mine development provides a credible EPC contractor, reducing execution risk for the development phase.
  • Ara Partners equity commitment: ~US$100M equity commitment from Ara Partners is non-binding but contingent on FID. This is a credible infrastructure-specialist investor validating the project thesis.
  • FID timing: The chairman has stated a Q1 2026 FID deadline. As of April 2026, the FID has not yet been formally announced. The delay itself is a yellow flag — the market is watching closely.
  • Lithium price context: Lithium prices have been volatile, ranging from $80,000/tonne peaks to $15,000/tonne troughs. Tavasciute's economics are viable at a range of lithium prices, but lower prices compress NPV and may complicate financing terms.

Key Risks

  1. 1FID delay or cancellation: The non-binding Ara Partners commitment and chairman's Q1 2026 FID target are meaningful but not yet binding. If either financing or permitting slips, the investment case deteriorates sharply. This is the primary binary risk.
  2. 2Equity dilution: If the equity raise required to fund Alkemy's portion of development costs is large relative to current market cap, existing shareholders face dilution at prices below current levels. Watch the raise size carefully at FID.
  3. 3Lithium price volatility: Tavasciute's NPV is sensitive to lithium prices. A sustained fall in lithium to below $20,000/tonne would reduce the equity's claim on project value and complicate debt financing terms.
  4. 4Lithuania geopolitical risk: Any escalation of Baltic/NATO geopolitical tension would make a Lithuanian lithium mine a more complex proposition for European infrastructure capital.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Should own: Speculative investors who understand development-stage mining equities and want direct exposure to European lithium self-sufficiency. Those who believe in the battery materials secular theme and want a more asymmetric entry than established producers. Position sizing should be small — this is binary project finance risk.

Should avoid: Anyone who needs operating cash flows or dividends. Investors who cannot tolerate the binary nature of FID-linked equity events. Anyone who lacks the patience for development-stage mining timelines (typically 2–4 years from FID to first production).
