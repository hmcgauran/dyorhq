Executive Summary

Avacta is a clinical-stage oncology company built around its pre|CISION platform - a tumour-targeted prodrug activation technology that releases active drug preferentially in the tumour microenvironment rather than systemically. The core technology uses Fibroblast Activation Protein (FAP), expressed in the stroma of approximately 90% of solid tumours, as the activation trigger.

This report incorporates material new data disclosed by CEO Dr Alastair Smith in an April 2026 Therapy4me podcast interview. The disclosures are substantive: the doxorubicin lifetime dose cap has been formally removed by both MHRA and FDA following zero cardiac toxicity observed across all patients dosed to date; a 90% disease control rate has been achieved in chemorefractory salivary gland cancer; the company tolerates four times the normally permissible doxorubicin dose; and the next programme (AVA6103, exatecan-PDC) has a five-day sustained-release half-life, materially expanding the treatable tumour types. AVA6103 IND filing is imminent and first patient dosing is weeks away.

The investment case has shifted materially. The pre|CISION platform is now generating human clinical data that substantiates the mechanistic thesis. The binary character of the investment remains - partnership and pivotal trial execution risk are still ahead - but the data quality has improved meaningfully since the last report.

Bottom line: BUY (strong) at 71.90p. Conviction upgraded from 63 to 68. The removal of the lifetime dose cap by regulators is a significant de-risking event that validates the cardiac safety thesis. Near-term catalysts are well-defined: AVA6000 salivary gland data before end of H1 2026, AVA6103 first patient dosing imminent, and AVA6103 signal read-out later in 2026. Size positions for binary outcome.

Business Model

Avacta's value is entirely in its pre|CISION platform and the clinical pipeline it generates. The platform activates cancer drugs specifically in the tumour microenvironment using FAP as the enzymatic trigger. FAP is expressed on cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs) in the tumour stroma of most epithelial solid tumours - not on the tumour cells themselves. This stromal targeting approach means the technology is tumour-agnostic in principle, though clinical efficacy is ultimately payload-dependent.

The key scientific advance is the capping group plus self-immolative linker technology, which controls the rate of drug release in the tumour microenvironment. This is what enables sustained release rather than burst release - the five-day half-life achieved with AVA6103 versus 45 minutes with the first-generation AVA6000 being a concrete demonstration of this control.

The platform is now expanding in two directions simultaneously. The first is broader tumour coverage through optimised payloads: AVA6000 (doxorubicin) has demonstrated 90% disease control in salivary gland cancer, TNBC, ovarian, and soft tissue sarcoma, with no activity seen in pancreatic cancer - a failure of payload sensitivity (doxorubicin does not work in pancreatic cancer), not FAP expression. This is a critical and honest platform learning: FAP expression alone does not guarantee efficacy across all tumour types. The second direction is entirely new payload classes: AVA6103 uses exatecan (a topoisomerase-1 inhibitor, same class as the highly active trastuzumab deruxtecan), and AVA6207 is the first dual-payload peptide drug conjugate (DAR 2:1, two drugs on one peptide), developed in response to a BD partner asking whether it was possible.

The commercial model is most likely a partnership for AVA6000 given the 24-person company size would require significant hiring to run pivotal trials independently. Active partnering conversations are ongoing. A US listing remains a strategic option but is less urgent if a partnership delivers upfront cash.

Recent Catalysts

  • Lifetime dose cap removed (MHRA and FDA, April 2026): Both regulators have formally approved protocol amendments removing the doxorubicin lifetime dose cap for AVA6000. This is the single most significant de-risking event since the programme began. Zero cardiac toxicity has been observed across all patients dosed to date. The CEO stated explicitly: "We've now eliminated the lifetime maximum because guess how much cardiac toxicity we see? Zero. Nothing."
  • 90% disease control rate in salivary gland cancer (chemorefractory patients): Materially stronger than any prior public disclosure. These are chemorefractory patients with limited treatment options - a 90% DCR is a compelling efficacy signal. Full data readout expected before end of H1 2026.
  • AVA6103 (exatecan-PDC) IND imminent: First patient dosing weeks away. Half-life extended to five days sustained release (from 45 minutes with AVA6000) via the capping group plus self-immolative linker. Initial four indications: small cell lung, gastric, cervical, and pancreatic. Most differentiated opportunity in gastric cancer, where FAP is expressed in approximately 100% of cases versus HER2 at only 15-20%.
  • AVA6207 dual-payload IND-enabling studies (late 2026/early 2027): First dual-payload peptide drug conjugate in the industry (DAR 2:1). Developed from a BD partner conversation. Represents a new dimension of the platform not previously in the public pipeline.
  • AVA6000 tolerates 4x the standard doxorubicin dose: Patients can receive four times the doxorubicin dose they would normally tolerate. Phase 1b expansion is ongoing. Project Optimus feedback from FDA has been received and incorporated.
  • AVA6000 partnering conversations active: A 24-person company would need to hire significantly to run pivotal trials alone. Partnership discussions are the most likely path to monetise AVA6000 and fund the broader pipeline.

Thesis Evaluation

The bull, base, and bear cases are recalibrated around the new clinical data. The lifetime dose cap removal materially reduces binary execution risk on the cardiac safety thesis. The 90% DCR in chemorefractory salivary gland cancer provides a credible efficacy signal in a real patient population. AVA6103 IND is imminent, expanding the pipeline to a potentially larger addressable market (gastric cancer alone is a significant indication with FAP expression near 100%).

The platform limitation is now clearly defined in public data: pancreatic cancer failure is a payload sensitivity problem (doxorubicin does not work in pancreatic cancer), not a FAP expression problem. FAP is still expressed in pancreatic cancer. This is important because it means the platform's 90% solid tumour expression claim remains valid - but investors must evaluate each payload-tumour combination independently. Not all FAP-expressing tumours will respond to all payloads.

The dual-payload capability (AVA6207) represents optionality that was not present in prior reports and is being developed in response to BD partner demand, which itself is a form of external validation of the platform's scientific basis.

Key Risks

  1. 1AVA6000 Phase II pivotal trial execution: Phase I data is encouraging but pivotal trial design, patient recruitment, and regulatory interactions for a conditional or full approval pathway are complex, expensive, and timeline-dependent. A 24-person company running its first pivotal trial is a significant operational challenge.
  2. 2AVA6103 gastric cancer signal read-out: FAP expression in gastric cancer is near 100% versus HER2 at 15-20%, which is encouraging. However, the first-in-human signal data is not expected until later in 2026, and early signals can be misleading in either direction. This is the primary near-term binary for the second programme.
  3. 3Partnership dependency: A deal at attractive terms is not guaranteed. Biotech partnering processes are long, diligence is intensive, and terms can disappoint. Delays in completing a partnership could force a dilutive financing before the platform is fully validated in a pivotal setting.
  4. 4Payload-tumour combination risk: The pancreatic cancer failure clarifies an important limitation: FAP expression does not guarantee efficacy for all payload classes. Each new tumour indication requires independent validation that the payload is active in that tumour type. This is manageable but means the "90% solid tumour" claim must be applied carefully.
  5. 5DOX cardiotoxicity residual uncertainty: Zero cardiac toxicity observed to date is genuinely encouraging. However, the patient numbers in Phase I are small and longer-term follow-up across a broader population is required to confirm the cardiac safety profile fully. The formal lifetime dose cap removal by regulators addresses this risk materially but does not eliminate it entirely.
  6. 6Funding runway and dilution: The GBP 9.45M raised in early 2026 provides meaningful runway, but a pivotal trial programme for AVA6000 will require significant additional capital. Timing of any fundraising relative to clinical catalysts will affect the dilution profile.
