Executive Summary

Avacta Group is a UK-listed biotech developing a pipeline of cancer therapeutics based on its proprietary Affimer scaffold platform and the pre|CISION tumour-activated chemotherapy technology. The investment case rests on two pillars: the scientific validation of the FAP-targeting pre|CISION mechanism, supported by peer-reviewed literature, and the near-term catalysts from the ongoing Phase 1 trial of faridaksorubicin and the upcoming AACR 2026 conference presentations (17-22 April 2026, New Orleans -- conference is currently in session). The shares trade at a significant discount to the implicit value of a successful partnership or NASDAQ listing, both of which management is actively exploring. With the AACR conference underway and Phase 1 data expected in H1 2026, the risk-reward is compelling at current levels. BUY (STRONG). Conviction Score: 57/100.

Business Model

Avacta operates a pure-play biotech model: develop proprietary therapeutic candidates through early clinical validation, then out-license or partner with larger pharmaceutical companies for late-stage development and commercialisation. The company retains rights for the US market in certain structures, providing optionality for a NASDAQ listing or direct US partnership. The Affimer platform is the core technology engine: small (approximately 12kDa), cysteine-free scaffold proteins engineered to bind selectively to specific disease targets. Compared to monoclonal antibodies, Affimers are smaller, cheaper to manufacture, and offer potential advantages in tumour penetration and immunogenicity. The pre|CISION technology is the proprietary tumour-targeting layer: a peptide linker selectively cleaved by fibroblast activation protein (FAP), an enzyme highly expressed in the tumour stroma of over 90% of epithelial cancers but largely absent from normal tissue. By linking doxorubicin to this FAP-cleavable linker, pre|CISION prodrugs release the active drug preferentially within the tumour microenvironment, dramatically reducing systemic toxicity. Revenue model: no current revenue. Value creation path is through out-licensing deals (upfront + milestone + royalty) or a corporate transaction (partnership, acquisition, NASDAQ listing).

Key Risks

  1. #1 Phase 1 clinical failure: If faridaksorubicin shows unacceptable toxicity or insufficient efficacy, the investment case materially deteriorates. Cardiotoxicity of doxorubicin is the primary safety risk to watch in the data. The FAP targeting mechanism is scientifically validated in the literature but clinical translation is not guaranteed.
  2. #2 Financing and dilution risk: With only approximately GBP 9.45m in cash and no revenue, Avacta will need additional capital within 18-24 months if no partnership materialises. A down-round placement would be significantly dilutive at current market capitalisation of approximately GBP 70m.
  3. #3 Partnership process failure: If Phase 1 data is positive but no major pharmaceutical partner emerges, the NASDAQ listing path becomes the primary option. US equity capital markets for pre-revenue biotech are currently challenging and a discounted listing would reprice the equity significantly.
  4. #4 Scientific competition: Several companies are developing FAP-targeted radionuclide therapies and ADCs. While this validates the FAP mechanism, it also means Avacta faces competition for partner attention and commercial positioning. The peer-reviewed literature confirms FAP targeting is valid but the space is becoming more crowded.
  5. #5 AACR abstract data quality: Conference presentations at AACR are often preliminary. The abstract data needs to be confirmed in the full Phase 1 dataset. Share price reaction to conference presentations can be volatile and short-lived without a clear partnering signal.
