Executive Summary

Simulations Plus Inc is a specialised developer of simulation software for the pharmaceutical industry, operating across two distinct divisions: simulation and data-centric services. The company has a long-standing relationship with the FDA and a well-regarded capability in physiologically-based pharmacokinetic (PBPK) modelling. However, the company is currently loss-making with declining revenues, elevated operating costs, and a dependency on a small number of large contracts. The stock has declined materially from its 52-week high, but with no clear near-term path to profitability and no revenue growth to show, the risk-reward does not favour a position at current levels. Business Model

Simulations Plus Inc provides modelling and simulation software and services to the pharmaceutical industry. Its primary platform is GastroPlus, a PBPK simulation tool used by drug developers and regulatory scientists to predict drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (ADME). The company also offers a set of data-centric services, working with pharmaceutical clients to analyse clinical trial data and support regulatory submissions. Revenue is generated through software licence fees, maintenance contracts, and service contracts. The customer base is concentrated -- a meaningful portion of revenue is derived from a small number of large pharmaceutical clients and from government/agency contracts (notably with the FDA). The business has high operating leverage given its fixed cost base of R&D and engineering staff, but revenue has been declining in recent periods as pharmaceutical clients have tightened external spending.

Key Risks

  2. #2 Path to profitability: The company is currently loss-making with negative EPS of -$3.12. If revenues do not stabilise, the company may face cash burn concerns or require capital raise, diluting existing shareholders.
  3. #3 Competitive dynamics: PBPK modelling is a niche but not isolated field. Competitors and in-house tools developed by large pharmaceutical companies could erode demand for GastroPlus licences over time.
  4. #4 LIVERVE platform execution risk: The AI-driven liver toxicity prediction platform is still in a relatively early commercial phase. Failure to gain traction would represent a wasted investment and diminish the bull case materially.
  5. #5 Regulatory and pharma spending cycle: The FDA and large pharmaceutical clients are the backbone of this business. Changes in FDA modelling requirements or broad cuts to pharma R&D budgets would directly impact Simulations Plus revenues.
