Executive Summary

Dell Technologies is the dominant US enterprise AI server infrastructure provider, having converted its Dell AI Factory partnerships with NVIDIA into a multi-billion-dollar AI revenue stream. Record FY2026 revenue of $113.5 billion, a $43 billion AI server backlog, and Goldman Sachs upgrading the price target to $215 make the bull case for AI infrastructure spending very concrete. At approximately 18x P/E on $6.40 EPS, Dell trades at a significant discount to pure-play AI names and offers a meaningful entry point for investors willing to wait for the AI infrastructure buildout to mature. The OPPORTUNISTIC BUY thesis is that the AI infrastructure buildout is still in its early innings and that Dell's channel relationships, enterprise install base, and supply chain scale create a durable competitive advantage in AI servers that the market is not fully crediting at current levels.

Business Model

Dell operates across three core segments. Client Solutions (CSG) designs, manufactures, and sells hardware to commercial and consumer customers -- PCs, workstations, and peripherals -- leveraging one of the largest channel distributions networks in the world. Infrastructure Solutions Group (ISG) is the AI story: rack servers, storage arrays (PowerStore, PowerMax), data protection, and networking, including the Dell AI Factory initiative which bundles Dell server hardware with NVIDIA GPU infrastructure for enterprise AI deployments. VMware (post-spin-off carve-out) provides virtualisation, cloud management, and container platform software that extends Dell's infrastructure reach into software-defined data centre and hybrid cloud environments.

Dell's competitive moat is its supply chain scale and enterprise channel depth. It is one of only three Tier-1 global server vendors with hyperscaler relationships, and its partner ecosystem spans over 100,000 channel partners worldwide. This gives it an advantaged position in winning enterprise AI infrastructure deals where customers want a single trusted vendor for full-stack delivery.

Recent Catalysts

Dell's AI-optimized server revenues grew more than fourfold year-on-year in fiscal 2026, with the company projecting nearly $50 billion in AI server revenue for fiscal 2027. The $43 billion AI server backlog provides exceptional revenue visibility heading into FY2027 and underscores the sustained nature of hyperscaler and enterprise AI infrastructure capex. Q4 FY2025 results were strong: Dell reported revenue of $23.9 billion in the quarter (up 7% year-on-year on a fiscal year basis), with operating income of $2.2 billion up 40% and record diluted EPS of $2.15. Full-year fiscal 2025 revenue came in at $95.6 billion.

Goldman Sachs responded by upgrading Dell's price target to $215 from $195, citing AI server dominance and DRAM supply advantages as key drivers. The upgrade is notable: Goldman had been cautious on enterprise hardware through 2024-2025, and the shift in stance signals that Wall Street is beginning to take the AI infrastructure super-cycle seriously at the hardware level. The Dell AI Factory with NVIDIA -- now spanning liquid-cooled PowerEdge servers including the XE9812, XE9880L, and XE9885L launching through 2026 -- positions Dell as a primary beneficiary of enterprise AI adoption beyond the hyperscaler layer.

Dell's stock is up approximately 16% year-to-date, outpacing its industry, as the AI server demand narrative has re-rated the name. Technical breakouts and inclusion in broad AI/tech portfolios have attracted institutional flow, even as some memory cost pressures and softer traditional PC demand provide a partial counterbalance.

Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case (75)

AI server demand accelerates through 2026 and beyond. Dell AI Factory revenue reaches $50B+, hyperscaler AI orders continue growing quarter-on-quarter, and management guides to sustained high-teens revenue growth. The stock re-rates to 28-32x P/E as the market prices in multi-year AI infrastructure durability. Goldman $215 target reached and exceeded. Price target: $200-$235.

Base Case (58)

Steady AI infrastructure growth continues through 2026-2027. Traditional enterprise IT stabilises. EPS grows to $8-$9 as ISG mix drives revenue and operating leverage improves margins modestly. The Goldman $215 price target is reached within 12-18 months. Stock drifts to $180-$215 at 22-25x P/E. Price target: $180-$215.

Bear Case (35)

Hyperscale capex plateau emerges in H2 2026 as GPU supply normalises and cloud providers slow infrastructure builds. AI server competition from HPE/Juniper/custom hyperscaler builders intensifies, compressing margins. Traditional PC demand continues to soften. Multiple compresses to 14-17x and stock falls to $90-$110. Price target: $90-$110.

Key Risks

  1. AI spending cyclicality: Hyperscale capex cycles are unpredictable. Dell's AI server revenue is heavily concentrated in a small number of large cloud customers; any pullback in GPU infrastructure orders would hit revenue and margins disproportionately.
  2. Intensifying server competition: HPE, Juniper Networks, and hyperscalers' own custom-built server programmes are eroding Dell's pricing power in AI infrastructure. Custom silicon (Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia) could over time displace NVIDIA GPU servers that Dell sells.
  3. China exposure: Dell has meaningful revenue exposure to China markets where geopolitical tensions, domestic competition (Inspur, Huawei), and regulatory headwinds pose ongoing structural risk.
  4. Complex EMC/VMware integration: The legacy EMC relationship (Dell acquired EMC in 2016 in the largest tech M&A at the time) and the ongoing VMware carve-out integration create structural complexity, ongoing debt obligations, and potential for margin drag that investors must monitor.
  5. Margin compression on AI hardware: AI server hardware carries lower gross margins than software and traditional enterprise infrastructure. As AI servers become a larger share of revenue mix, overall group margins could come under pressure -- a structural risk that the P/E multiple does not fully reflect.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Investors seeking direct AI infrastructure exposure without paying 50-80x P/E for pure-play AI software names. The ~1.2% dividend provides a modest floor while the AI growth narrative plays out. Long-term value investors who believe the market is underweighting Dell's durable competitive position in enterprise hardware.

Avoiders: Investors who want pure AI software/GPU leverage should look at NVIDIA, Arista, or pure-play AI SaaS. Those concerned about near-term valuation risk should wait for a better entry. Margin-focused investors should be cautious on the mix-shift risk toward lower-margin AI hardware.

Recommendation

OPPORTUNISTIC BUY (conviction: 58). The AI infrastructure buildout is real and Dell is uniquely positioned to capture it. At approximately 18x P/E on $6.40 EPS, the stock is inexpensive relative to the quality of AI infrastructure exposure it offers -- well below the multiples commanded by pure-play AI software names and meaningfully below the Goldman Sachs price target of $215. The $43 billion backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility.

Entry framework:

  • BUY below $95 -- attractive risk-reward given base-case upside to $180-$215 and the AI infrastructure secular tailwind

Position sizing should reflect the binary nature of the AI capex cycle risk. The bear case ($90-$110) represents a meaningful drawdown from current levels; do not overweight on pure momentum.
