Executive Summary

Linde is one of the highest-quality industrial gas businesses in the world, and that matters because industrial gases are unusually resilient, sticky and cash generative when managed well. The company benefits from long-term on-site contracts, disciplined pricing and exposure to attractive end markets ranging from healthcare to electronics. It is rarely cheap, but there are good reasons why investors often accept that.

Business Model

Linde supplies atmospheric, process and specialty gases through bulk, cylinder and on-site arrangements. The model is attractive because customer switching is difficult, reliability matters and many contracts are embedded in critical industrial or medical processes. Growth comes from a mix of base demand, pricing, productivity and carefully selected project wins, including potential upside from energy transition infrastructure where the economics are sound.
