Thesis Evaluation

Bull Case (83): da Vinci 5 launch accelerates procedure conversions and drives system placements above 15% annual growth. Ion platform achieves meaningful revenue scale. International markets (China, India) open materially. EPS grows to $10+. Price: $530+.

Base Case (70): System placements grow in line with procedure volumes at 12-14%. da Vinci 5 ramps successfully. Ion contributes increasingly. ISS and MIS procedure volumes stable. EPS: $8.50-9.50. Price: $460-520.

Bear Case (52): da Vinci 5 transition disappoints or capital equipment budget constraints slow system placements in the US and Europe. Chinese domestic robotic surgery competitors (Mazor, Tinavi) accelerate. EPS falls to $7-7.50. Price: below $400.

Key Risks

  1. Capital equipment cycle risk: da Vinci systems represent significant capital expenditure for hospitals. Any rationalisation of hospital capital budgets directly impacts system placement volumes.
  2. da Vinci 5 ramp uncertainty: New system launches carry execution risk. Any clinicalcomplication or technical issue with the dV5 platform would be material to the investment thesis.
  3. Competitive entry: Medtronic's Hugo RAS, Johnson & Johnson's Ottava, and Chinese domestic competitors (Mazor, Robdoc) represent meaningful long-term competitive threats.
  4. Procedure reimbursement risk: CMS reimbursement rates for robotic procedures could come under pressure, reducing the financial incentive for hospitals to adopt robotic surgery.
  5. China geopolitical risk: Sino-US tensions could restrict Intuitive's ability to operate in China, a meaningful long-term growth market.

Who Should Own It / Avoid It

Owners: Long-term investors seeking surgical robotics pure-play with an unmatched installed base, recurring service revenue and durable competitive moat.

Avoiders: Value investors at current multiples. Investors concerned about competitive disruption from Medtronic Hugo or Chinese domestic alternatives. Short-term traders.
