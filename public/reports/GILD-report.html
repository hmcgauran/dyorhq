Key Risks

  • 1HIV franchise competitive entry: GSK/ViiV's long-acting cabotegravir directly competes with lenacapavir in prevention. Pricing and access negotiations could limit peak penetration.
  • 2Trodelvy competitive dynamics: The ADC space in oncology is rapidly becoming crowded (AstraZeneca/Daiichi, Gilead's ownpipeline). Confirmatory trials and survival data may not support current trajectories.
  • 3Biktarvy patent cliff: While Biktarvy is protected into the early 2030s, any future generic entry would represent a significant revenue event for the core HIV business.
  • 4Liver disease revenue volatility: Revenue from HCV treatments is declining as the treated patient pool normalises. NASH/ASH programmes remain speculative.
  • 5Acquisition integration and capital allocation: Gilead's $21bn Immunomedics acquisition has performed better than feared but the high acquisition price constrains management flexibility.
