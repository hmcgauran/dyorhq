Executive Summary

Manchester United is not really an operating fundamentals story in the normal sense. It trades as a premium sports brand wrapped around ownership, sentiment and occasional corporate optionality. At $17.65 with a market cap of roughly $3.06B and negative EPS, the stock is hard to justify on conventional financial quality alone.

Bottom line: REDUCE. Strong brand, weak investment case.

Business Model

Revenue comes from broadcasting, commercial partnerships, sponsorship and matchday income. The global brand is powerful, but football clubs are structurally awkward listed assets because sporting results, fan politics and ownership questions distort the fundamentals.
