Executive Summary

Spire is one of the more interesting smaller-cap space data names because it sells data and analytics rather than relying entirely on long-dated hardware optionality. The proposition, satellite-derived weather, maritime and aviation data, is commercially understandable. At $20.36, however, the stock is only 0.7% below its 52-week high, so the market is already giving it a lot of credit.

Unlike several speculative peers, Spire at least has a visible commercial use case and a positive earnings profile on the figures available here. Bottom line: HOLD - SPECULATIVE. Better business model than the theme crowd, but still extended.

Business Model

Spire operates a constellation of small satellites and monetises the resulting data through subscription analytics products for weather, maritime, aviation and government customers. The core appeal is recurring data revenue rather than one-off hardware wins.

The moat is moderate rather than absolute. The company benefits from proprietary data collection infrastructure, but must still prove that its data products are sticky and valuable enough to resist pricing pressure and competitive overlap.
