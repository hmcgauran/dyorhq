Business Model

Cisco designs, manufactures, and sells networking equipment and software globally, serving enterprise, service provider, and public sector customers. The business has evolved through three phases: routers and switches (core), security and collaboration (supplementary), and now AI infrastructure (strategic growth). Three revenue engines drive the model.

Product revenue (65% of total) covers switches, routers, wireless, and emerging AI networking hardware including the 800G platforms and Spectrum X family co-developed with NVIDIA. This segment is cyclic, driven by product refresh cycles and hyperscaler capex. Cisco's competitive position in enterprise switching remains strong; the AI networking play with hyperscalers is newer and gaining traction.

Services revenue (35%) comprises software subscriptions, technical support, and managed services attached to the installed base. This recurring revenue stream provides revenue visibility and higher gross margins than hardware. Maintenance contract renewal rates are the key metric. Gross margins of 64.8% TTM reflect the software/services mix advantage.

Segment breakdown includes Secure Networks (security switching, zero-trust architecture), Silicon and Optics (custom ASICs, 800G optics, coherent DSPs), and Collaboration (Webex). The Splunk acquisition added data telemetry and security analytics to the portfolio. Cisco's competitive moat is the installed base (over 500 million connected devices), the partner ecosystem (600,000+ channel partners), and R&D scale ($7B+ annual R&D spend).
