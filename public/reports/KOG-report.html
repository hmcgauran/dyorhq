Executive Summary

Kongsberg Gruppen is a high-quality Norwegian defence and maritime technology group operating three divisions: Kongsberg Maritime (marine electronics), Kongsberg Defense & Aerospace (military systems), and Kongsberg Digital (industrial software). The shares hit a fresh 52-week high of NOK 426.30 on 7 April 2026 — effectively at all-time highs — after a remarkable 41.5% gain in the past three months and 63.8% year-to-date. The re-rating is driven by Europe's accelerating defence spending buildout

Business Model

Kongsberg Gruppen operates three distinct but complementary divisions. Kongsberg Maritime (KM) is the world's leading supplier of marine electronics, navigation systems, and automation for the commercial and defence maritime sectors — a structurally growing market as vessels become more complex and regulators tighten safety requirements. Kongsberg Defense & Aerospace (KDA) is the core earnings driver: it designs and manufactures naval weapons systems (including the renowned Naval Strike Missile), remote weapon stations, communication systems, and missile technology for defence customers globally. Kongsberg Digital (KDI) is the emerging software division building industrialdigitisation platforms for energy and maritime customers. The company's competitive moat is deep: its missile and weapons systems are integrated into NATO-member defence programs, creating high switching costs and long revenue visibility. Kongsberg is a trusted supplier to the Norwegian Armed Forces, the US DoD, and a broad coalition of European defence ministries — a relationship capital that takes decades to build. Defence contracts typically carry long lead times and multi-year duration, providing significant earnings visibility. The company targets a dividend of NOK 5.70 per share, implying a 1.35% yield at current prices — modest, but the total shareholder return story is primarily capital appreciation driven by the defence cycle.

Recent Catalysts

European defence spending acceleration: NATO members have committed to increasing defence budgets, with Germany, Poland, and the Nordic countries leading the charge. Kongsberg's positioning in naval weapons, maritime surveillance, and land systems makes it a direct beneficiary of this structural spending increase. New contract awards: Kongsberg has been securing significant new contracts across its KDA division, particularly in naval missile systems and communication suites. The order backlog is believed to be at historically high levels, providing multi-year revenue visibility. 52-week high breakout: The stock broke to all-time highs on 7 April 2026 — confirming the uptrend but also signalling that the market is paying a premium for the defence cycle narrative. Kongsberg Digital progress: KDI continues to scale its industrial digital twins and energy sector software. While still a small proportion of group earnings, this division represents the long-term growth optionality beyond the current defence cycle. NOK currency: As a NOK-denominated equity, Kongsberg is exposed to NOK/EUR and NOK/USD movements. A stronger Norwegian krone would boost reported earnings in EUR terms for international investors.
