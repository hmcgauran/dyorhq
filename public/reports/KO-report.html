Executive Summary

Business Model

Coca-Cola operates one of the world's most iconic brand portfolios, generating revenue through the manufacture, marketing, and distribution of beverages across four principal segments: Europe, Middle East & Africa (EMEA): A portfolio of sparkling beverages, juices, water, sports drinks, and energy drinks sold through bottling partners and direct relationships with retail chains. Latin America: Large-scale emerging market distribution with strong per-capita consumption growth, particularly in Brazil, Mexico, and Argentina. North America: Mature market operations including Coca-Cola, Diet Coke, Fanta, Sprite, Dasani, and newer innovations such as Coca-Cola Zero Sugar and AHA sparkling water. Asia Pacific: China, Japan, India, and South-East Asia operations, with significant exposure to evolving consumer taste preferences towards premium and functional beverages. Coca-Cola has been accelerating its move towards higher-margin premium and functional beverages (energy drinks, sports hydration, coffee) while divesting lower-margin legacy brands — a structurally positive shift for gross margins.

Key Risks

  • 1Consumer spending deterioration: Coca-Cola's revenue is sensitive to global consumer disposable income. A broad economic slowdown — particularly in emerging markets — would compress volumes and limit pricing power.
  • 2Health regulatory headwinds: Expanding sugar taxes, mandatory front-of-pack labelling, and bans on advertising to children in key markets (UK, EU, Mexico, South Africa) could structurally impair volume growth in core sparkling categories.
  • 3Currency volatility: With over 60 per cent of operating income generated outside the US, a sustained stronger dollar would translate to meaningful reported earnings headwinds regardless of underlying business performance.
  • 4Premium acquisition integration risk: The BodyArmor and Costa acquisitions need to perform to justify their price tags; underperformance would trigger goodwill impairments and damage management credibility.
  • 5Climate and water risk: As a water-intensive business, increasing water scarcity and stricter environmental regulations in bottling geographies represent long-term supply-chain and reputational risks.
