## Business Model
Beeks operates three core product lines: low-latency cloud infrastructure, proximity hosting, and network connectivity. Its customers are banks, brokers, hedge funds, proprietary trading firms, and exchanges -- all of whom require sub-millisecond execution environments and deterministic network performance. The business is not a general-purpose cloud provider; it competes in a specialist segment where latency is existential for clients' trading strategies. The proximity hosting product places customer infrastructure in data centres adjacent to major exchange matching engines -- a critical advantage for traders who cannot afford the latency penalty of distance. Beeks has built a network of such facilities across London, Frankfurt, Tokyo, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Sydney. Revenue is recurring by nature -- co-location and connectivity contracts are long-term -- but the customer base is concentrated among a relatively small number of institutional participants. Retention rates are high because switching costs are significant: moving a trading infrastructure is disruptive and costly. This creates a defensible recurring revenue base, though one that is exposed to consolidation in the broker/dealer community.
