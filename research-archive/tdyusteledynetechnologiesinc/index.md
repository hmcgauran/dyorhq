## Business Model
Teledyne's competitive position rests on deep technical expertise in sensing, imaging, and electronics that is difficult to replicate. Defence Electronics supplies electronic warfare systems, RF and microwave components, and communications systems to the US Department of Defence and international customers. Imaging provides sensors and cameras used in space, aerial surveillance, industrial inspection, and medical imaging. Instrumentation serves environmental monitoring and precision measurement markets. Aerospace provides turbine engine components to commercial and defence aircraft OEMs. The company's M&A track record is notable: Teledyne has been disciplined about acquiring businesses at reasonable multiples and then driving operational synergies. The acquisition of e2v (imaging sensors), DALSA (imaging), and FLIR (thermal imaging, $8bn deal in 2021) are the most significant. FLIR in particular gave Teledyne a consumer and defence-grade thermal imaging platform that is now being integrated into AI analytics products.
