## Business Model
Alphabet operates three core business segments: Google Services (Search, YouTube, advertising technology, Android, hardware), Google Cloud (B2B cloud computing, AI infrastructure, Workspace), and Other Bets (Waymo autonomous driving, Verily, Wing drone delivery, Calico). The financial heart of the company is Google Services, which generates the vast majority of revenue and almost all operating profit. The Search business monetises through a combination of text ads, shopping ads, and display advertising. Google's advertising technology stack - DV360, Display & Video 360, and the Google Ads platform - is the dominant buy-side and sell-side infrastructure for digital advertising globally. This duopoly with Meta Platforms gives Alphabet pricing power with advertisers that is now being tested by AI-driven alternatives that deliver answers rather than blue links. Google Cloud is Alphabet's strategic growth vehicle: a full-stack cloud platform competing with AWS and Azure for enterprise workloads. GCP is differentiated by its AI infrastructure (TPU chips, Vertex AI, Gemini API) and the security and compliance benefits of being built on Google's own infrastructure. Cloud revenue was approximately $36bn+ ARR as of late 2025, growing at 30%+ annually, but cloud-specific profitability remains elusive at the segment level. Waymo is the clearest commercial success among the Other Bets: a commercial robotaxi service operating in San Francisco, Phoenix, Los Angeles, and Austin without a safety driver. It is the acknowledged leader in autonomous driving technology. International expansion is capital-intensive and will take years to replicate regulatory approvals and HD mapping coverage. A ruling is expected in 2026. This is the single largest known risk to the investment thesis. ## Key Risks
1. DOJ structural remedies (HIGH): The remedies phase of the antitrust case is the single most significant known risk. If the court orders Chrome to be spun off or forced to carry rival search engines as defaults, the economics of the Search business could be materially impaired. This is not a tail risk - the government won the liability phase and is pushing for aggressive remedies. Monitor remedy proposals and appeal timelines closely.
2. AI search structural displacement (HIGH): The threat to Google's search ad model is not theoretical. Each AI overview replaces multiple organic search results and potentially multiple ad clicks. If AI-native search alternatives take 20-30% of high-intent query volume, Google's ad inventory and pricing power erode structurally. This is a 5-10 year risk but it is now credibly on the table.
3. Cloud margin and capex pressure (MEDIUM): $70bn+ annual capex is the right strategic investment but creates near-term margin pressure as assets are built ahead of revenue. If Cloud revenue growth decelerates from 30%+ to 20% while capex remains elevated, free cash flow generation will disappoint consensus estimates.
4. Competitive gap in frontier AI (MEDIUM): Google is a credible but not dominant player in the current wave of AI development. Anthropic (Claude) and OpenAI (GPT-4/5) have established mindshare with developers and enterprises. Google's Gemini ecosystem (Vertex, TPU, DeepMind) is real but the brand perception gap in the developer community has not fully closed.
