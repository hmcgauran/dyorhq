## Executive Summary
Raytheon's successor entity benefits from substantial defence spending tailwinds and Pratt & Whitney's commercial aviation recovery, but the AGT1000 turbine defect issue and elevated P/E of 40.61x create a more nuanced risk-reward profile - HOLD.

## Business Model
RTX (formed from the United Technologies and Raytheon merger) operates three divisions. Collins Aerospace Systems is a leading supplier of avionics, interiors, sensors and power systems for commercial and defence aviation. Pratt & Whitney manufactures jet engines for commercial and military aircraft, including the Geared Turbofan (GTF) and the F135 fighter engine. Raytheon Missiles & Defence supplies precision weapons, air defence systems (Patriot, Iron Dome in the US), sensors and advanced missiles to the US and allied governments.

## Recent Catalysts
The AGT1000 turbofan defect - involving metallic powder contamination in certain engine cohorts - has required a large number of shop visits and engine replacements, primarily affecting Airbus A320neo family aircraft. The commercial narrowbody market remains supply-constrained due to this issue and ongoing aircraft delivery ramp-up. On the defence side, RTX has been a primary beneficiary of increased NATO defence spending and US Army/USAF procurement modernisation budgets. The company won the next-generation Long Range Hypersonic Weapon (LRHW) prime contract.

## Key Risks
1. AGT1000 turbofan defect: The metallic powder contamination issue in certain AGT1000 engines has created a large and ongoing shop visit burden. If the root cause is more systemic than initially assessed, costs could be significantly higher and the fleet grounding more extensive.
2. Defence budget sensitivity: RTX is heavily reliant on US DoD procurement. Changes in administration, shifts in defence priorities (e.g. towards personnel vs. equipment) or fiscal constraints could affect Raytheon Missiles & Defence's order flow.
3. Elevated valuation: At 40.61x P/E, RTX is pricing in a relatively optimistic scenario for the Pratt & Whitney recovery and defence demand. The margin for error is limited.
4. Commercial aerospace cycle: Collins and Pratt & Whitney are exposed to the commercial aviation cycle. A demand slowdown or new aircraft programme delays could impair volumes.
