## Business Model
Bioventix develops and manufactures recombinant antibodies using phage display and other display technologies. Unlike traditional monoclonal antibodies derived from animal sources, recombinant antibodies are produced via microbial expression systems, offering superior consistency, scalability, and regulatory tractability for diagnostic and pharmaceutical applications. The revenue model is split across three streams: direct sale of antibodies to IVD kit manufacturers (the largest component), contract research and development services for pharmaceutical and biotech partners, and royalties from partnered products that reach commercial stage. IVD customers use Bioventix reagents in immunoassay platforms -- including ELISA, lateral flow, and chemiluminescence formats -- for applications spanning clinical chemistry, infectious disease, and autoimmune conditions. Gross margins are high, consistent with specialty reagent businesses. The recurring nature of IVD supply contracts provides reasonable revenue visibility. The pharma contracting arm adds a growth optionality, though timelines are long and binary outcomes are possible on the pharmaceutical collaboration side.
