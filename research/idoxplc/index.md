## Business Model
IDOX operates as a software-as-a-service and licence-based business serving the UK public sector. Core product lines fall into three categories: Document Management: The historical foundation of the business. IDOX provides electronic document management systems (EDMS) to local authorities and government agencies, enabling the creation, storage, retrieval, and governance of public records. This is a recurring revenue line characterised by long-term contracts and high retention, as switching costs for document management systems in government are substantial. Planning and Development: IDOX's planning application software is deployed across a significant majority of UK local planning authorities. It handles the entire application lifecycle from submission through validation, consultation, determination, and appeal. With the UK government's stated ambition to accelerate housing delivery and planning reform, this division has meaningful long-term demand tailwinds, though procurement cycles are slow and multi-year. Regulatory Compliance: A smaller but growing segment covering compliance software for regulatory bodies, including environmental permitting and monitoring solutions. This aligns with the government's regulatory digitisation agenda. Revenue is predominantly contract-based with a mix of SaaS subscriptions and multi-year support and maintenance agreements. The public sector procurement cycle typically produces lumpy contract wins, which can create period-to-period revenue volatility. Customer concentration is a risk: a small number of large contracts with major local authorities or central government departments can represent significant portions of annual revenue.
