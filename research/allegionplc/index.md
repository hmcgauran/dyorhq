## Executive Summary
Allegion sells mechanical and electronic security products, especially doors, locks, access control and related hardware, with a strong installed-base advantage in institutional and commercial settings. It is a quality industrial, but the investment case usually hinges on whether electronic security growth can outpace any cyclical weakness in non-residential construction. That makes it more of a compounder-at-the-right-price story than a rapid re-rating opportunity.

## Business Model
The company earns from security products used in schools, hospitals, commercial buildings and residential markets, with North America carrying significant weight. Its better attributes are specification-driven demand, trusted brands, aftermarket support and exposure to retrofit as well as new build. The strategic attraction is the shift from purely mechanical hardware towards connected access and software-adjacent offerings, though it remains an industrial business rather than a pure software story.
