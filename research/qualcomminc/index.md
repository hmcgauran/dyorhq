## Executive Summary
Qualcomm's dominance in wireless semiconductors and the AI-driven upgrade cycle in smartphones provide a solid foundation, but the licensing (QTL) business remains structurally challenged and the valuation at 26.45x P/E is reasonable rather than compelling - HOLD.

## Business Model
Qualcomm operates two complementary businesses. QCT (Qualcomm CDMA Technologies) designs and sells wireless semiconductors: Snapdragon processors for smartphones, RF front-end components, automotive chips (Snapdragon Ride, Snapdragon Car-to-Cloud) and IoT connectivity chips. QTL (Qualcomm Technology Licensing) licenses the company's extensive patent portfolio covering CDMA, 4G and 5G standards, generating substantial royalty revenue. The company serves Apple, Samsung, Xiaomi, OPPO, Vivo and other OEMs globally.

## Key Risks
1. Smartphone market cyclicality: Qualcomm's fortunes are closely tied to global smartphone unit volumes, which remain cyclical despite 5G upgrades. A sustained downturn in China would significantly impact QCT revenues.
2. QTL licensing headwinds: QTL revenues are sensitive to OEM unit volumes and royalty rates. Ongoing disputes or regulatory intervention in key jurisdictions (particularly Asia) could compress royalty income.
3. In-house silicon competition: Apple (custom Bionic), Google (Tensor) and Samsung (Exynos variants) are reducing reliance on Qualcomm in flagship devices, a structural headwind to premium Snapdragon volumes.
4. Automotive execution risk: Qualcomm has built a large automotive pipeline, but converting design wins to revenues on schedule and at target margins requires sustained execution over multiple years.
