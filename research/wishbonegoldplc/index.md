## Business Model
Wishbone Gold PLC is a junior precious metals explorer operating in West Africa, with project interests in Ghana and Cote d'Ivoire -- two of Africa's most established mining jurisdictions. The company's model is characteristic of the junior mining peer group: financed by equity raises, progressing exploration assets through the resource pipeline from greenfield drilling to measured resource definition, with the ultimate exit being either a major mining company acquisition or development of a producing asset. Revenue is currently negligible; the company is entirely dependent on capital markets to fund exploration activity. The West African geological belt is well-established -- multi-hundred-ounce gold deposits are documented across Ghana and Cote d'Ivoire -- but geological promise does not guarantee commercial discovery, and junior explorers at this stage carry a very high failure rate. The company has historically sought to diversify into broader natural resources including base metals, which introduces execution risk across multiple commodity cycles.
