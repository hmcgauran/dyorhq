## Business Model
Amaroq operates as a formally constituted investment company, regulated in the UK and listed on the London Stock Exchange. Its mandate is long-term capital appreciation through direct equity stakes in unlisted and listed Icelandic companies. The investment approach is selective, thesis-driven, and oriented around co-investment alongside Iceland's domestic institutional ecosystem. Portfolio construction is governed by a published investment policy. The manager seeks controlling or significant minority stakes in businesses with identifiable exit pathways - trade sales to European strategic buyers, IPO on Nasdaq Iceland, or secondary sale to another private equity vehicle. The portfolio is not managed to a benchmark or index. Fee structure is typical for a closed-ended investment company: a management fee on deployed capital plus a performance fee (carried interest) on realised gains above a hurdle rate. This aligns manager and shareholder interests over a multi-year horizon, though it does create complexity when assessing true cost of ownership.
