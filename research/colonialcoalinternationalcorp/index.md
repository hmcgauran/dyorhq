## Business Model
Colonial Coal's core asset is the Steelmaking Coal joint venture (SMC), a producing metallurgical coal operation. The SMC produces premium PCI (pulverised coal injection) and coking coal used in steel blast furnaces. Unlike thermal coal, which is burned for power, metallurgical coal commands a significant price premium and is structurally more tightly supplied - new projects take 7-10 years to reach production, making supply inelastic. The company generates revenue from coal sales under offtake agreements, primarily to Japanese and South Korean steelmakers. Pricing is linked to the quarterly benchmark metallurgical coal price, which is volatile but has been supported by Chinese steel production, Australian weather disruptions, and a multi-year underinvestment cycle in new coal supply. The strategic value of Colonial Coal lies not just in the operating asset but in the substantial resource base - the Peace River coalfield is one of the few large, well-understood met coal basins in the Western world with established rail and port infrastructure. This makes it a logical consolidation target.
