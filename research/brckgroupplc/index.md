## Business Model
BRCK Group designs, manufactures, and sells hardware devices purpose-built for unreliable, high-latency, or physically demanding connectivity environments. The founding product, the BRCK router, was conceived in Kenya and designed to provide continuous connectivity in areas with unstable power grids and intermittent internet access - a genuine problem across large swathes of Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and Latin America. The product portfolio has expanded to include the Kito Cam line of ruggedised IoT cameras, which target surveillance and environmental monitoring applications in mining, agriculture, logistics, and conservation. The Kito Cams are designed to operate on solar power with cellular connectivity, addressing exactly the off-grid monitoring use case prevalent in emerging-market extractive industries and conservation projects. BRCK also offers its NOC (Network Operations Centre) backend platform, providing remote device management and monitoring as part of the subscription and service layer attached to hardware sales. The revenue model is hardware-led with a services component: BRCK sells devices directly to enterprise customers, NGOs, and government agencies, with after-sales support and NOC services generating recurring revenue. The business has historically derived significant revenue from development-sector contracts (USAID, DFID, and similar bilateral and multilateral funders active in emerging-market connectivity and conservation projects). The balance between direct enterprise sales and development-sector contracts is an important factor in assessing revenue predictability. The competitive positioning rests on purpose-built ruggedisation rather than consumer-grade hardware adapted for harsh environments. BRCK's devices are certified to operate across extreme temperature ranges, high humidity, and variable power conditions - specifications that mainstream networking hardware from Cisco, Ubiquiti, or Huawei does not explicitly target in this form factor.
