## Executive Summary
CVS Health is a vertically integrated healthcare company spanning retail pharmacy, pharmacy benefit management (PBM), and health insurance (Aetna); the combination of PBM margin pressure, medical benefit ratio deterioration, and retail headwinds makes REDUCE the appropriate call at $79.33.

## Business Model
CVS Health operates three integrated segments. Key revenue streams: Health Care Benefits (Aetna): Medical, dental, and pharmacy benefit plans for commercial, Medicare, and Medicaid populations. Revenue is premium-based; profitability depends on medical benefit ratio (MBR). Pharmacy & Consumer Wellness: Retail pharmacy dispensing, front-of-store retail, and MinuteClinic walk-in services. Scripts filled at ~9,000+ retail locations. Caremark PBM: Pharmacy benefit management for employers, unions, and health plans; negotiates rebates with drug manufacturers and manages drug spend. Rebate revenue is a key profitability component. The integrated model allows CVS to manage patients across the healthcare continuum - from PBM formulary design to retail clinic to insurance coverage - though execution across three complex businesses is operationally demanding.

## Key Risks
1. PBM regulatory risk: Proposed legislation requiring passthrough of manufacturer rebates to patients could fundamentally restructure PBM economics - the single largest structural risk.
2. Medical cost inflation: Aetna's medical benefit ratio (MBR = claims paid / premiums received) is the central profitability metric; rising medical trends directly compress earnings.
3. Retail pharmacy structural headwinds: script automation (mail order), vertical integration by insurers, and potential PBM reform all reduce CVS retail traffic independently.
4. Competition from Amazon/online pharmacies: Amazon Pharmacy and other online dispensaries are taking share in chronic disease maintenance medications.
5. Aetna integration execution: The Aetna acquisition was completed in 2018; ongoing integration challenges, provider network management, and care management investment requirements remain.
