## Business Model
Starbucks operates the world's largest chain of specialty coffee shops, with approximately 37,000 stores globally across company-owned and licensed formats. The company generates revenue through beverage sales (espresso-based drinks, cold brew, seasonal specials), food (bakery items, lunch offerings) and licensed store royalties. The Stored Value Card (gift card) programme is a significant source of free cash flow. The growing 'Third Place' concept aims to differentiate the in-store experience from quick-service competitors. Digital channels (mobile order and pay, delivery partnerships) now represent a substantial portion of US transactions.

## Recent Catalysts
Starbucks has undergone a significant US operational and cultural reset under new CEO Brian Niccol, who returned to the company in late 2024. The strategy prioritises in-store experience, partner (barista) working conditions and a slowdown of new store openings in saturated US markets. China, once the growth engine, has seen traffic declines as local competitors (Luckin Coffee, Cotti) expand aggressively with value pricing. The menu simplification initiative has removed lower-margin SKUs. However, the reset comes with elevated restructuring costs and near-term earnings pressure.
