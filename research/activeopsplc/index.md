## Business Model
Activeops sells its workforce management (WFM) platform on a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) basis, charging annual subscriptions per agent seat or module. The platform encompasses scheduling, attendance tracking, performance analytics, and compliance reporting. Clients include large financial services institutions, healthcare trusts, and retail organisations -- sectors with high regulatory requirements around rostering, attendance, and workforce planning. The business model is characterised by multi-year contract terms (typically 1-3 years) with high renewal rates. Land-and-expand dynamics are evident: clients initially deploy a single module (typically scheduling) and expand to additional modules over time, increasing average revenue per account. This creates a natural upsell pipeline and improves customer lifetime value. Revenue is substantially recurring. Geographic concentration is a feature: UK and ANZ together represent the majority of ARR. The ANZ operation is a particular strength, with a well-established distribution network and established relationships with major banking and government clients.
