## Key Risks
1. Memory cyclicality: Lam's revenue is heavily exposed to DRAM and NAND capex cycles. The memory market has historically been highly cyclical with dramatic upcycles and painful downcycles.
2. HBM concentration risk: While HBM is currently a major growth driver, it represents a small fraction of total DRAM bit demand. The durability of the HBM upcycle depends on sustained AI GPU demand.
3. China domestic substitution: China's equipment Self-Sufficiency drive (Made in China 2025) targets semiconductor capital equipment. This is a long-term headwind for Western equipment companies' China revenues.
4. Competitive dynamics: Applied Materials and Tokyo Electron are direct competitors in etch and deposition. Technological leadership shifts between competitors can be sudden.
5. Capex concentration risk: TSMC, Samsung and SK Hynix represent a disproportionate share of Lam's revenue. Any single customer's capex pivot has meaningful financial impact.
