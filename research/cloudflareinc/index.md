## Business Model
Cloudflare operates a consumption + subscription hybrid model. Enterprise customers pay annual subscriptions for security and performance services (DDoS protection, WAF, Zero Trust / Cloudflare One). Developers use the free Workers tier and convert to paid plans as usage scales. This creates a natural land-and-expand dynamic: a developer starts free, adds paid bandwidth, then the enterprise security team adopts Cloudflare One - creating two revenue streams from a single network relationship. The key quality metric is large enterprise growth: customers with over $100k ARR are growing faster than the SMB segment, reflecting the shift of Cloudflare from developer tool to enterprise-grade security platform. Net revenue retention above 120% indicates strong expansion within existing accounts. The competitive moat is the anycast network: routing all traffic through 300+ owned data centres creates geographic diversification and attack absorption that competitors cannot easily replicate. When Cloudflare absorbs the largest DDoS attacks in internet history, it validates the infrastructure advantage. Post-quantum cryptography leadership further extends this moat as quantum computing approaches - a long-duration competitive advantage.
