## Business Model
Arbuthnot Banking Group operates three complementary divisions targeting the UK high-net-worth segment: Arbuthnot Latham is the private banking arm, offering current accounts, deposits, mortgages, and specialist lending to HNW individuals and their associated businesses. The lending book is deliberately conservative, weighted towards residential and commercial mortgage finance secured against UK property. The Private Office provides holistic wealth management and financial planning for ultra-high-net-worth families, including investment strategy, estate planning, and tax-efficient structuring. Revenue is largely fee-based, generating predictable recurring income. Elevate Portfolio Management is the discretionary investment management arm, offering bespoke portfolio construction and management for clients seeking active allocation across equities, fixed income, and alternative assets. The group's revenue mix is weighted towards net interest income (from the lending and deposit-taking book) supplemented by recurring fee income from wealth management mandates. This hybrid model provides some inflation protection through margin adjustment while retaining the stickiness of long-term client relationships. The HNW focus means lower customer concentration risk than mainstream retail banks, though acquisition is slower and more relationship-dependent.

## Key Risks
1. UK macroeconomic deterioration (HIGH): Arbuthnot's lending book is secured against UK property. A sharp deterioration in UK house prices or commercial real estate values would directly impair the mortgage book and reduce capital ratios. The HNW client segment is not recession-proof - it is merely more resilient than mainstream retail.
2. Net interest margin compression (HIGH): As the Bank of England eases, the group's NIM will compress. The pace and magnitude of this compression is the single largest earnings risk. Unlike large banks with diversified income, Arbuthnot cannot offset margin pressure with fee income at scale.
3. Liquidity and register (MEDIUM): As a sub-GBP100m market cap stock, trading liquidity is thin. Institutional investors face position-sizing challenges, and the bid-offer spread deters active trading. This contributes to the persistent discount to book value.
4. Concentrated client base (MEDIUM): The HNW segment is by definition concentrated. While the group avoids excessive single-client concentration, a material loss of relationship managers or a key family office departure would have a disproportionate impact on fee income relative to a diversified wealth manager.
5. Regulatory capital requirements (MEDIUM): As a regulated bank, Arbuthnot faces ongoing capital adequacy requirements. Any material increase in risk-weighted assets or reduction in capital could restrict dividend payments or require capital raises that dilute existing shareholders.
