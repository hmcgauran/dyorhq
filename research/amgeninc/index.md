## Executive Summary

## Business Model
Amgen discovers, develops and manufactures biopharmaceuticals across several therapeutic areas: oncology (Prolia, Xgeva, Kyprolis, Blincyto), inflammation (Enbrel, Tezspire, Otezla), cardiovascular (Repatha, Evolocumab PCSK9 inhibitor), and nephrology (Aranesp). The company is notable for its pioneering role in the US biotech industry and its substantial manufacturing footprint, which it has leveraged to enter the biosimilar market with Amjevita (adalimumab biosimilar, referencing Humira). Amgen's pipeline includes MariTide (AMG 133), a GIPR/GLP-1 bispecific receptor agonist in Phase II for obesity - a potentially transformative asset that would position Amgen in the largest pharmaceutical market globally. The biosimilar strategy is a deliberate response to patent expirations on key biologics, generating revenue from differentiated but lower-margin versions of blockbuster reference products.

## Recent Catalysts
MariTide Phase II data readout showed compelling weight loss results (22.5% body weight reduction at highest dose at 52 weeks) - meaningfully competitive with semaglutide and tirzepatide benchmarks. This positions Amgen as a potential challenger in the GLP-1 obesity market, though Phase III trials and regulatory approval remain prerequisites. In the near term, Amgen's existing portfolio faces biosimilar competition for Prolia/Xgeva (romosozumab) in Europe and Tezspire faces IL-5 competition in severe asthma. The stock has re-rated upward since the MariTide data but remains below 52-week highs.

## Key Risks
1. MariTide Phase III failure: Phase II obesity data was encouraging but Phase III outcomes are binary. Failure to replicate Phase II weight loss efficacy or emergence of safety signals would eliminate the most important re-rating catalyst and likely trigger a significant stock decline.
2. GLP-1 competitive intensity: The obesity market will ultimately be contested by Eli Lilly (tirzepatide), Novo Nordisk (semaglutide), Pfizer (oral GLP-1), and others. Amgen's differentiation may not be sufficient to achieve blockbusters revenue given the entrenched incumbent advantages of Novo and Lilly in this therapeutic area.
3. Biosimilar erosion of key brands: Prolia/Xgeva (romosozumab for osteoporosis) faces imminent biosimilar competition. If biosimilar uptake is rapid, it could create a material revenue hole before MariTide can offset it.
4. Pipeline productivity risk: Beyond MariTide, Amgen's early-stage pipeline is less visible to markets. A high Phase III failure rate on any of the oncology or inflammation candidates would damage long-term earnings visibility.
5. Pricing and reimbursement pressure: US IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) price negotiation provisions could affect Amgen's Medicare revenue from 2026 onwards, suppressing net realised prices on established products.
