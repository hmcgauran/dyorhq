## Business Model
CrowdStrike sells its Falcon platform as a modular SaaS subscription: customers pay an annual subscription fee for individual modules (Endpoint Protection, Cloud Security, Identity Protection, ITDR, Data Security, AI/BIOME). The single-agent architecture means customers can add modules without deploying additional agents, creating a natural land-and-expand revenue dynamic. Revenue model is approximately 75% subscription, 20% professional services, 5% other. Net revenue retention of 115%+ is the key quality metric - existing customers are expanding their deployment by ~15% annually without new customer acquisition. The competitive moat is the data network effect: Falcon processes trillions of security events daily, training the AI models that power its detection engine. The more customers, the better the detection. This flywheel is difficult to replicate.
