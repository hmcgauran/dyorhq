## Business Model
M P Evans operates as an investment company with a primary focus on Indonesian palm oil plantations. Its assets comprise direct ownership stakes in plantation companies and associated processing infrastructure in Indonesia, together with a portfolio of listed equities in the palm oil and broader agriculture space. The core value driver is the underlying value of its plantation assets versus the market value of the listed entity. Plantation assets are valued on a combination of EBITDA multiples from disclosed results and independent valuations of land banks. The listed equities portfolio provides liquidity and a secondary return driver alongside the direct holdings. Revenue derives from plantation operations (crude palm oil and palm kernel sales), together with dividends and realised gains from the listed investments portfolio. The group reports annually and its NAV is assessed periodically by the board with independent verification. The December year-end means the annual report cycle concludes in the first quarter. The Indonesian operating environment introduces specific risks: land title complexity, replanting obligations, sustainability certification requirements (RSPO and ISPO), labour practices scrutiny, and regulatory uncertainty around export bans and pricing controls. These risks are structural to the sector and are reflected in the discount at which MPE trades relative to comparable ASEAN or Latin American plantation companies.

## Key Risks
1. CPO price volatility (HIGH): Palm oil prices are among the most volatile of any major agricultural commodity. A sustained fall in CPO prices directly compresses plantation margins. MPE has no meaningful hedging programme and is exposed to spot price movements. This is the single largest driver of earnings volatility.
2. Indonesian regulatory risk (HIGH): Indonesia accounts for the majority of global palm oil supply. Regulatory interventions including export bans, domestic market obligations, and export duty changes can cause sharp short-term price dislocations that affect MPE's earnings and NAV in ways that are difficult to predict or hedge.
3. ESG and deforestation pressure (MEDIUM-HIGH): European institutional investors apply significant ESG discounts to palm oil plantation owners. MPE's RSPO certification is necessary but not sufficient to neutralise this overhang. Any reported deforestation, land dispute, or labour practice incident could trigger a significant de-rating. This risk is sector-wide but particularly acute for Indonesian operators.
4. Currency risk (MEDIUM): MPE reports in GBP but operates primarily in IDR. The Indonesian Rupiah has been subject to depreciation pressure. A significant IDR depreciation against GBP would reduce translated earnings and NAV for GBP-reporting investors, all else equal.
5. Concentration risk (MEDIUM): As a small-cap investment company, MPE's NAV is sensitive to a small number of underlying holdings. Underperformance or impairment at the level of a key holding would have a disproportionate effect on group NAV.
