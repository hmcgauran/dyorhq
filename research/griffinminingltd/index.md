The mine is a long-life, multi-phase operation that has been producing for over a decade, with the orebody hosting significant zinc grades alongside gold and silver. This multi-commodity profile is the defining characteristic of the investment case: unlike a pure-play gold miner, Griffin receives a meaningful revenue contribution from zinc concentrate sales, which provides a degree of insulation from gold price volatility and effectively reduces the apparent all-in cost of gold production. Revenue is derived from the sale of gold dor bars, zinc concentrate, and silver. Gold is sold at prevailing market prices, typically via Chinese domestic channels or international offtake agreements. Zinc concentrate is sold to Chinese smelters under long-term offtake arrangements. The split between gold and zinc revenue has fluctuated with commodity prices, but zinc typically contributes 25-40% of total revenue depending on the zinc price environment.
