## Business Model
Hargreaves Services operates across three divisions: Rail Services: Asset management, logistics, and maintenance services delivered to Network Rail and train operators across the UK. This is the largest division by revenue. The business benefits from the inherently recurring, non-discretionary nature of rail maintenance expenditure - track, signalling, and infrastructure require ongoing upkeep that does not disappear in economic downturns. The UK rail network's upgrade and renewal programmes provide a steady pipeline of work. Mining Services: Provides services to the mining sector, primarily in the UK and internationally. The exposure here is more cyclical and commodity-price-sensitive than rail, and the margin profile tends to be lower. This division acts as a partial hedge: when commodity markets are strong, mining clients increase spending on services. Property: Management of the group's own property portfolio, generating rental income and providing some NAV upside optionality through development or disposal. The revenue model is contract-based, with a mix of framework agreements and project-specific work. Client concentration is a material risk - the rail division in particular is reliant on a small number of key customers in the UK rail ecosystem.
