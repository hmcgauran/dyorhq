## Business Model
Midwich Group operates as a trade-only specialist distributor, representing international AV, broadcast, and professional lighting equipment manufacturers to a customer base of professional installers, dealers, and rental companies. This trade-only model -- selling exclusively to qualified trade customers rather than end users -- is structurally important: it preserves vendor relationships and brand positioning while preventing direct price competition with the reseller channel. The business generates revenue from the margin spread between distributor purchase price and trade sell price, supplemented by vendor rebates and volume incentives that are an inherent part of the distribution model. The complexity of the proposition -- technical pre-sales support, logistics, demo equipment, and solution design assistance -- creates a degree of switching friction that protects the distributor relationship, though this is never a permanent moat. Key vendor relationships span the audio, video, broadcast, and lighting equipment supply chain. The international footprint -- UK, Ireland, Netherlands, Germany, France, Spain, and Australia -- provides the geographic reach that makes Midwich attractive to vendors seeking pan-European or international distribution without building their own infrastructure. This international scale is one of the group's more durable competitive advantages. The model is inherently cyclical: demand from integrators and rental companies tracks corporate capital expenditure cycles, live events activity, and broader economic confidence in professional AV infrastructure investment. The replacement cycle for installed AV equipment provides some floor, but cannot fully offset a sustained deterioration in new project activity.
