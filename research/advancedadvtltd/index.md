## Business Model
AdvancedAdvT operates at the intersection of programmatic advertising technology and data-driven marketing intelligence. Its product suite spans three interconnected layers: Demand-Side Platform (DSP): ADVT's core product. The DSP allows agencies and brands to purchase digital advertising inventory programmatically across multiple ad exchanges, SSPs (supply-side platforms), and publisher networks. The DSP aggregates inventory from thousands of publishers and apps and enables media buyers to target audiences, set bid strategies, and optimise campaigns in real time. Revenue is earned on a take-rate basis (a percentage of spend passing through the platform) and, increasingly, on a performance or retainer model with enterprise clients. Data Management Platform (DMP): ADVT's DMP collects, organises, and activates first-party and third-party audience data. In a post-cookie, post-GDPR regulatory environment, the ability to build, enrich, and activate audience segments from consented first-party data is increasingly valuable. The DMP enables brands to unify customer data across channels, create lookalike audiences, and feed activation signals back into the DSP for optimised targeting. DMP revenue is typically subscription- or licence-based. Attribution and Analytics Tools: ADVT's attribution layer measures the contribution of each touchpoint in a customer journey to a conversion, enabling clients to understand which channels, publishers, and creative executions actually drive business outcomes. This is ADVT's differentiation versus commoditised DSP competitors: the ability to close the loop between media spend and business result. Revenue models include SaaS subscriptions and professional services for implementation and reporting. The model is ultimately a technology licensing and.margin capture business: ADVT earns a margin on technology services rendered to media buyers, with limited balance sheet risk. The company does not hold inventory or carry significant receivables risk, as billing is typically on a pass-through basis with the DSP.
