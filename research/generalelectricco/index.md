## Business Model
General Electric is an industrial conglomerate focused on three core businesses following years of portfolio simplification: GE Aerospace: The crown jewel. Aircraft engines (LEAP, GE9X) and aviation systems for commercial and military aircraft. The LEAP engine (co-produced with Safran) powers the Airbus A320neo family and is in high-demand given the aircraft backlog. GE9X powers the Boeing 777X. This segment generates the highest margins and most durable growth in the portfolio. GE Vernova: Energy infrastructure including gas turbines, steam turbines, power conversion, and grid solutions. The business is a beneficiary of AI data-centre power demand and grid modernisation spending globally. GE Vernova is a separate publicly traded entity following its 2024 spinoff. GE HealthCare: Medical imaging equipment (MRI, CT, X-ray), ultrasound, and digital infrastructure for healthcare. Spun off in January 2023 as a separately traded company. GE retains a meaningful stake. The investment case centres on the GE Aerospace franchise - one of the few truly dominant industrial franchises globally, with a large installed base generating recurring aftermarket revenue from spare parts, maintenance, and upgrades.
