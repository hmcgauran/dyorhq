## Business Model
Rhi Office Group generates revenue through the supply and installation of office furniture, interior design services, and ongoing workspace optimisation contracts. The business serves corporate clients -- ranging from mid-size enterprises to large multinationals -- on a project and repeat-order basis. Revenue is denominated in euros and derived primarily from the Benelux region and France. The business model is essentially a distribution and installation model with design services layered on top. Margins are governed by supplier relationships, logistics efficiency, labour costs in Belgium and France, and the pricing power of the brand in competitive tender processes. There is limited recurring revenue outside of maintenance and repeat furniture orders; the bulk of revenue is project-based and therefore cyclical with corporate capital expenditure budgets. Key characteristics: low-technology, asset-light in manufacturing (outsourced to furniture manufacturers), higher-touch in design and project management. Competitive differentiation is driven by local relationships, project delivery track record, and breadth of product range rather than proprietary technology.
