## Executive Summary
AerCap is the largest listed aircraft lessor and its investment case is driven by fleet quality, lease yields, funding access and residual value discipline. The company benefits when airlines want capacity but prefer not to own aircraft outright, especially in a market where manufacturer delivery delays keep equipment tight. The question is less about top-line growth than about how long the current favourable supply-demand balance can support lease economics without a credit or travel downturn intervening.

## Business Model
AerCap buys commercial aircraft, engines and helicopters, then leases them to airlines and other operators on multi-year contracts. Scale matters because it improves purchase terms, fleet placement flexibility and funding access. The business is capital intensive and cyclical, but the current backdrop has been supportive because Boeing and Airbus delivery constraints have kept in-service aircraft valuable and extended lease demand for existing fleets.
