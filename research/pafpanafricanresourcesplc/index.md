## Business Model
Pan African Resources operates a single primary asset: the Barberton gold mine complex, located in the Barberton Greenstone Belt of Mpumalanga, South Africa. The Barberton operation consists of two primary underground mines - Fairview and New Consort - with associated processing infrastructure. The company produces gold in dore bar form, which is sold to refining houses and ultimately flows into the global gold market. The business model is straightforward: extract gold-bearing ore from underground operations, process it through a combination of mechanised and conventional mining methods, and sell the resulting gold at spot prices. Revenue is entirely gold-price-dependent, with no hedging of significance. The company does not operate across multiple commodities or jurisdictions - Barberton is the asset, and the investment case lives or dies with its performance. Production volumes are in the region of 150,000 to 180,000 ounces per annum, with all-in sustaining costs (AISC) typically in the USD900-USD1,100 per ounce range depending on the gold price environment and operational performance. The Barberton complex benefits from established infrastructure, a skilled local workforce, and a mineral resource base that extends beyond current mining operations. The orebody is mature but well-understood, with ongoing exploration and development activity targeting resource extension at depth and along strike. Cost structure is dominated by labour, electricity, and consumables - all rand-denominated. This means the company's cost base in USD terms is highly sensitive to ZAR/USD exchange rate movements. A weaker Rand acts as a natural hedge against input cost inflation and improves USD profitability at the same gold price. This asymmetry is a structural feature of the business model that is not always reflected in sell-side models.
