## Business Model
FW Thorpe designs, manufactures, and distributes specialist lighting fixtures and systems. Its products are sold under brand names including Thorpe, Zano, and others, and are characterised by high compliance requirements, long product lifecycles, and deep customer relationships with specifiers, contractors, and facilities managers in the UK and Europe. The product portfolio spans emergency lighting (a regulatory requirement in most commercial buildings), industrial IP-rated luminaires (dust and moisture protection for harsh environments), architectural lighting (high-specification visual design applications), and smart lighting systems (integration with building management systems, sensor-driven control, and LED efficiency upgrades). Revenue is generated through a mix of direct specification relationships with architects and engineers, distribution through electrical wholesalers, and tender business with public-sector clients. Customer relationships tend to be sticky: switching costs are non-trivial given the compliance documentation, testing, and certification associated with specialist lighting installations. The business is not a pure-play smart building story. LED retrofits and smart controls represent a growing share of revenue but sit alongside the core replacement cycle business for conventional emergency and industrial luminaires. Margins are under modest pressure from raw material and energy costs, partially offset by operational efficiency improvements and product mix towards higher-value smart systems.
