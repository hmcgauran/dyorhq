## Business Model
Niox generates revenue across two distinct streams: device sales and recurring consumables. The Niox Vero hardware is sold to healthcare providers, pharmacies, and specialist respiratory clinics. The consumables revenue — consisting of single-use sensor cartridges and related accessories — is the higher-margin, annuity-like component of the model. This consumables re-order cycle is the financial anchor of the business, providing predictable recurring revenue and strong customer retention. The commercial model varies by geography. In the UK, Niox sells directly to NHS trusts, GP practices, and pharmacy chains through a combination of direct sales and specialist distributors. In Europe and the United States, commercial activity is structured through distributor relationships and, in selected territories, direct sales engagement with major healthcare systems and integrated delivery networks. The company has invested in regulatory clearance breadth, with the Niox Vero and associated consumables holding clearances across multiple major markets including the UK, major European Union member states, and the United States. This regulatory platform provides optionality for market expansion and reduces dependence on any single geography. Research and development is directed at expanding the software capabilities of the device platform, improving connectivity and integration with electronic health record systems, and developing next-generation sensor technology. The company has historically maintained a disciplined approach to operating cost management, targeting positive adjusted operating profit in all meaningful periods.
