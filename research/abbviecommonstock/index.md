## Business Model
AbbVie is a biopharmaceutical company focused on immunology, oncology, neuroscience and aesthetics. Its flagship product, Humira (adalimumab), was the world's best-selling drug for a decade, generating over $20 billion annually at peak, but has faced biosimilar competition in the US since January 2023. AbbVie has managed this cliff through Skyrizi (risankizumab, IL-23 inhibitor) and Rinvoq (upadacitinib, JAK inhibitor), both of which have demonstrated superior clinical profiles in immunology and are growing rapidly. The Immunology segment accounts for approximately 55% of total revenues. The Botox, Juvederm and other aesthetic products form theAllergan (Allergan) aesthetics franchise. The acquisition of ImmunoGen (targeting antibody-drug conjugates in oncology) adds an emerging ADC platform. Cash generation remains strong, supporting AbbVie's dividend and debt service obligations from theAllergan acquisition.

## Key Risks
1. Skyrizi/Rinvoq execution risk: These two drugs must collectively replace $20+ billion of annual Humira revenue. Any clinical setback, safety signal, payer pressure or competitive entry that slows uptake directly impairs the investment thesis.
2. JAK class safety risk: JAK inhibitors carry boxed warnings for serious cardiovascular events and cancer. Regulatory scrutiny could tighten prescribing labels, benefiting competing IL-inhibitor mechanisms (Skyrizi's advantage over Rinvoq in safety profile may erode over time).
3. Valuation sensitivity: At 88x P/E, AbbVie is priced for near-perfect execution. A single quarter of disappointing guidance could produce significant multiple compression, as the market reprices the timeline for Humira replacement.
4. ImmunoGen/ADC platform uncertainty: The ADC space is crowded with competitors (Roche, AstraZeneca/Daiichi, Gilead). Elahere's commercial trajectory and the broader platform's productivity remain uncertain.
5. Aesthetics cyclicality: TheAllergan aesthetics franchise is discretionary and sensitive to consumer sentiment and economic conditions. A consumer slowdown disproportionately affects Botox and filler revenues.
