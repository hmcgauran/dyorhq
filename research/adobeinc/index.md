## Executive Summary
Adobe's shift to subscription cloud Creative Cloud and the expansion into digital experience with Experience Manager and Marketo represent a structural transition, but generative AI disruption to its core creative tools market poses a genuine long-term threat to revenue durability - HOLD is the weighted recommendation.

## Business Model
Adobe operates three main segments. Digital Media includes Creative Cloud (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere, After Effects and the full Adobe suite) and Document Cloud (Acrobat, Acrobat Sign, and the Adobe Express platform). These are sold via annual subscription, generating highly predictable recurring revenues from over 30 million paid members. Digital Experience covers the Adobe Experience Platform, Marketo, and Workfront - enterprise marketing and customer experience management tools. The subscription transition completed years ago; the key question now is whether the secular tailwind of digital content creation continues to accelerate, and whether Adobe's AI features (Firefly generative AI) are sufficiently differentiated to retain and expand its installed base. The Document Cloud opportunity - turning PDFs and documents into actionable digital workflows - is underpenetrated and growing. However, the competitive landscape in creative tools has materially intensified with AI-native competitors offering comparable capabilities at lower price points.

## Recent Catalysts
Adobe's Figma competing product - called Adobe XD - has failed to gain meaningful market share against Figma's browser-native collaborative design tool, which was itself briefly subject to a terminated Adobe acquisition attempt blocked by regulators. The acquisition termination was a significant strategic setback. Adobe has since invested heavily in Firefly, its own generative AI model, embedding it across Creative Cloud and Document Cloud. Early adoption metrics have been positive, but monetisation of AI features is still in early stages. Recent quarters have shown revenue growth decelerating to mid-single digits as the pandemic-era creative boom fades.

## Key Risks
1. Generative AI creative tools disruption: This is the primary structural risk. AI image/video generation tools (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, Runway) and AI design tools (Figma, Canva's AI suite) are rapidly commoditising capabilities that Adobe has monetised for decades. The threat is not imminent but the trajectory is concerning.
2. ARPU and churn dynamics: Adobe has historically grown EPS through a combination of subscriber growth and price increases. If AI tools satisfy casual users' needs at lower price points, Adobe's pricing power in its core market weakens.
3. Figma and competitive design tools: Figma's collaborative, browser-native design tool has won significant market share among product designers and is now embedding AI features. Adobe XD has not been a viable competitive response. This represents a genuine competitive loss in a high-value segment.
4. Digital Experience competitive intensity: The marketing cloud market (Adobe Experience Platform vs Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Braze, and others) is highly competitive. Enterprise deals are long sales cycles and subject to budget pressure in economic downturns.
5. Valuation re-rating risk: The stock has re-rated downward from 50x+ P/E at peak to 13x today. While this appears cheap, it reflects legitimate concerns about long-term earnings durability in the face of AI disruption.
