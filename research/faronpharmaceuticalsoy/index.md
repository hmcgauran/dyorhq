## Business Model
Faron is a pure-play clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company. It has no marketed products and no commercial revenue. The company operates from Helsinki, Finland, with development operations extending across Europe and into US clinical trial sites. Its strategy is to develop bexmarilimab through proof-of-concept in haematological malignancies, demonstrating clinical activity before seeking a licensing or acquisition partnership with a larger pharma entity. The Clever-1 (CD163) target is expressed on tumour-promoting M2 macrophages and represents a distinct mechanism from checkpoint inhibitors targeting T-cell pathways. Bexmarilimab is designed to deplete or re-polarise these immunosuppressive macrophages, restoring anti-tumour immune surveillance. This mechanism has potential synergy with existing checkpoint inhibitors and may be applicable across a broad range of solid tumours. Faron's business model is entirely dependent on external financing or partnership milestones. The company has historically used equity raises, including subscription arrangements, to fund operations. The company does not operate a generic revenue-producing business. Investors should understand this is an asset-valuation story, not a multiples story.
