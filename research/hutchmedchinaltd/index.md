The company operates a hybrid model: a commercial-stage revenue base in China, balanced against the significantly larger addressable opportunity as it builds out US and global commercial presence. ## Business Model
HUTCHMED operates across two distinct value chains: the established China commercial business and the emerging US/global franchise anchored on fruquintinib. In China, surufatinib is approved for two indications: advanced neuroendocrine tumours (NET) of pancreatic and non-pancreatic origin, and advanced biliary tract cancer (BTC). Fruquintinib is approved in China for advanced metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) under the brand name Orpathys. These approvals generate product revenue through the China commercial infrastructure, with sales distributed via standard hospital and pharmacy channels. In the US, fruquintinib received FDA approval in November 2023 for adult patients with refractory mCRC - making it the first and only domestically-developed Chinese novel oncology drug to receive US approval. This is a landmark achievement, but commercialising it in the US market requires building a sales force, market access infrastructure, and brand presence from scratch. This is where the company is still in investment mode. The pipeline extends beyond the two lead assets, with several earlier-stage candidates in oncology and immunology. The company is also actively pursuing partnership discussions for ex-China licensing of fruquintinib. The revenue model is product sales in China, supplemented by milestone payments from partnership deals. R&D spend is substantial and ongoing, reflecting the multi-indication development strategy for the lead assets and the maintenance of the early-stage pipeline.
