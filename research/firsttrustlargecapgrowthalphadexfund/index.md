## Business Model
First Trust Portfolios, the fund sponsor, operates FTC as a single-factor enhanced index fund. The underlying S&P 500 AlphaDEX Growth Index applies a proprietary ranking methodology to the S&P 500 universe, selecting the top-scoring growth stocks based on: (1) price momentum, (2) earnings growth momentum, (3) return on equity, and (4) management change signals. The fund holds approximately 200-300 stocks across sectors, with a deliberate growth and momentum tilt relative to the cap-weighted S&P 500 benchmark. FTC distributes dividends quarterly, has an expense ratio of approximately 0.60-0.70% (confirmed against current First Trust ETF fee schedules), and tracks the index using physical replication. The fund's AlphaDEX methodology is distinct from passive cap-weighted indexing: it systematically overweights stocks with positive growth and momentum signals while underweighting those that score poorly on those dimensions. The result is a portfolio with higher turnover and higher sector active weights compared to a standard S&P 500 ETF, with performance that diverges materially in factor-driven market environments.
