## Business Model
Costco operates on a high-volume, low-margin membership warehouse model that creates a near-unbreakable competitive moat: Membership Fees: The core economic engine. Costco charges an annual membership fee (Gold Star $65, Business $65, Executive $130) that is nearly pure profit. With ~76 million paid members and a renewal rate exceeding 93 per cent, this is one of the most predictable revenue streams in retail. Warehouse Sales: A curated selection of approximately 4,000 SKUs across groceries, electronics, appliances, apparel, tyres, and home goods. Costco sells at thin gross margins (approximately 11-12 per cent net of membership benefit) but extremely high inventory turnover. E-commerce and ancillary services: Costco.com for home delivery, Costco Travel, optical centres, hearing aid centres, pharmacies, and food courts - all serving to deepen member engagement and increase visit frequency. Kirkland Signature: The proprietary Kirkland Signature brand delivers higher-margin private-label products across food, apparel, and household goods - increasingly important to margin stability. Costco's genius is using ultra-low prices to attract members, then generating nearly all its operating profit from membership fees while the merchandise sales cover costs. It is one of the most capital-efficient retail models ever constructed.
