## Business Model
Fintel operates as a B2B information and workflow services provider to UK retail financial advisers and mortgage brokers. The core revenue streams are: Mortgage Support Services provides mortgage research, product sourcing, and suitability tools used by advisers in their client work. This is the most established product line and the primary revenue contributor. Advisers use the platform to search across lender products, run suitability checks, and manage ongoing client cases. Retail Distribution Services covers compliance support, training, and regulatory content for financial advisers. Given the ongoing regulatory burden on UK advisers under FCA rules, this division benefits from a structural compliance need. Advisers must stay current on regulation, making this a recurring utility spend. Moneycompare.com is a price comparison website covering financial products. Revenue here is primarily affiliate-led: Fintel earns commissions when users click through to underlying product providers. This is a lower-margin, more traffic-dependent business than the subscription lines. The subscription model for the first two divisions provides reasonable revenue visibility. Customer retention is aided by the switching costs associated with adviser workflow software and the regulatory dependency of the compliance/training products.
