## Business Model
Dotdigital operates on a SaaS subscription model, charging clients an annual platform fee based on the number of contacts in their database and the modules activated. Core revenue streams are email marketing automation, SMS messaging, push notification capabilities, and a customer data platform (CDP) that aggregates and unifies customer data across channels. The platform is designed for mid-market clients - businesses too large for basic email tools but not enterprise-scale enough to require Salesforce-level customisation. Target sectors include retail, e-commerce brands, travel and hospitality, and B2B organisations. Geographic exposure is predominantly UK and Europe, with meaningful presence in the US and Australia. Revenue is approximately 80% recurring subscription and 20% professional services and variable usage (SMS at volume being the main variable component). Net revenue retention is the key quality metric to track - at above 100% the business is expanding existing customer wallets; below 100% signals churn problems. Customers typically sign annual contracts, providing a degree of revenue visibility. The competitive moat is shallow. Dotdigital competes on ease of use, mid-market pricing, and customer service, but none of these are durable advantages against a determined competitor with greater resources. The CDP play is the most strategically important product development: if executed well it creates a data asset that improves retention and allows upsell across the engagement stack.
