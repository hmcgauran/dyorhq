## Business Model
Accesso provides technology infrastructure for attractions, venues, and leisure destinations across three core product pillars: virtual queuing systems (including the flagship Qless platform), e-ticketing and admission management, and point-of-sale payments. A fourth leg, mobile applications, extends the platform to visitor-facing experiences. The Qless virtual queuing product is the most differentiated: it allows venues to manage walk-up and advance queues digitally, reducing physical wait times and improving visitor satisfaction scores. For operators, it provides data on walk-up demand patterns that informs staffing and pricing decisions. The business model transition from hardware to SaaS has been underway for several years and is now the majority of revenue. Customers pay annual or multi-year subscription fees, improving revenue visibility. Professional services and transaction-based fees (per ticket or per transaction) provide additional revenue streams, the latter creating a usage-revenue link that is directly exposed to visitor volume at client sites. Geographic revenue split: approximately 60% United States, with meaningful positions in the UK and continental Europe. The US predominance reflects the scale of the American theme park and attractions market and Accesso's penetration of major operators there.
