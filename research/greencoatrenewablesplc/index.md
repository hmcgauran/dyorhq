## Business Model
Greencoat Renewables PLC acquires and holds operational onshore wind farms in Ireland and the UK. Its portfolio is structured to deliver stable, long-term revenues through a combination of two revenue streams: OREG (Ophel gate agreements): Long-term fixed-rate contracts, typically 15-20 years, that guarantee a fixed price per megawatt hour (MWh) of electricity generated. These agreements are the bedrock of the revenue model, providing revenue visibility that is largely independent of spot power prices. Power prices and renewable energy certificates: Above the OREG fixed price, wind farm revenues benefit from market power prices and the value of Renewable Energy Guarantees of Origin (REGOs) or equivalent certificates. When power prices are elevated, this component can be substantial and provides material upside. The investment company structure means GRP does not develop or build wind farms - it acquires operational assets from developers and operators seeking to exit. This limits upside from greenfield development but eliminates construction risk. The manager's skill lies in asset selection, contract structuring, and active portfolio management. As a closed-ended investment company, GRP retains earnings to reinvest in new acquisitions, grow the portfolio, and smooth dividends through economic cycles. The board targets a sustainable dividend covered by portfolio revenues.
