## Business Model
Virgin Galactic was founded by Richard Branson in 2004 as a developer of commercial suborbital spaceflight vehicles. The original programme operated SpaceShipTwo (VSS Unity), a suborbital, air-launched Spaceplane carrying up to six passengers and two pilots. Flights launched from Spaceport America in New Mexico, with the carrier aircraft (WhiteKnightTwo / VMS Eve) lifting the Spaceplane to altitude before release and rocket-powered ascent to the edge of space. Ticket prices were set at USD 450,000 per seat, generating significant consumer interest but limited revenue given the extended development timeline. In 2021, a mid-flight accident involving VSS Unity grounded the programme permanently. The company subsequently pivoted to a new vehicle class (Delta class), intended to be fully reusable, with a larger payload capacity and improved safety architecture. Revenue generation is anticipated from space tourism, research missions, and government contracts, but no commercial flights have been completed as at the date of this report. The company is loss-making with no meaningful operating revenue.
