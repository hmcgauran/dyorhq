## Business Model
The Pebble Group operates across two distinct but complementary divisions. Pebble FX handles promotional merchandise and branded items -- the kind of imprinted corporate gifts, event giveaways, and branded consumables that businesses use for client engagement, staff recognition, and marketing campaigns. PCP Workwear supplies workwear, safety footwear, and personal protective equipment to commercial and industrial clients. Both divisions share a common distribution model: procurement at scale from manufacturers, warehousing, and fulfilment to business customers. The model is asset-light in manufacturing but requires working capital for inventory and receivables. Customer relationships are typically B2B frameworks with recurring purchase patterns, particularly in workwear where safety regulation creates genuine re-buy demand. The competitive landscape is highly fragmented. There are hundreds of promotional merchandise distributors in the UK and Europe ranging from micro-businesses to larger national operators. Scale advantages are limited; the primary competitive levers are supplier relationships, product range breadth, digital procurement platforms, and account management quality. In workwear, there is more differentiation potential around certification, compliance knowledge, and managed inventory services. The group's strategic focus appears to be on growing the PCP Workwear division given its more predictable recurring revenue profile, while managing Pebble FX for margin stability in a more competitive promotional merchandise market.
