The trust's investment objective is long-term total return, combining capital growth with income generation through a blended portfolio. As a listed investment trust, POLR trades at a premium or discount to net asset value (NAV). That discount/premium dynamic is itself an investment variable - the trust can repurchase shares at a discount to intrinsic value, which is accretive to remaining shareholders when the board authorises buybacks. The management fee structure and annual charges are a key determinant of long-term net return. Investment trusts have a perpetual structure, which eliminates maturity risk but requires ongoing discipline from the manager to maintain portfolio quality without the pressure of callable debt or finite fund life.
