## Business Model
Caledonia Mining generates revenue entirely from gold production at the Blanket Mine in the Gwanda Greenstone Belt, Zimbabwe. The mine is an underground operation with a long history, having produced gold continuously for decades. The company sells gold at prevailing market prices, with revenue denominated in USD but reported in GBP on the LSE. The Blanket Mine operates a trackless mechanised underground mining method, processing ore through a conventional carbon-in-leach (CIL) plant. The operation has a nameplate capacity of approximately 100,000 ounces per year, though actual production has tracked in the 65,000-80,000 ounce range in recent years, reflecting grade variability and equipment availability. Caledonia holds a 64% interest in Blanket Mine, with the remaining interest held by a Zimbabwean indigenous empowerment entity as required by local regulations. Revenue is entirely gold-price dependent. There is no hedging programme of note, meaning the company is a direct leveraged play on the gold price. All-in sustaining costs (AISC) are the critical operational metric. At current AISC levels in the range of USD 1,100-1,300 per ounce against a gold price that has spent most of 2025-2026 above USD 2,800 per ounce, the margin structure is robust.

## Key Risks
1. Zimbabwe country risk (HIGH): Zimbabwe carries material regulatory, currency, and political risk. The Indigenisation Act requires majority indigenous ownership of mining assets. While Caledonia has complied and retains operational control at 64%, any change in the ownership requirement or government stance could dilute the company or restrict repatriation of dividends. The political environment is unpredictable and the rule of law is weak by developed-market standards.
2. Gold price dependence (HIGH): With no hedging and revenue entirely tied to the gold price, CMCL is a high-beta gold play. A 20% correction in gold would compress margins dramatically and likely force a dividend review. Investors should size accordingly and monitor the gold price constantly.
3. Ore body maturity and grade risk (MEDIUM): Blanket Mine is an aging underground operation. Grade variability is a known issue. If head grades fall below the levels assumed in the business plan, AISC will rise and production will fall short of guidance. This is the primary operational risk at the asset level.
4. Currency and inflation risk (MEDIUM): While revenue is USD-denominated, local operating costs are incurred in ZWG and USD-linked expenses. Currency volatility and local inflation create cost uncertainty. A sharp depreciation in the ZWG could cause local cost inflation that partially offsets the USD revenue benefit.
5. Dividend sustainability (MEDIUM): A 10% yield on a market-cap of £200m company implies annual dividend payments of roughly £20m. At a gold price of USD 2,800/oz and 70,000 oz production, gross revenue is approximately USD 196m. After AISC, capital expenditure, and Zimbabwe withholding tax on dividends, cover on the dividend requires scrutiny. A detailed cash flow analysis is warranted before treating the yield as secure.
