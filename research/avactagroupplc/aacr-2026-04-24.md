# Avacta Group plc — AACR 2026 Presentation Summary

**Date:** 24 April 2026
**Source:** AACR Annual Meeting, San Diego — dual payload (AVA6207) presentation
**Speaker(s):** Francis Wilson, David Jones, Chris (Avacta)

---

## Technology

- FAP-cleavable peptide drug conjugate releasing **two payloads simultaneously**: topoisomerase 1 inhibitor + ATR inhibitor
- Single FAP cleavage event → two payloads released in tumour microenvironment
- FAP expressed in ~90% of solid tumours (1+ to 3+)
- Tumour-selective delivery: robust payload deposition in tumour vs plasma (therapeutic window argument)

## Bystander Effect

- Cleaved payload taken up by FAP+ stromal cells AND FAP- tumour cells
- Rapid tumour penetration, sustained release mechanism

## Synthetic Lethality Signals

- **ATM-deficient background** → greater cell kill with dual payload (DNA damage + blocked repair)
- **Schlafen11 knockout/reduced** → improved cell kill over topoisomerase 1 alone
- Both exploitable resistance mechanisms in human tumours

## In Vivo Efficacy

- **FAP-overexpression model:** complete tumour growth inhibition (3 doses, weekly)
- **HER2+ gastric PDX:** outperformed Enhertu (direct comparison — first time Avacta has done this)
- **HCFAP model:** rapid complete responses with dual payload compounds

## Key Conclusions from Presentation

1. AVA6207: dual payload (topoisomerase 1 + ATR inhibitor) from single FAP cleavage
2. Synthetic lethality demonstrated in ATM-deficient and Schlafen11-deficient models
3. PK: robust tumour-selective release, very high tumour:plasma AUC ratio
4. In vivo: tumour growth inhibition, complete responses, beats Enhertu in gastric PDX
5. Potential to dramatically increase therapeutic window and reduce systemic toxicities vs combination therapy
