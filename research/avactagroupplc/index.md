## Executive Summary

Avacta is a clinical-stage oncology company built around its pre|CISION platform — a tumour-targeted prodrug activation technology that releases active drug preferentially in the tumour microenvironment rather than systemically. The core technology uses Fibroblast Activation Protein (FAP), expressed in the stroma of approximately 90% of solid tumours, as the activation trigger. The platform's core claim — FAP-mediated tumour concentration with reduced systemic toxicity — has now been validated in humans: zero cardiac toxicity at 4x standard doxorubicin dosing, with a 90% disease control rate in chemorefractory salivary gland cancer. The lifetime doxorubicin dose cap has been formally removed by both MHRA and FDA. FOCUS-01 (AVA6103) is active with first patient dosed March 2026, in six tumour types. Initial clinical signal expected H2 2026. The chemistry team has demonstrated sustained half-life extension (45 min to 5 days) and dual-payload capability (DAR 2:1), positioning the platform beyond a single-programme story. Bottom line: SPECULATIVE BUY at 71.90p. Conviction 86/100. The binary character of the investment remains — partnership execution, Ph1 data, and pivotal trial risk are still ahead — but the data quality has improved meaningfully across every dimension: cardiac safety validated, efficacy signal strengthened, pipeline breadth expanded, and platform optionality demonstrated. Size positions for binary outcome.

## Business Model

Avacta's value is entirely in its pre|CISION platform and the clinical pipeline it generates. The platform activates cancer drugs specifically in the tumour microenvironment using FAP as the enzymatic trigger. FAP is expressed on cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs) in the tumour stroma of most epithelial solid tumours — not on the tumour cells themselves. This stromal targeting approach means the technology is tumour-agnostic in principle, though clinical efficacy is ultimately payload-dependent. The key scientific advance is the capping group plus self-immolative linker technology, which controls the rate of drug release in the tumour microenvironment. This is what enables sustained release rather than burst release — the five-day half-life achieved with AVA6103 versus 45 minutes with the first-generation AVA6000 being a concrete demonstration of this control.

The platform is now expanding in two directions simultaneously. The first is broader tumour coverage through optimised payloads: AVA6000 (doxorubicin) has demonstrated 90% disease control in salivary gland cancer, TNBC, ovarian, and soft tissue sarcoma, with no activity seen in pancreatic cancer — a failure of payload sensitivity (doxorubicin does not work in pancreatic cancer), not FAP expression. This is a critical and honest platform learning: FAP expression alone does not guarantee efficacy across all tumour types. The second direction is entirely new payload classes: AVA6103 uses exatecan (a topoisomerase-1 inhibitor, same class as the highly active trastuzumab deruxtecan), and AVA6207 is the first dual-payload peptide drug conjugate (DAR 2:1, two drugs on one peptide), developed in response to a BD partner asking whether it was possible.

The commercial model is most likely a partnership for AVA6000 given the 24-person company size would require significant hiring to run pivotal trials independently. Active partnering conversations are ongoing. A US listing remains a strategic option but is less urgent if a partnership delivers upfront cash.

## Recent Catalysts

**Lifetime dose cap removed (MHRA and FDA, April 2026):** Both regulators have formally approved protocol amendments removing the doxorubicin lifetime dose cap for AVA6000. This is the single most significant de-risking event since the programme began. Zero cardiac toxicity has been observed across all patients dosed to date. The CEO stated explicitly: "We've now eliminated the lifetime maximum because guess how much cardiac toxicity we see? Zero. Nothing."

**90% disease control rate in salivary gland cancer:** Materially stronger than any prior public disclosure. These are chemorefractory patients with limited treatment options — a 90% DCR is a compelling efficacy signal. Full data readout expected at a medical conference in H1 2026.

**FOCUS-01 Phase 1 active (AVA6103, first patient dosed March 2026):** IND cleared January 2026. Three specialist oncology centres open in the US. Six tumour types now included: small cell lung, gastric, cervical, pancreatic, colorectal, and HR+ breast cancer. The two new indications (CRC and HR+ BC) were added in April 2026 based on Tempus LENS biomarker data. Clinical signal expected H2 2026.

**AVA6103 vs Enhertu preclinical data (AACR April 2026):** Using an AI synthetic comparator arm matched to AstraZeneca's published dataset, AVA6103 demonstrated: tumor Cmax more than one log higher than Enhertu; Tumor Selectivity Index (TSI) 3x higher than Enhertu; robust complete responses in multiple PDX models; activity even at low FAP expression. This validates the FAP-targeting thesis in a rigorous comparative framework.

**AVA6207 dual-payload (TOP1i + ATRi):** First in vivo efficacy data presented at AACR April 2026. The dual payload overcomes TOP1i resistance mechanisms (ATM deficiency and SLFN11 loss both create ATR dependency — hitting both simultaneously creates synthetic lethality). Gastric HER2+ PDX (FAP-low) outperformed Enhertu. IND-enabling studies expected late 2026/early 2027.

**AVA6000 tolerates 4x the standard doxorubicin dose:** Patients can receive four times the doxorubicin dose they would normally tolerate. Phase 1b expansion is ongoing. Project Optimus feedback from FDA has been received and incorporated.

**AVA6000 partnering conversations active:** A 24-person company would need to hire significantly to run pivotal trials alone. Partnership discussions are the most likely path to monetise AVA6000 and fund the broader pipeline.
