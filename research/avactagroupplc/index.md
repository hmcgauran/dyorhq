## Business Model
Avacta's value is entirely in its pre|CISION platform and the clinical pipeline it generates. The platform activates cancer drugs specifically in the tumour microenvironment using FAP as the enzymatic trigger. FAP is expressed on cancer-associated fibroblasts (CAFs) in the tumour stroma of most epithelial solid tumours - not on the tumour cells themselves. This stromal targeting approach means the technology is tumour-agnostic in principle, though clinical efficacy is ultimately payload-dependent. The key scientific advance is the capping group plus self-immolative linker technology, which controls the rate of drug release in the tumour microenvironment. This is what enables sustained release rather than burst release - the five-day half-life achieved with AVA6103 versus 45 minutes with the first-generation AVA6000 being a concrete demonstration of this control. The platform is now expanding in two directions simultaneously. The first is broader tumour coverage through optimised payloads: AVA6000 (doxorubicin) has demonstrated 90% disease control in salivary gland cancer, TNBC, ovarian, and soft tissue sarcoma, with no activity seen in pancreatic cancer - a failure of payload sensitivity (doxorubicin does not work in pancreatic cancer), not FAP expression. This is a critical and honest platform learning: FAP expression alone does not guarantee efficacy across all tumour types. The second direction is entirely new payload classes: AVA6103 uses exatecan (a topoisomerase-1 inhibitor, same class as the highly active trastuzumab deruxtecan), and AVA6207 is the first dual-payload peptide drug conjugate (DAR 2:1, two drugs on one peptide), developed in response to a BD partner asking whether it was possible. The commercial model is most likely a partnership for AVA6000 given the 24-person company size would require significant hiring to run pivotal trials independently. Active partnering conversations are ongoing. A US listing remains a strategic option but is less urgent if a partnership delivers upfront cash.
