# AVA6207 AACR 2026 Abstract #5656

**Source:** AACR Annual Meeting 2026 poster presentation
**Link:** https://avacta.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/AACR-2026-AVA6207.pdf

## What It Is
FAP-activated dual payload: TOP1i (topoisomerase I inhibitor) + ATRi (ATR inhibitor), released simultaneously by a single FAP cleavage event.

## Synthetic Lethality Rationale
TOP1i resistance arises via two mechanisms — ATM deficiency or SLFN11 loss. Both make cells more reliant on ATR DNA damage repair pathway. Hitting both simultaneously creates synthetic lethality.

## Key Efficacy Data
- **Gastric PDX (FAP-low, HER2+):** Dual payload outperformed Enhertu
- **FAP-high HEK293T-FAP CDX:** Rapid complete responses
- **3D spheroid co-culture (MDA-MB-231 + primary human mammary fibroblasts):** Strong FAP-dependent tumour cell killing vs tumour cells alone
- **SLFN11 KO cells:** Dual payload forced cells into premature mitotic entry (mitotic catastrophe = cell death), even when TOP1i alone was ineffective

## Pharmacokinetics
Both payloads showed high intratumoral concentrations at 4h, 24h, 48h. Plasma levels far lower than tumour levels — high TSI.

## Important Caveats
- HEK293T-FAP is an engineered FAP-overexpression model — not representative of endogenous FAP in real tumours
- Gastric PDX comparison to Enhertu: not a true head-to-head (Enhertu i.v., compounds s.c., different dosing schedules)
- Still pre-clinical/IND-enabling stage
