### The pre|CISION Mechanism of Action

The mechanism of action of the pre|CISION peptide-drug conjugates: the peptide is cleavable specifically by an enzyme, fibroblast activation protein or FAP, which is expressed in the fibroblasts or the cancer-associated fibroblasts that exist in the vast majority of solid tumors. FAP specifically removes the peptide. The free payload is then released and moves into the FAP-negative tumour cells, mediating a bystander effect. The cleavage happens extracellularly; payload is released into the extracellular space and moves into FAP-negative tumour cells.

---

### Large Market Opportunity for the pre|CISION Platform

**Rory Edwards:** The Tempus data helped us understand patient populations that express FAP. Tempus dataset contains about 160,000 solid tumour samples. Around 90% of solid tumours express FAP and are FAP-positive. Broad patient population for both the pre|CISION platform as a whole and the AVA6103 program.

---

### The pre|CISION PDC Platform Addresses Challenges in the ADC Field

**Christina Coughlin:** Four key advantages over traditional cathepsin-based cleavage mechanism of ADCs:

1. **Tumor-specific release** — following AVA6000 clinical data, payload is specifically released in the tumour. None of the toxicities that have plagued other traditional ADCs due to non-specific release.

2. **Very rapid penetration into tumours** — payload release within minutes as opposed to very slow uptake kinetics with an ADC. Gets a high Cmax in the tumour at the time of initial delivery.

3. **Extracellular vs internalisation-based payload release** — ADCs require internalisation of the antibody and extrusion of the released payload. pre|CISION is an extracellular payload release.

4. **Broad market opportunity** — goal with AVA6103 was to take forward a payload with broader reach in solid tumours and more potent.

---

### Gen Two pre|CISION (AVA6103): Optimizing Payload Delivery Kinetics with Sustained Release Mechanism

**Curtis Rink:** The image shows 6103 docked into the FAP enzymatic pocket. Two key areas: the capping group and the self-immolative linker.

- **Capping group:** Sits in the binding pocket of FAP to enable optimal binding affinity. Held in the binding site to enable sustained release of the warhead. Also important for ADME and PK properties — enhances plasma protein binding and reduces the rate of clearance.

- **Self-immolative linker:** Required to achieve FAP cleavability with the pre|CISION peptide. Allows variation of the rate of cleavage — by slowing the rate of enzyme efficiency, sustained release from FAP in the tumour microenvironment is achieved.

---

### AVA6103 Demonstrates FAP Enzymatic Selectivity Across Panel of DASH Subfamily Members and PREP

AVA6103 is highly specific for FAP enzyme. Incubation with a panel of DASH enzymes (structurally and mechanistically similar to FAP) and PREP showed exatecan release only in the presence of FAP. All DPP enzymes and PREP showed exatecan levels comparable to no enzyme controls. This is important because these enzymes are structurally and mechanistically very similar to FAP but their expression is not limited to the same places as FAP — off-target activity in these enzymes would lead to toxicities.

---

### AVA6103 Demonstrates Robust and Durable Activity in Multiple PDX Models Regardless of FAP Expression

Panel of efficacy studies in PDX models: gastric, pancreatic, colorectal, small cell lung cancer.

Once-weekly dosing regime for three weeks:
- Significant reductions in tumour growth compared to exatecan
- Durable complete responses even after treatment cessation for weeks
- Clear separation between 6103 and exatecan in colorectal cancer models

**Critical observation:** Regardless of FAP expression (brown staining on IHC), durable responses achieved even in models with lower FAP expression (e.g., small cell lung). FAP expression not the determining factor — confirms platform works across FAP expression levels.

---

### pre|CISION Tumor-Specific FAP Cleavage Mechanism Delivers More Favorable Drug PK Profile than ADCs (Enhertu)

**Rory Edwards:** Three key advantages vs Enhertu:

1. **Rapid tumour penetration:** Tumor Tmax is minutes after dosing. ADC (Enhertu): 24 hours post-dosing. This rapid penetration is a major driver of efficacy.

2. **Cmax:** Maximum concentration in the tumour. AVA6103 Cmax is **11 times higher** than Enhertu.

3. **Tumor selectivity:** TSI approximately **three times greater** than Enhertu.

---

### Tumor Selectivity Index (TSI) for AVA6103 Demonstrates Significantly Higher Selectivity v. Cleavable ADC (T-DXd, Enhertu)

Complex PK graph showing:
- **AVA6103 exatecan in plasma (orange):** Very rapid decline — very little systemic exposure
- **AVA6103 exatecan in tumour (solid red):** Very high concentration sustained
- **Enhertu deruxtecan in plasma:** Prolonged exposure over time
- **Enhertu deruxtecan in tumour:** Lower relative concentration

TSI calculation: exposure in tumour / exposure in plasma
- AVA6103 TSI: **126**
- Enhertu TSI: **47**
- Ratio: 2.68x (reported as ~3x)

### Leveraging AI to Predict the Most Sensitive Tumor Types to Increase Probability of Success in Phase 1

**Tempus collaboration for dual biomarker approach:**
- **FAP expression** — ability to target tumour and release drug
- **SLFN11 expression** — predicts sensitivity to topoisomerase 1 inhibitors and DNA damaging agents

Indications with high FAP + high SLFN11 (teal panels in figures): HER2-positive breast cancer, colorectal cancer, pancreatic cancer. Large proportion of patients in these indications express both markers — high probability of response.

**Biomarker stability (Figure B and C):** FAP and SLFN11 expression pre- and post-treatment with topoisomerase 1 inhibitor in breast cancer patients. **No difference in expression between pre- and post-treatment** — patients pre-treated with topo1 inhibitors remain likely to respond to AVA6103.

### FAP-EXD (AVA6103): Phase 1 Trial Design and Patient Population

**Christina Coughlin:** Hormone receptor-positive breast cancer and colorectal cancer showed strong correlation coefficients in SLFN11 and FAP expression, plus two CRC models showed dramatic activity including complete responses. These data led to changes in the ongoing Phase 1 trial:

- **Two additional indications added:** colorectal cancer and hormone receptor-positive breast cancer
- **Expansion cohorts will begin Phase 1B** once dose is identified
- Trial continues to enroll

---

### Conclusions

**Curtis Rink:** AVA6103 is the second pre|CISION clinical candidate based on novel sustained release mechanism providing prolonged release directly into the tumour, minimising systemic exposure via specific cleavage by FAP.

Key findings:
- Robust and durable complete responses in multiple PDX models regardless of FAP expression level
- Three key PK differences vs Enhertu:
  1. More rapid tumour penetration and payload release
  2. Tumor Cmax more than one log higher (11x) with AVA6103 vs Enhertu
  3. TSI three times higher (126 vs 47) with 6103 vs Enhertu
- Preclinical data suggest sustained release mechanism has optimised payload delivery with high intratumoral concentration and prolonged exposure, coupled with limited systemic exposure
- pre|CISION peptide cleaved only in presence of membrane-bound FAP on cancer-associated fibroblasts — active exatecan released in extracellular space. Masks toxic effects in healthy tissues.
- **FOCUS-01 Phase 1 ongoing — first patients dosed March 2026 — evaluating seven indications:** small cell lung cancer, pancreatic cancer, gastric cancer, cervical cancer, vulvar cancer, colorectal cancer, and hormone receptor-positive breast cancer
