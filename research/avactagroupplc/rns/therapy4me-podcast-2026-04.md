## KEY EXTRACTS

### Cardiac Toxicity — Lifetim Maximum Dose Eliminated
> "We've essentially gone well beyond that six doses [with our drug] and we took those data to health authority and we've now eliminated the lifetime maximum because guess how much cardiac toxicity we see? Zero. Nothing. Our pharmacokineticist gives a great explanation of why it's just not reaching the heart. But that was a pivotal moment for the company in that we really do have this separation and really worked on that therapeutic index."

**Interpretation:** Both MHRA and FDA have approved protocol amendments removing the doxorubicin lifetime dose cap for AVA6000. Zero cardiac events observed across all patients dosed to date. This is regulatory validation of the therapeutic index.

---

### AVA6000 Clinical Data
- **Salivary gland cancer:** "90% disease control rate" — first tumor type with "exquisite activity," previously chemorefractory
- Breast (TNBC), ovarian, soft tissue sarcoma: also active
- **Pancreatic cancer: NO activity** — explicitly tied to payload sensitivity (doxorubicin doesn't work in pancreatic), NOT FAP expression. Critical platform learning.
- Dose: 4x the doxorubicin dose tolerated by patients in clinic
- MTD neutropenia: 90% severe neutropenia (manageable, expected)
- Phase 1b expansion: ongoing, Project Optimus feedback received

---

### AVA6103 (Exatecan-PDC) — Platform Inflection Point
- Half-life extended to **5 days sustained release** (capping group + self-immolative linker invention)
- DAR 2:1 (dual payload — two different drugs on same peptide)
- First-in-human: imminent (weeks), signal expected **later 2026**
- Most likely response: **gastric cancer** (FAP 100% vs HER2 15-20%, similar payload to trastuzumab deruxtecan which is highly active in gastric)
- Initial 4 indications: small cell lung, gastric, cervical, pancreatic
- Next wave: ER+ breast, non-small cell lung cancer
- Skepticism to overcome: "Are you going to be able to make that pivot from doxorubicin? We did get some exposure. We had to stop at four times the dose level."

---

### Dual Payload (AVA6207)
- First dual-payload peptide drug conjugate
- DAR 2:1 achieved by inserting second drug in the linker
- Came from BD partner question: "Can you put two on?"
- Francis (CSO): "Hold my beer. Be right back."
- IND-enabling studies: later 2026 or early 2027
- Rationale: hit tumor with two mechanisms simultaneously, overcome resistance

### Partnering

### Platform Principle
- "XDC or any kind of drug conjugate is to protect normal tissues from that drug"
- FAP expressed in tumor stroma (fibroblasts), not cancer cells themselves
- Peptide (2 amino acids) shields normal tissues, FAP cleaves it in tumor microenvironment
- 100:1 tumor-to-plasma concentration ratio demonstrated in first biopsy data
- Platform claim: attach FAP-cleavable peptide to almost any drug
