## Business Model
Progressive is the third-largest personal auto insurer in the United States by market share, famous for its Name Your Price tool and direct-to-consumer digital distribution model. The business operates in three segments: Personal Lines (private passenger auto, which accounts for approximately 85% of earnings), Commercial Lines (small fleet trucking and business auto), and Property (standard and preferred homeowners insurance). Progressive's competitive edge lies in its proprietary pricing algorithms (the Name Your Price portal and its real-time telematics-based Snapshot programme) that allow granular risk selection and pricing precision competitors struggle to match. The combined ratio - a measure of underwriting profitability - has historically been the best in the industry, often below 90%, meaning Progressive earns an underwriting profit rather than relying solely on investment income.

## Key Risks
1. Auto claims severity inflation: The most pressing risk is whether Progressive can maintain its underwriting edge in an environment where bodily injury jury awards, medical costs and vehicle repair costs are all rising faster than historical trends. This directly compresses the combined ratio.
2. Competitive pressure on pricing: If Progressive raises rates aggressively to protect margins, competitors may undercut on price, causing policy churn that reduces the floater and increases loss ratios as risk selection deteriorates.
3. Regulatory and political risk: Auto insurance pricing is subject to state-level regulation. Several states have restricted the use of credit scores and telematics data in underwriting, which could impair Progressive's pricing advantage relative to competitors.
4. Catastrophe exposure in Property: The Property segment exposes Progressive to weather-related catastrophe losses. A severe hurricane season or widespread severe convective storms could materially impact earnings in a single quarter.
