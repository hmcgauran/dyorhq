## Business Model
JD.com operates three reportable segments: JD Retail (core online direct-sales and marketplace platform, including JD Health and JD Industrials), JD Logistics (internal and external logistics and supply chain services), and New Businesses (primarily JD Food Delivery, JD Property, Jingxi, and overseas operations including Joybuy). JD's model differs meaningfully from Alibaba. Where Alibaba runs a light-asset marketplace connecting buyers and sellers, JD operates a hybrid direct-sales model : it purchases inventory directly from suppliers, sells to consumers, and fulfills orders through its owned logistics network. This gives JD greater quality control and consumer trust — particularly important in a market plagued by counterfeit goods — but at the cost of higher capital intensity and lower gross margins (~8% vs Alibaba's ~35%+). JD's owned logistics network (JD Logistics, 2618.HK) is a structural differentiator. It can deliver same-day or next-day delivery across much of China, a service standard no Western competitor can match. JD Logistics also serves third-party customers and is expanding internationally (first overseas LangzuTech automated warehouse launched in the UK in Q4 2025). JD Health (6618.HK) and JD Industrials (7618.HK, listed December 2025) are separately material businesses: JD Health is a leading online pharmacy and healthcare platform; JD Industrials is an industrial supply chain technology provider. Both are growing rapidly and could eventually warrant independent valuation. Revenue is split between product revenues (electronics, home appliances, general merchandise) and service revenues (logistics, marketplace and marketing services). Service revenues are higher-margin and growing faster (+23.6% in FY2025), improving the mix over time.
