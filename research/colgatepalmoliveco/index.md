The model is defensive by design: high household penetration, frequent purchase cycles, pricing power through brand equity, and geographic diversification across 200+ countries.

## Key Risks
1. Emerging market exposure: ~50% of revenue from high-growth markets where economic slowdowns hit volumes and private label penetration increases. FX headwinds compound.
2. Private label competition: In mature and emerging markets alike, store-brand toothpaste and soap products capture share during consumer belt-tightening.
3. Commodity input costs: Oil-derived surfactants and packaging materials mean raw material inflation can pressure margins without full pricing pass-through.
4. Innovation risk: Maintaining premium pricing requires continuous product innovation; failed launches in key categories can lead to lasting share loss.
5. Category stagnation: Oral care and soap categories are mature and growing slowly; long-term volume growth is limited without geographic or category expansion.
