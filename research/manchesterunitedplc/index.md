## Business Model
Manchester United generates revenue across three principal streams. Matchday revenue covers ticket and matchday hospitality sales at Old Trafford (capacity circa 74,000), which remains one of the largest club stadiums in Europe. Broadcasting revenue derives from Premier League domestic and international TV rights distributions, UEFA competition participation fees, and related media income — this stream is highly sensitive to final league position, with Champions League qualification worth substantially more than Europa League or mid-table finishes. Commercial revenue encompasses the Adidas kit partnership (a ten-year agreement worth over GBP 900 million from 2015, with renegotiation terms under review), the Snapdragon front-of-shirt sponsorship (from 2023), TeamViewer's training ground naming rights, and broader merchandise and licensing income. The critical structural feature of the business model is its operating leverage to league position. A club finishing fourth versus twelfth in the Premier League can see hundreds of millions of pounds of difference in annual broadcasting income, given the new 2025-26 TV deal structure. This makes the commercial and broadcasting revenue streams effectively cyclical, not recurring, in practice. INEOS has sought to dampen this by cutting the wage bill and reducing the scouting network, but the primary variable remains on-pitch performance.
