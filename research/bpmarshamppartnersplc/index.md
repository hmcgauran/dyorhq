## Business Model
B.P. Marsh operates as a listed venture capital company, deploying capital into unlisted financial services businesses in exchange for minority equity stakes. The investment strategy has several defining characteristics: Niche specialism: The firm limits its investment universe to financial services, insurance, and fintech. This deliberate concentration is a competitive differentiator: relationships in these sectors generate deal flow that generalist VCs cannot access. The management team's decades-long tenure in financial services intermediation creates a proprietary origination channel. Investment size and stage: Initial investments typically range from GBP250,000 to GBP5 million. The focus is on businesses that have demonstrated proof of concept and early commercial traction but are pre-Series A or at early Series A in terms of external funding round. This avoids the high-valuation, heavily competed-for growth equity deals while maintaining meaningful ownership at exit. Minority stakes: B.P. Marsh almost always takes a minority equity position rather than a controlling stake. This preserves founder relationships, limits operational involvement, and allows the firm to spread capital across a larger portfolio. The trade-off is less control over exit timing and portfolio company strategy. Portfolio approach: The portfolio is deliberately diversified across geography (UK, Europe, North America), sub-sector (insurance, wealth management, payments, credit, regtech), and vintage year. Realised exits from prior investments have historically funded new commitments, and the firm has supplemented this with periodic capital raises on AIM. Realisation focus: The investment returns are realised through trade sales, secondary buyouts, and to a lesser extent IPOs. The AIM listing provides some optionality for portfolio companies at the appropriate stage. Management has historically prioritised distributing realised proceeds via dividends rather than continuous reinvestment, providing a yield component to the total return. Fee structure: As a listed investment company, B.P. Marsh charges management fees on net assets and may take performance-carried stakes in individual realisations. The fee load is modest relative to closed-ended private equity funds, but the lack of a formal carried interest structure can blunt incentive alignment compared to private VC partnerships.
