## Business Model
Duke Energy is a regulated electric and gas utility. Key revenue streams: Regulated Electric Utilities: Generates, transmits, and distributes electricity across six states. Revenue is approved by state utility commissions; recovery of costs plus an allowed return on regulated assets (rate base). Regulated Natural Gas: Pipeline and distribution operations in Ohio, Kentucky, and the Carolinas. Similar regulatory model with approved rates. Commercial Renewables: Growing but still modest portfolio of utility-scale solar and wind assets; unregulated but contracted under long-term PPAs. The regulated utility model is highly predictable: a state commission sets rates to allow recovery of prudent costs plus ~10% return on equity. Growth comes from rate base expansion - building new substations, smart meters, grid hardening, and new generation - approved through capital expenditure programmes.
