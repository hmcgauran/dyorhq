## Business Model
Glencore PLC is a Swiss-headquartered multinational commodity trading and mining company, dual-listed on the London Stock Exchange (LON:GLEN) and Johannesburg Stock Exchange. The group operates across two core divisions: Marketing (commodity trading, logistics, and financing across metals, minerals, and agricultural products) and Industrial Assets (copper, zinc, nickel, coal, and oil production across Australia, Africa, South America, and Europe). Glencore's marketing arm is a structural competitive advantage -- it is one of the world's largest commodity traders, generating stable fee-style income from its trading book and providing unique market intelligence that informs production decisions. The industrial assets provide the physical underlying. This hybrid model is rare and defensible. Revenue is heavily weighted to industrial product volumes and commodity prices; the marketing division adds a degree of earnings stability. Geographic exposure spans global -- Australia, Africa (copper belt), South America (colombia), Europe and North America.
