## Business Model
BWX Technologies operates through three primary segments: Naval Nucleonics (c. 65% of revenue): The company's core and most defensible business. BWXT is the sole US supplier of nuclear fuel assemblies and associated components for US Navy submarines and aircraft carriers under long-term, sole-source contracts with the Naval Sea Systems Command. These are cost-plus or firm-fixed-price contracts with durations typically spanning five to ten years, providing exceptional revenue visibility. The business is inseparable from national security policy and benefits from bipartisan congressional support regardless of administration. Nuclear Operations Group (c. 25% of revenue): Manufactures and procures heavy nuclear components including pressure vessels, reactor internals, and steam generators for US naval reactors and commercial nuclear plants. This segment also serves the Canadian and UK naval nuclear supply chains. Margins here are thin, but scale effects and operational leverage improve returns as volume grows. BWXT Commercial Group (c. 10% of revenue): Provides nuclear services, equipment, and medical isotope production to commercial customers. This includes the production of medical isotopes such as Molybdenum-99 used in diagnostic imaging, and the fabrication of components for advanced small modular reactors (SMRs) and next-generation nuclear programmes. This is the highest-risk, highest-potential option embedded in the business. The model's structural strength lies in its near-monopoly position in naval nuclear propulsion, long-duration government contracts that provide forward visibility, and the sticky, regulated nature of nuclear supply chains that creates meaningful barriers to entry.

While commercial revenue remains small today, the option value is increasing as project sanctioning accelerates.
