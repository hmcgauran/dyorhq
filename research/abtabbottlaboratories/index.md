## Business Model
Abbott operates across four principal divisions. Medical Devices covers cardiovascular stents, rhythm management, neuromodulation and Alinity diagnostics platforms. Diagnostics - a legacy COVID-19 strength - includes point-of-care and laboratory-based testing. Nutrition (Similac, Ensure, Pedialyte) serves maternal, infant and adult nutritional needs globally. Established Pharmaceuticals sells generic branded medicines in emerging markets. The diversity of revenue streams is Abbott's structural advantage: no single product or therapy represents an existential dependency. The FreeStyle Libre continuous glucose monitor is the primary growth accelerant within Medical Devices, having displaced traditional fingerstick monitoring in type 2 diabetes management across major markets. Abbott's emerging-market exposure (Established Pharmaceuticals and Nutrition) provides long-term demographic tailwinds, though near-term currency and macroeconomic volatility are headwinds.

## Key Risks
1. FreeStyle Libre competitive intensity: Dexcom's G7 and next-generation platforms are competitive. Pricing pressure or market share loss in Abbott's most important growth product would be material.
2. Elective procedure volumes: Cardiovascular implant procedures are sensitive to healthcare system capacity, reimbursement rates and patient co-pays. A recession-driven reduction in non-urgent procedures would hit device revenues.
3. Currency and emerging market risk: Approximately 40% of revenues are international. Emerging market currency depreciation, economic instability or geopolitical disruption (Latin America, Africa, Asia) could suppress reported growth.
4. COVID-19 test revenue normalisation: Although largely normalized, residual rapid-test inventory builds in prior years created tough comps; the base effect can produce misleading near-term reported growth figures.
5. Supply chain and manufacturing concentration: Abbott's manufacturing footprint is concentrated in the US, Ireland and certain emerging market facilities; disruption at key sites could affect multiple product lines simultaneously.
