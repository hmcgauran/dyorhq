## Business Model
TJX is the world's largest off-price retailer, operating over 4,700 stores across four primary brands. Its competitive model is built on buying excess inventory from brand-name manufacturers at a significant discount and passing those savings on to consumers. Key business drivers: Marmaxx (T.J. Maxx, Marshalls, HomeGoods) ~65% of revenue; US off-price apparel and home goods HomeSense / Homesense Canada ~10% of revenue; broader home formats TJX Europe (TK Maxx, Homesense UK) ~18% of revenue; international expansion TJX Canada (Winners, HomeSense, Marshalls) ~7% of revenue TJX's buyer organisation is its core asset: hundreds of expert buyers who source brand-name goods at 20-60% below department store prices. The "treasure hunt" in-store experience drives customer visit frequency. TJX does not own manufacturing -- all inventory is purchased from third-party brands seeking to clear excess stock.
