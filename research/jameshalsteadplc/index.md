## Business Model
James Halstead operates across two broad segments: commercial interiors and residential flooring, with commercial interiors representing the larger share of group revenue. The group's product portfolio includes heterogenous vinyl sheet flooring, carpet tiles, smooth vinyl flooring, and rubber flooring, sold under a range of brand names that carry recognition within the specification and architectural community. The commercial business is predominantly project-driven, with flooring specified by architects and interior designers for new-build and refurbishment contracts in healthcare, education, retail, and office environments. This specification-driven model creates meaningful switching friction: once a product is specified, contractors and facilities managers are reluctant to deviate during the construction or fit-out phase. This is a structural advantage not always present in building materials businesses. Manufacturing is primarily UK-based, with production facilities in the North West of England. European distribution extends across France, Germany, the Benelux countries, and Scandinavia, providing the group with its principal source of geographic diversification beyond the domestic UK market. The residential side of the business operates at lower margin than commercial but provides volume stability across the economic cycle. Products are distributed through merchants, retail outlets, and direct to housing associations.
