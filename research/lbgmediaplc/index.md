## Business Model
LBG Media operates a portfolio of social-first digital media brands, most notably The Lad Bible and UNILAD. Content is created and distributed primarily through owned social channels -- Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and Snapchat -- as well as through the company's own digital properties. The model is platform-agnostic in distribution but structurally dependent on these platforms for audience reach. Revenue is generated through three principal streams: programmatic digital advertising sold against the group's owned audience; branded content and sponsorship deals where brands pay for integration within or alongside editorial content; and licensing of IP and content to third parties including broadcasters and publishers. The key operational metrics are monthly unique users or reach figures, engagement rates, and revenue per thousand impressions (RPM). The group targets the 18-34 demographic demographic, which is attractive to advertisers given purchasing power and brand-interaction patterns, but this cohort is also the most fluid in its platform preferences -- a structural risk that requires constant content investment to counter. The competitive moat is weak to moderate. Brand equity in The Lad Bible and UNILAD provides some differentiation and audience loyalty, but the underlying content is replicable and the distribution is largely controlled by two or three dominant platforms. LBG Media does not own its distribution; it rents it. This asymmetry is the central vulnerability of the business model and is shared with most digital media publishers at this scale. Geographically, the group generates the majority of its revenue in the UK and the United States, with smaller contributions from other English-speaking markets. Currency exposure is material: GBP weakness against USD reduces reported revenue and profits for sterling-reporting periods.
