## Business Model
Ab Dynamics designs and manufactures high-precision automotive testing systems. Its core product families include suspension testing rigs (measuring force-deflection characteristics of vehicle suspensions), steering test systems (evaluating steering feel and response), crash simulation equipment (sled tests, barrier tests, and associated data capture), and data acquisition systems (DAQ platforms that integrate across the test rig ecosystem). Customers purchase bespoke systems that are typically integrated into dedicated test facilities, creating a high-switching-cost relationship. The business is project-based with long sales cycles: a suspension rig or crash simulation installation can take 12-18 months from specification to commissioning. Revenue recognition is weighted toward the commissioning and acceptance phase of each project. This creates lumpiness in reported revenue that investors should account for when assessing half-year versus full-year figures. Geographic exposure spans the UK, continental Europe, and Asia (principally China, South Korea, and Japan). The Chinese EV market is both a growth driver and a risk: Chinese domestic OEMs are investing heavily in testing infrastructure, but geopolitical friction and domestic champion policies could redirect capital away from UK-sourced equipment. The competitive moat is accumulated domain expertise in precision measurement and the relationships built over decades with major automotive engineering teams. This is not a commoditised market - qualification cycles for crash test equipment are lengthy, and procurement decisions sit with senior engineering and procurement functions at major OEMs.

## Key Risks
1. Automotive capex cyclicality (HIGH): Automotive OEM capex is highly correlated with vehicle production cycles. A sustained downturn in global light vehicle production - whether from demand weakness, EV transition disruption, or geopolitical disruption - would directly reduce demand for testing equipment. This is the primary cyclical risk.
2. Customer concentration (MEDIUM): As a niche supplier to major OEMs, Ab Dynamics likely has significant concentration in a small number of large customers. Loss of a major OEM relationship or a key testing programme would have a material revenue impact that cannot be quickly replaced.
3. Chinese domestic competition (MEDIUM): Chinese domestic equipment manufacturers are building capability in precision testing. Government policy actively supports domestic champions. As Chinese EVs become more globally competitive, the domestic testing infrastructure supporting them is more likely to source domestically, not from UK suppliers.
4. Geopolitical and export control risk (MEDIUM): Any escalation in UK-China trade friction, export control regimes affecting precision equipment, or broader geopolitical decoupling could disrupt the Asian revenue stream, which is material to the overall business.
