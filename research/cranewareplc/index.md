## Business Model
Craneware operates on a SaaS-adjacent model, licensing its software to US hospital systems on multi-year subscription agreements. The company's three product lines address distinct administrative workflows within US hospitals: Chargemaster is the core product and the largest revenue contributor. It manages the hospital chargemaster -- the comprehensive list of medical services, procedures, and supplies and their associated prices -- ensuring billing accuracy, regulatory compliance, and optimal reimbursement. Hospitals that overcharge risk audits and penalties; those that undercharge leave money on the table. Chargemaster sits at the intersection of compliance and revenue optimisation, which gives it strong customer retention characteristics. Trending provides claims analytics, allowing hospital finance and revenue cycle teams to identify patterns in claim denials, reimbursement variances, and billing errors. It is a diagnostic tool that complements Chargemaster, giving customers analytical insight into where their revenue is leaking. Pharmacy addresses the 340B drug pricing programme, which allows qualifying safety-net hospitals to purchase outpatient drugs at discounted prices and receive reimbursement at standard rates, retaining the margin. The 340B programme is complex, heavily regulated, and subject to ongoing legal and policy uncertainty -- which creates ongoing demand for compliance software. Pharmacy is the highest-growth segment but from a small base. Revenue is predominantly subscription-based, invoiced annually in advance. The customer base is US hospital systems and health systems, with high retention driven by the embedded nature of Chargemaster in hospital billing operations. Switching costs are meaningful: a hospital would not casually replace its chargemaster system given the billing disruption that would cause.
